### NCT04852978

---
trial_id: NCT04852978
title: A Study of Casirivimab+Imdevimab and Moderna mRNA-1273 Vaccine in Patients With COVID-19
phase: 2
status: completed
primary_sponsor: regeneron
co_sponsors: []
drugs: ["Casirivimab+imdevimab", "Moderna mrna-1273 vaccine"]
indications: []
enrollment: 295
primary_endpoint: vaccine-induced 50% inhibitory dilution neutralising antibody titres to SARS-CoV-2 spike protein, 56 days after the first vaccination
primary_completion_date: 2022-11-21
has_results: true
primary_result_value: "Titres were decreased up to 4-fold (median titres 280-450 IU/mL for casirivimab and imdevimab vs 1160 IU/mL for vaccine only on day 56) when casirivimab and imdevimab was given 85 days or less before vaccination (150-1200 mg intravenously) or co-administered subcutaneously (600 mg or 1200 mg) with vaccination."
last_updated: 2024-05-16
---

**Phase:** 2 | **Status:** completed | **Sponsor:** [[regeneron]]

### Design
This randomized controlled trial (RCT) studied the effect of casirivimab and imdevimab, given before or at the time of COVID-19 vaccination, on vaccine-induced SARS-CoV-2 neutralising antibody titres. It involved 295 patients and compared the combination with mRNA-1273 vaccine only.

### Primary endpoint
Vaccine-induced 50% inhibitory dilution neutralising antibody titres to SARS-CoV-2 spike protein, 56 days after the first vaccination. Titres were decreased up to 4-fold (median titres 280-450 IU/mL for casirivimab and imdevimab vs 1160 IU/mL for vaccine only on day 56) when casirivimab and imdevimab was given 85 days or less before vaccination (150-1200 mg intravenously) or co-administered subcutaneously (600 mg or 1200 mg) with vaccination.

### Results summary
Casirivimab and imdevimab administration before or at the time of COVID-19 vaccination reduced the elicitation of SARS-CoV-2 neutralising antibodies. Minimal reduction was observed when mAbs were given 113 or 169 days before vaccination, or when vaccination occurred before mAb administration. The mAbs had a minimal effect on vaccine-induced total antibodies and T-cell responses to the spike protein. The combination was generally well tolerated, with a slight increase in treatment-emergent adverse events compared to vaccine-only.
