## A Study of Atezolizumab Monotherapy Versus Chemotherapy in Patients With Previously Untreated Locally Advanced or Metastatic Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (NSCLC) Who Are Not Eligible for Platinum-Based Chemotherapy (IPSOS)

**Phase:** 3 | **Status:** completed | **Sponsor:** [[roche]]

### Design
This Phase 3 trial compared [[atezolizumab]] monotherapy to single-agent chemotherapy (gemcitabine, vinorelbine, paclitaxel, or docetaxel) in treatment-naïve locally advanced or metastatic non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) patients unsuitable for platinum-doublet chemotherapy.

### Primary endpoint
Overall Survival (OS).

### Results summary
The IPSOS trial demonstrated significant overall survival (OS) improvement in the atezolizumab arm (HR 0.78, 95% CI: 0.63-0.97; p = 0.028).

### Clinical evidence
A model-based meta-analysis (MBMA) was conducted to compare the IPSOS trial arms to historical single-agent chemotherapies.
- **Study design:** meta-analysis
- **Sample size:** 3637 participants
- **Comparator:** single-agent chemotherapy
- **Primary outcome:** Overall Survival (OS) Hazard Ratio for IPSOS control arm relative to historical trials
- **Primary result:** Model-predicted HR for the IPSOS control arm relative to historical trials was 0.543 (95% CI: 0.435-0.677)
- **Secondary results:** Model-predicted HR for the IPSOS atezolizumab monotherapy arm relative to historical trials was 0.418 (95% CI: 0.335-0.522)
- **Safety:** None
- **Conclusions:** Overall, the MBMA results support the benefit of atezo seen in the IPSOS trial.
- **Journal:** CPT: pharmacometrics & systems pharmacology, 2026
- **Industry sponsored:** True

## A Study of Tiragolumab Plus Atezolizumab and Chemotherapy in Oesophageal Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SKYSCRAPER-08)

**Phase:** 3 | **Status:** completed | **Sponsor:** [[roche]]

### Design
This randomised, double-blind, placebo-controlled, phase 3 trial (SKYSCRAPER-08) enrolled 461 adult patients with treatment-naive, unresectable locally advanced, unresectable recurrent, or metastatic oesophageal squamous cell carcinoma. Patients were randomly assigned (1:1) to receive tiragolumab plus [[atezolizumab]] and chemotherapy (paclitaxel and cisplatin) or placebo and chemotherapy.

### Primary endpoint
independent review facility-assessed progression-free survival and overall survival.

### Results summary
Median independent review facility-assessed progression-free survival in the tiragolumab plus atezolizumab and chemotherapy group was 6.2 months (95% CI 5.7-7.2) versus 5.4 months (95% CI 4.4-5.5) in the placebo and chemotherapy group (HR 0.56, 95% CI 0.45-0.70; p<0.0001). Median overall survival was 15.7 months (95% CI 13.3-20.4) and 11.1 months (95% CI 9.6-13.6; HR 0.70, 95% CI 0.55-0.88; p=0.0024). The most common grade 3-4 adverse events in the tiragolumab plus atezolizumab and chemotherapy group versus the placebo and chemotherapy group were white blood cell count decrease (46 [20%] of 228 vs 35 [15%] of 227), neutrophil count decrease (78 [34%] vs 78 [34%]), and anaemia (19 [8%] vs 24 [11%]). Serious adverse events occurred in 94 (41%) of 228 patients in the tiragolumab plus atezolizumab and chemotherapy group and 89 (39%) of 227 in the placebo and chemotherapy group; the most common serious adverse event was pneumonia (17 [7%] of 228 and 13 [6%] of 227). Treatment-related deaths occurred in six patients (3%) in the tiragolumab plus atezolizumab and chemotherapy group (immune-mediated lung disease, pneumonitis, cardiac arrest, gastrointestinal haemorrhage, hepatic failure, and bacterial pneumonia) and two (1%) in the placebo and chemotherapy group (gastrointestinal infection and death of unknown cause). No new safety signals were identified. Independent review facility-assessed progression-free survival and overall survival were significantly better in the tiragolumab plus atezolizumab and chemotherapy group compared with chemotherapy alone for unresectable locally advanced, unresectable recurrent, or metastatic oesophageal squamous cell carcinoma. These data support the rationale for exploring dual checkpoint inhibition added to chemotherapy for this group of patients with a high unmet need.
