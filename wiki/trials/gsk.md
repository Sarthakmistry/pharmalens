## A Phase 1 Study of GSK3326595 Monotherapy and in Combination with Pembrolizumab in Patients with Advanced Solid Tumors or Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma

**Phase:** 1 | **Status:** completed | **Sponsor:** [[gsk]]

### Design
This was a first-in-human, open-label, multicenter, three-part phase I study (METEOR-1) evaluating GSK3326595 monotherapy and in combination with [[pembrolizumab]] in patients with advanced solid tumors and non-Hodgkin lymphoma. The study aimed to determine the recommended phase II dose (RP2D), assess safety, evaluate preliminary clinical activity, and study the pharmacokinetics of GSK3326595.

### Primary endpoint
Determine the recommended phase II dose (RP2D), assess safety, evaluate preliminary clinical activity, and study the pharmacokinetics of oral GSK3326595 monotherapy.

### Results summary
GSK3326595 monotherapy demonstrated modest antitumor activity. The RP2D was amended from 400 mg to 300 mg once daily due to toxicities. Common adverse events at the RP2D included fatigue (57%), nausea (48%), and anemia (48%). Four partial responses were observed in part 1. In part 2, two complete responses and one partial response were seen in NHL, one partial response in adenoid cystic carcinoma, and one partial response in HR-positive breast cancer. No responses were observed in part 3 (GSK3326595 with pembrolizumab).
