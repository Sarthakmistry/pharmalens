## A Study of Pembrolizumab (MK-3475) vs. Placebo in Participants With Resected Stage IB, II, or IIIA Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (NSCLC) (KEYNOTE-091)

**Phase:** 3 | **Status:** completed | **Sponsor:** [[merck]]

### Design
This is a randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, multicenter Phase 3 study evaluating the efficacy and safety of [[pembrolizumab]] versus placebo as adjuvant therapy in participants with resected Stage IB, II, or IIIA non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC). Participants were randomized 1:1 to receive [[pembrolizumab]] or placebo for up to 1 year.

### Primary endpoint
Disease-free survival (DFS) as assessed by investigator per RECIST v1.1, measured from randomization until recurrence or death, whichever occurs first.

### Results summary
[[Pembrolizumab]] significantly improved disease-free survival (DFS) compared to placebo in participants with resected Stage IB, II, or IIIA NSCLC. The hazard ratio for DFS was 0.76 (95% CI 0.63-0.91; p=0.0014). Safety profiles were consistent with previous studies of [[pembrolizumab]].

## A Study of Pembrolizumab and Lenvatinib Combinations in Advanced Clear-Cell Renal Cell Carcinoma

**Phase:** 2 | **Status:** completed | **Sponsor:** [[merck]]

### Design
This was a Phase 2 randomized controlled trial evaluating various combinations of [[pembrolizumab]] and lenvatinib, with or without novel agents like quavonlimab, favezelimab, belzutifan, or vibostolimab, in participants with advanced clear-cell renal cell carcinoma (ccRCC). The study compared these combinations against [[pembrolizumab]] plus lenvatinib as a reference.

### Primary endpoint
Objective response rate (ORR) per RECIST v1.1 by blinded independent central review in all randomized participants (excluding safety lead-in), and safety in all treated participants.

### Results summary
The efficacy and safety of [[pembrolizumab]] plus lenvatinib were confirmed. Some novel combinations (pembrolizumab plus lenvatinib plus belzutifan, and quavonlimab/pembrolizumab plus lenvatinib) showed similar ORR to the reference arm, warranting further investigation. Other combinations did not show similar efficacy.
