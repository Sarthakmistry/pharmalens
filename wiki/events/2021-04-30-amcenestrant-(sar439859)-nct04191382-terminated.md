## Sanofi's Amcenestrant (SAR439859) Phase 2 Trial NCT04191382 Terminated

**Date:** 2021-04-30 | **Type:** trial_termination | **Signal:** bearish

### Summary
Sanofi's Phase 2 clinical trial (NCT04191382) investigating Amcenestrant (SAR439859) for ER+/HER2- breast cancer was terminated on April 30, 2021. Despite the termination, the study later published findings indicating that amcenestrant demonstrated antiproliferative activity in postmenopausal women with previously untreated, operable ER+/HER2- breast cancer.

### Market implication
The termination of a Phase 2 trial is generally a negative signal for the drug program, suggesting potential strategic shifts or issues, although the later publication of positive clinical data from this study provides some mitigating context regarding amcenestrant's efficacy.

### Clinical evidence
- **Study design:** RCT
- **Sample size:** 95
- **Comparator:** letrozole
- **Primary outcome:** change in Ki67 between baseline and Day 15 (i.e., day of surgery)
- **Primary result:** Relative change from baseline in Ki67 was - 75.9% (95% confidence interval [CI] - 81.9 to - 67.9) for amcenestrant 400 mg, - 68.2% (- 75.7 to - 58.4) for amcenestrant 200 mg, and - 77.7% (- 83.4 to - 70.0) for letrozole (geometric least-squares mean [LSM] estimates).
- **Secondary results:**
    - Absolute change in ER H-score from baseline (LSM estimate) was - 176.7 in the amcenestrant 400 mg arm
    - Absolute change in ER H-score from baseline (LSM estimate) was - 202.9 in the amcenestrant 200 mg arm
    - Absolute change in ER H-score from baseline (LSM estimate) was - 32.5 in the letrozole arm
- **Safety:** There were no Grade ≥ 3 treatment-related adverse events.
- **Conclusions (verbatim):** Both amcenestrant and letrozole demonstrated antiproliferative activity in postmenopausal women with previously untreated, operable ER+/HER2- breast cancer and had good overall tolerability.
- **Journal:** Breast Cancer Res., 2023
