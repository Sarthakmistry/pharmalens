## Trial Completion: Casirivimab and Imdevimab's Effect on mRNA-1273 Vaccine Response

**Date:** 2022-11-21 | **Type:** trial_completion | **Signal:** neutral

### Summary
A Phase 2 randomized controlled trial (NCT04852978) investigating the effect of casirivimab and imdevimab on the immune response to the Moderna mRNA-1273 vaccine has completed. The study found that administration of casirivimab and imdevimab before or concurrently with vaccination reduced SARS-CoV-2 neutralizing antibody titres, though the clinical significance of this reduction remains unclear.

### Market implication
The findings suggest a potential interaction between monoclonal antibodies and vaccine-induced immunity, but the unclear clinical significance means there is no immediate material market impact for Regeneron or the involved drugs.

### Clinical evidence
- **Study design:** RCT
- **Sample size:** 295 patients
- **Comparator:** mRNA-1273 only
- **Primary outcome:** vaccine-induced 50% inhibitory dilution neutralising antibody titres to SARS-CoV-2 spike protein, 56 days after the first vaccination
- **Primary result:** Titres were decreased up to 4-fold (median titres 280-450 IU/mL for casirivimab and imdevimab vs 1160 IU/mL for vaccine only on day 56) when casirivimab and imdevimab was given 85 days or less before vaccination (150-1200 mg intravenously) or co-administered subcutaneously (600 mg or 1200 mg) with vaccination.
- **Secondary results:**
    - All vaccinated participants developed neutralising antibodies to SARS-CoV-2, with median titres above the published protective threshold (100 IU/mL) against the SARS-CoV-2 D614G variant.
    - Minimal reduction in neutralisation titres was observed in the 48 mg and 12 mg intravenous groups, corresponding to receipt of casirivimab and imdevimab 113 days and 169 days, respectively, before vaccination, and when administering the vaccine 6 days before the mAb.
    - Across all groups, mAbs had a minimal effect on vaccine-induced total antibodies and T-cell responses to the spike protein.
- **Safety:** Casirivimab and imdevimab plus mRNA-1273 was generally well tolerated; a slight increase in treatment-emergent adverse events was observed in the casirivimab and imdevimab plus vaccine groups versus the vaccine-only group.
- **Conclusions:** Casirivimab and imdevimab administration before or at the time of COVID-19 vaccination reduced the elicitation of SARS-CoV-2 neutralising antibodies, but minimal effect was observed when vaccination occurred before mAb administration. Although the clinical significance of this decrease in neutralisation is unclear, this evidence suggests that further investigation of potential interactions could be warranted before concurrent clinical use of mAbs and vaccines targeting the same viral proteins as their main modes of action for the prevention or treatment of infectious diseases.
- **Journal:** Lancet Infect Dis., 2025
- **Industry sponsored:** True
