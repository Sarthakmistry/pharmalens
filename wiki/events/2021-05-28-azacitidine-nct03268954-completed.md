## Takeda's Pevonedistat Phase 3 Trial (NCT03268954) Completes, Failing to Meet Primary Endpoint

**Date:** 2021-05-28 | **Type:** trial_completion | **Signal:** bearish

### Summary
A Phase 3 randomized controlled trial (NCT03268954) evaluating Pevonedistat in combination with azacitidine completed on May 28, 2021. The study, sponsored by [[takeda]], aimed to improve event-free survival in patients but did not demonstrate a statistically significant benefit over azacitidine alone. This outcome indicates that the combination therapy did not meet its primary efficacy endpoint.

### Market implication
The failure of Pevonedistat to meet its primary endpoint in this Phase 3 trial is a negative signal for [[takeda]]'s development pipeline for this asset.

### Affected entities
- Drug: Pevonedistat, Azacitidine
- Company: [[takeda]]
- Indication: None

### Clinical evidence
- Study design: RCT
- Sample size: 280 patients
- Comparator: azacitidine alone
- Primary outcome: event-free survival (EFS)
- Primary result: Median EFS was 21.0 months for P+A vs 16.6 months for A (HR, 0.82; 95% CI, 0.61-1.10; P = .18)
- Secondary results:
    - Median OS was 23.7 months for P+A vs 20.0 months for A (HR, 0.80; 95% CI, 0.59-1.08; P = .14)
    - CR rate was 34.3% for P+A vs 25.7% for A (odds ratio, 1.49; 95% CI, 0.89-2.50; P = .12)
- Safety: Grade ≥3 treatment-emergent adverse events (TEAEs) occurred in 87.9% of P+A patients and 80.0% of A patients. The most common grade ≥3 TEAEs in the P+A arm were thrombocytopenia (49.3%), neutropenia (45.7%), and anemia (25.7%).
- Conclusions: Pevonedistat plus azacitidine did not significantly improve EFS, OS, or CR rate compared with azacitidine alone in this patient population. The safety profile was consistent with known toxicities of both agents. Further investigation of Pevonedistat in specific subgroups or different combinations may be warranted.
- Journal: Blood Adv, 2022
- Industry sponsored: True
