## Atezolizumab and Bevacizumab Trial NCT04487067 Completed, Confirming Efficacy in Advanced HCC

**Date:** 2024-08-13 | **Type:** trial_completion | **Signal:** bullish

### Summary
The Phase 3 trial NCT04487067, investigating [[atezolizumab]] plus [[bevacizumab]] for advanced and unresectable hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC), reached primary completion on August 13, 2024. Interim analysis results are consistent with prior data, further confirming the combination as a standard of care for this patient population.

### Market implication
This trial completion with consistent positive results reinforces the established efficacy and market position of [[atezolizumab]] and [[bevacizumab]] for [[roche]] in the advanced HCC indication.

### Affected entities
- Drug: [[atezolizumab]], [[bevacizumab]]
- Company: [[roche]]
- Indication: Hepatocellular Carcinoma (HCC)

### Clinical evidence
- **Study design:** None
- **Sample size:** 149 patients
- **Comparator:** None
- **Primary outcome:** incidence of grade 3-5 bleeding/hemorrhages
- **Primary result:** The incidence of grade 3-5 bleeding/hemorrhages was 11.4%.
- **Secondary results:** The median OS was 18.2 months (95% confidence interval 15.4 months to not evaluable); the median PFS was 8.5 months (95% confidence interval 7.5-11.2 months)
- **Safety:** results of other safety endpoints were consistent with the safety profile of atezolizumab plus bevacizumab, and the underlying disease, without any new safety observation.
- **Conclusions:** Results from the interim analysis are consistent with data from the IMbrave150 trial, and further confirm first-line atezolizumab plus bevacizumab as a standard of care for patients with systemic treatment-naive advanced and unresectable HCC.
- **Journal:** ESMO Open, 2025
- **Industry sponsored:** True
