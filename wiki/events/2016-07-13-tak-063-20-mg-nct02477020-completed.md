## Takeda's investigational drug Tak-063 20 mg Phase 2 trial completes, missing primary endpoint but showing supportive secondary results.

**Date:** 2016-07-13 | **Type:** trial_completion | **Signal:** neutral

### Summary
Takeda's investigational drug Tak-063 20 mg completed its Phase 2 trial (NCT02477020) on July 13, 2016. The study did not meet its primary endpoint, which was the change from baseline in the Positive and Negative Syndrome Scale total score at week 6. However, secondary endpoints were generally supportive of antipsychotic efficacy, and the drug demonstrated a safe and well-tolerated profile.

### Market implication
The mixed outcome, with a missed primary endpoint but some positive secondary signals, creates uncertainty for the continued development of Tak-063 by Takeda.

### Affected entities
- Drug: Tak-063 20 mg
- Company: [[takeda]]

### Clinical evidence
- **Study design:** RCT
- **Sample size:** 164
- **Comparator:** placebo
- **Primary outcome:** change from baseline in the Positive and Negative Syndrome Scale total score at week 6
- **Primary result:** not achieved (least-squares mean difference vs placebo [standard error] = -5.46 [3.44]; p = 0.115)
- **Secondary results:** Secondary endpoints were generally supportive of antipsychotic efficacy.
- **Safety:** TAK-063 was safe and well tolerated, and most adverse events were mild or moderate in severity and did not result in discontinuation. No deaths occurred, and the incidence of akathisia and dystonia, categories of extrapyramidal syndromes, was more frequent in the TAK-063 group than placebo.
- **Conclusions:** Although the study did not meet the primary endpoint (effect size = 0.308), the effects of TAK-063 on the primary and secondary endpoints may be suggestive of antipsychotic activity. Interpretation of these results is confounded by a relatively high placebo effect and a lack of dose-ranging or active reference.
- **Journal:** Schizophr Res., 2019
- **Industry sponsored:** True
