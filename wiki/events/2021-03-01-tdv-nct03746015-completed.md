## Takeda's TAK-003 Dengue Vaccine Phase 2 Trial Shows Sustained Immune Response

**Date:** 2021-03-01 | **Type:** trial_completion | **Signal:** bullish

### Summary
Takeda announced the completion of its Phase 2 trial (NCT03746015) for its dengue vaccine candidate, TAK-003. The open-label study, involving 30 participants, demonstrated that TAK-003 induced neutralising antibodies across all DENV serotypes by Day 30, which were sustained for 12 months. The vaccine was well tolerated and elicited robust humoral and cellular immune responses.

### Market implication
The positive Phase 2 results for TAK-003 suggest a promising profile for Takeda's dengue vaccine candidate, potentially strengthening its infectious disease pipeline.

### Clinical evidence
- **Study design:** open-label phase 2 trial
- **Sample size:** 30
- **Comparator:** null
- **Primary outcome:** assessment of neutralising antibody geometric mean titres (GMTs) through 12 months post-vaccination
- **Primary result:** TAK-003 induced neutralising antibodies across all DENV serotypes by Day 30 in all thirty flavivirus seronegative and dengue seropositive adult participants, which were sustained for 12 months.
- **Secondary results:**
    - IFN-γ expressing T cell responder rates to any peptide pool spanning sequences of prME, NS1, NS3, and NS5 from all four serotypes were 100% for both flavivirus seronegative and dengue seropositive participants, from one month after the first vaccination through 12 months.
    - T cell reactivity against NS proteins of all 4 serotypes was observed after vaccination - responses were durable and remained above baseline levels throughout.
    - Multifunctional (expression of ≥2 cytokines of IFN-γ, IL-2 and TNF-α) CD4 and CD8 T cell responses were observed from Day 15 post vaccination and remained above baseline levels throughout.
- **Safety:** Vaccine RNAemia and safety findings were consistent with the known TAK-003 safety profile. TAK-003 was well tolerated.
- **Conclusions:** TAK-003 was well tolerated and elicited tetravalent humoral and cellular immune responses sustained for at least 12 months in flavivirus seronegative and dengue seropositive adults residing in areas without DENV circulation.
- **Journal:** Vaccine, 2026
