## Sanofi's AMEERA-5 trial (NCT04478266) of amcenestrant and palbociclib combination terminated due to futility in breast cancer

**Date:** 2022-06-28 | **Type:** trial_termination | **Signal:** bearish

### Summary
Sanofi's Phase 3 AMEERA-5 trial (NCT04478266), which was investigating amcenestrant (Sar439859) in combination with [[palbociclib]] for the treatment of advanced or metastatic breast cancer, was terminated. The decision to discontinue the study was based on the recommendation of the independent data monitoring committee following an interim futility analysis, indicating the combination did not meet its primary endpoint of progression-free survival.

### Market implication
The termination of a Phase 3 trial due to futility represents a significant setback for Sanofi's pipeline in [[oncology-breast]] and is a bearish signal for the company's future prospects in this indication.

### Clinical evidence
- **Study design:** RCT
- **Sample size:** 1068
- **Comparator:** letrozole plus palbociclib
- **Primary outcome:** progression-free survival (PFS)
- **Primary result:** At the interim analysis (median follow-up 8.4 months), the stratified hazard ratio for PFS was 1.209 (95% CI, 0.939 to 1.557; one-sided P value = .9304); therefore, the study was stopped for futility.
- **Secondary results:**
    - The 6-month PFS rate was 82.7% (95% CI, 79.0 to 85.8) with amcenestrant + palbociclib versus 86.9% (95% CI, 83.5 to 89.6) with letrozole + palbociclib.
    - In the amcenestrant + palbociclib versus letrozole + palbociclib groups, treatment-emergent adverse events (any grade) occurred in 85.6% versus 85.4% of patients and grade ≥3 events in 46.3% versus 60.8%, respectively.
- **Safety:** No new safety signals were identified.
- **Conclusions:** The AMEERA-5 study was discontinued on the basis of the recommendation of the data monitoring committee at the interim futility analysis. No new safety signals were identified.
- **Journal:** J Clin Oncol., 2024
- **Industry sponsored:** None
