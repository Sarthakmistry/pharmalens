## Sanofi's Efanesoctocog Alfa Phase 3 Trial NCT04759131 for Hemophilia A Reports Positive Completion

**Date:** 2023-01-18 | **Type:** trial_completion | **Signal:** bullish

### Summary
Sanofi announced the completion of its Phase 3 trial (NCT04759131) for efanesoctocog alfa (BIVV001) on January 18, 2023. The trial, which enrolled 74 participants, reported positive clinical findings, including zero bleeds in a significant portion of participants and high-sustained FVIII levels, with no new safety concerns.

### Market implication
The positive trial completion and clinical findings for efanesoctocog alfa are a bullish signal for Sanofi, potentially strengthening its position in the hemophilia A market.

### Clinical evidence
- **Study design:** observational
- **Sample size:** None
- **Comparator:** None
- **Primary outcome:** Annualized bleed rate
- **Primary result:** zero bleeds in 64% of participants and consistently low annualized bleed rates
- **Secondary results:**
    - high-sustained FVIII levels in the normal to near-normal range (>40 IU/dL) for 3 days and >10 IU/dL for approximately 7 days at steady state
    - PK parameters were similar in participants with blood groups O and non-O
    - A single dose of efanesoctocog alfa resolved 95% of bleeding episodes
    - The hemostatic efficacy of efanesoctocog alfa for the treatment of bleeding episodes was rated as excellent or good for nearly all evaluated first injections (97%)
- **Safety:** No inhibitors or unexpected safety findings were reported.
- **Conclusions:** Once-weekly prophylaxis with efanesoctocog alfa provided high-sustained FVIII levels and highly effective bleed treatment and prevention in previously treated children aged <12 years with severe hemophilia A.
- **Journal:** J Thromb Haemost, 2025
- **Industry sponsored:** True
