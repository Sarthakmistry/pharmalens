## Amgen Q2 2025 Earnings and Pipeline Updates

**Date:** 2025-08-06 | **Type:** earnings_signal | **Signal:** bullish

### Summary
Amgen reported strong Q2 2025 financial results with a 9% increase in total revenue to $17,328.0M for the six months ended June 30, 2025, and a 92% increase in net income to $3,162.0M for the three months ended June 30, 2025. The company also announced positive interim results from its Phase 2 MariTide study for obesity and type 2 diabetes, Phase 3 IMDELLTRA/IMDYLLTRA for SCLC, and Phase 3 bemarituzumab for G/GEJ cancer, alongside European marketing authorization for TEPEZZA for thyroid eye disease.

### Market implication
The significant revenue and net income growth, coupled with positive clinical trial readouts for key pipeline assets and a new marketing authorization, indicate a strong financial and developmental trajectory for Amgen, likely leading to a positive market response.

### Clinical evidence (Maridebart cafraglutide / MariTide)
- **Study design:** RCT
- **Sample size:** None
- **Comparator:** placebo
- **Primary outcome:** Average weight loss
- **Primary result:** In participants living with obesity without type 2 diabetes, MariTide demonstrated average weight loss up to approximately 20% compared to 2.6% in the placebo arm. In participants living with obesity and type 2 diabetes, MariTide demonstrated average weight loss up to approximately 17% compared to 1.4% in the placebo arm.
- **Secondary results:**
    - Weight loss had not plateaued by 52 weeks, indicating the potential for further weight reduction.
    - Weight loss with MariTide was associated with improvements in cardiometabolic parameters, including reductions in hemoglobin A1c (up to 2.2 percentage points), waist circumference, blood pressure, high-sensitivity C-reactive protein and select lipid levels.
- **Safety:** No new safety signals were identified. Tolerability was consistent with the GLP-1 class. Most frequently reported AEs were gastrointestinal (GI) related, mostly mild to moderate, and predominantly limited to initial dosing. Discontinuation rates due to GI AEs in dose escalation arms were up to 7.8%.
- **Conclusions:** MariTide demonstrated significant and sustained weight loss in both obese and obese with type 2 diabetes populations, along with improvements in cardiometabolic markers, with a safety profile consistent with the GLP-1 class and improved tolerability with dose escalation.
- **Journal:** The New England Journal of Medicine, 2025
- **Industry sponsored:** True
