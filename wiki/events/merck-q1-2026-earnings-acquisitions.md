## Merck Reports Q1 2026 Revenue Growth Driven by Oncology and New Products, Net Loss Due to Acquisition Charges and IRA Impacts

**Date:** 2026-05-04 | **Type:** earnings_signal | **Signal:** neutral

### Summary
Merck reported Q1 2026 total revenue of $16.3 billion, a 5% increase, driven by oncology and new product launches, but recorded a net loss of $3.0 million due to significant R&D charges from recent acquisitions of Terns Pharmaceuticals and Cidara Therapeutics, alongside ongoing impacts from the Inflation Reduction Act. Keytruda sales continued strong growth, while Januvia and Janumet faced pricing pressures from the IRA.

### Market implication
While Merck achieved 5% revenue growth and strong performance in oncology and new product launches, the company reported a net loss due to substantial R&D charges from strategic acquisitions. Additionally, regulatory pressures from the Inflation Reduction Act and a 'most-favored-nation' agreement pose significant headwinds for key products, leading to a neutral overall market signal.
