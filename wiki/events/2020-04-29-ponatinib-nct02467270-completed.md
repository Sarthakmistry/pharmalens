## Takeda's Ponatinib Trial NCT02467270 Reaches Primary Completion

**Date:** 2020-04-29 | **Type:** trial_completion | **Signal:** neutral

### Summary
The Phase 2 clinical trial NCT02467270, evaluating Ponatinib, sponsored by Takeda, reached its primary completion on April 29, 2020. Later published clinical findings from an observational study of 100 patients indicated that Ponatinib is an effective treatment option for patients with chronic-phase CML who failed 2G-TKIs, showing durable responses and high survival rates, although careful monitoring for arterial occlusive events is required.

### Market implication
The primary completion of this Phase 2 trial represents a development milestone for Ponatinib, with subsequent clinical data providing insights into its efficacy and safety profile.

### Affected entities
- Drug: Ponatinib

### Clinical evidence
- **Study design:** observational
- **Sample size:** 100 patients
- **Comparator:** null
- **Primary outcome:** major molecular response (MMR) rate
- **Primary result:** MMR rate was 38% (95% CI: 28.4%-47.6%)
- **Secondary results:** complete cytogenetic response (CCyR) rate was 47% (95% CI: 37.1%-56.9%), median follow-up duration was 40 months (range, 1-108 months), 3-year overall survival (OS) rate was 81% (95% CI: 72.8%-89.2%), 3-year progression-free survival (PFS) rate was 69% (95% CI: 59.7%-78.3%)
- **Safety:** Arterial occlusive events (AOEs) occurred in 19% of patients, including cardiovascular events (10%), cerebrovascular events (6%), and peripheral arterial occlusive events (3%).
- **Conclusions:** Ponatinib is an effective treatment option for patients with chronic-phase CML who failed 2G-TKIs, with durable responses and high survival rates. However, careful monitoring for AOEs is required.
- **Journal:** Am J Hematol, 2022
- **Industry sponsored:** False
