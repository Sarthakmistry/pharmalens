## Takeda's Idursulfase-IT Trial NCT01506141 Completed with Positive Safety and Efficacy Findings

**Date:** 2024-04-30 | **Type:** trial_completion | **Signal:** bullish

### Summary
A Phase 1/2 observational clinical trial (NCT01506141) for Idursulfase-IT, sponsored by [[takeda]], reached primary completion on April 30, 2024. The trial, which enrolled 15 participants, demonstrated that Idursulfase-IT was generally well tolerated with no life-threatening adverse events, and showed promising signs of efficacy including decreased cerebrospinal fluid glycosaminoglycans and stabilized cognitive function in some patients. These long-term results support the continued clinical development of Idursulfase-IT for patients with MPS II with cognitive impairment.

### Market implication
The positive safety and preliminary efficacy data from this completed trial could de-risk the further development of Idursulfase-IT, potentially enhancing its future market prospects for [[takeda]].

### Clinical evidence
- **Study design:** observational
- **Sample size:** 15
- **Comparator:** null
- **Primary outcome:** safety and tolerability
- **Primary result:** Idursulfase-IT was generally well tolerated; there were no life-threatening adverse events (AEs) or deaths.
- **Secondary results:**
    - cerebrospinal fluid glycosaminoglycans were decreased in all participants; these decreases were maintained
    - Cognitive function was stabilized in 3 of 4 testable participants at month 55
- **Safety:** Most serious AEs were related to the IT drug delivery device; only 2 serious AEs were related solely to idursulfase-IT.
- **Conclusions:** These long-term results support the clinical development of idursulfase-IT for patients with MPS II with cognitive impairment.
- **Journal:** Genet Med, 2022
- **Industry sponsored:** False
