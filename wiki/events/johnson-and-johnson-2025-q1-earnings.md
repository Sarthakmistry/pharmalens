## Johnson & Johnson Reports Q1 2025 Earnings with Mixed Performance Amidst STELARA Decline

**Date:** 2025-04-23 | **Type:** earnings_signal | **Signal:** neutral

### Summary
Johnson & Johnson reported Q1 2025 worldwide sales of $21.9 billion, a 2.4% total increase with 4.2% operational growth. This performance was significantly impacted by a 32.3% decline in STELARA (ustekinumab) sales due to biosimilar competition and Medicare Part D redesign, which negatively affected overall operational sales by approximately 4.7%. Strong growth in oncology products, including DARZALEX (daratumumab) and CARVYKTI (ciltacabtagene autoleucel), partially offset these declines.

### Market implication
Despite overall operational growth, the significant decline in STELARA sales due to biosimilar competition and the impact of Medicare Part D redesign created a neutral signal for Johnson & Johnson's Q1 2025 performance, partially offset by strong oncology growth.
