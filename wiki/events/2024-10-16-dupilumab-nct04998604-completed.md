## Dupilumab Phase 4 Trial (NCT04998604) Demonstrates Superiority Over Omalizumab in CRSwNP with Asthma

**Date:** 2024-10-16 | **Type:** trial_completion | **Signal:** neutral

### Summary
A Phase 4 randomized controlled trial (NCT04998604) for Dupilumab, sponsored by [[sanofi]], has completed, demonstrating Dupilumab's superiority over omalizumab in patients with severe Chronic Rhinosinusitis with Nasal Polyps (CRSwNP) and coexisting asthma. The trial enrolled 360 participants and reached primary completion on October 16, 2024, with results published in Lancet Respir Med in 2025.

### Market implication
This positive clinical outcome for Dupilumab strengthens its profile in type 2 respiratory diseases, potentially impacting its market position against competitors like omalizumab.

### Affected entities
- Company: [[sanofi]]

### Clinical evidence
- **Study design:** RCT
- **Sample size:** 360
- **Comparator:** omalizumab
- **Primary outcome:** change from baseline in endoscopic nasal polyp score and University of Pennsylvania Smell Identification Test (UPSIT) at 24 weeks
- **Primary result:** Least squares mean differences in change from baseline dupilumab over omalizumab were: nasal polyp score -1·60 (95% CI -1·96 to -1·25; p<0·0001) and UPSIT 8·0 (6·3 to 9·7; p<0·0001).
- **Secondary results:** []
- **Safety:** 115 (64%) of 179 participants in the dupilumab group and 116 (67%) of 173 participants in the omalizumab group reported treatment-emergent adverse events, the most common of which were nasopharyngitis, accidental overdose, headache, upper respiratory tract infection, and cough. There were no deaths in the study.
- **Conclusions:** Dupilumab was superior to omalizumab in patients with severe CRSwNP and coexisting asthma. These findings support the efficacy of dupilumab in patients with type 2 respiratory diseases versus an active biologic comparator, the known safety profiles of dupilumab and omalizumab, and could enable better treatment targeting for patients with CRSwNP and asthma in clinical practice.
- **Journal:** Lancet Respir Med, 2025
- **Industry sponsored:** True
