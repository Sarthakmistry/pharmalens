## Dupilumab (NCT03560466) Phase 3 Trial Completes, Confirming Acceptable Long-Term Safety in Children with Asthma

**Date:** 2025-04-01 | **Type:** trial_completion | **Signal:** neutral

### Summary
A Phase 3 observational study (NCT03560466), known as EXCURSION, for Dupilumab, sponsored by Sanofi, has completed, with primary completion on April 1, 2025. The study evaluated the long-term safety of Dupilumab in children aged 6-11 years with moderate-to-severe asthma who had previously completed the VOYAGE study. The trial's findings indicate that Dupilumab was well tolerated with an acceptable safety profile, consistent with observations from the parent study.

### Market implication
The confirmation of Dupilumab's long-term safety profile in pediatric asthma patients supports its continued use and market position, without immediate significant impact on valuation.

### Clinical evidence
- **Study design:** observational
- **Sample size:** 365
- **Comparator:** None
- **Primary outcome:** the number and proportion of patients with any treatment-emergent adverse event (TEAE) during the 52-week study period in the overall population (defined as children aged 6-11 years old with moderate-to-severe asthma who previously completed VOYAGE)
- **Primary result:** In the overall population, 232 (63·6%) of 365 patients experienced at least one TEAE (dupilumab/dupilumab: 147 [61·3%]; placebo/dupilumab: 85 [68·0%])
- **Secondary results:** []
- **Safety:** The safety profile and proportion of patients reporting TEAEs were consistent with those observed during the parent study (VOYAGE). The most frequently reported TEAEs were nasopharyngitis, pharyngitis, and upper respiratory tract infections.
- **Conclusions (verbatim):** In EXCURSION, long-term treatment with dupilumab was well tolerated with an acceptable safety profile.
- **Journal:** Lancet Respir Med, 2024
- **Industry sponsored:** True
