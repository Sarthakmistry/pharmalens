## Brigatinib Phase 3 Trial Fails to Show Superiority Over Alectinib in ALK+ NSCLC

**Date:** 2024-02-27 | **Type:** trial_completion | **Signal:** bearish

### Summary
A Phase 3 randomized controlled trial (NCT03596866) evaluating Brigatinib against Alectinib in crizotinib-pretreated ALK-positive non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) completed its primary endpoint. The study, sponsored by Takeda, met its futility criterion as Brigatinib was not found to be superior to Alectinib for progression-free survival.

### Market implication
This outcome represents a negative signal for Takeda's Brigatinib in the competitive ALK+ NSCLC treatment landscape, as it failed to demonstrate an advantage over an existing therapy.

### Clinical evidence
- **Study design**: RCT
- **Sample size**: 248
- **Comparator**: alectinib
- **Primary outcome**: blinded independent review committee-assessed progression-free survival (PFS)
- **Primary result**: Median blinded independent review committee-assessed PFS was 19.3 months with brigatinib and 19.2 months with alectinib (hazard ratio = 0.97 [95% confidence interval: 0.66-1.42], p = 0.8672]). The study met futility criterion.
- **Secondary results**:
    - Overall survival was immature (41 events [17%])
    - Exploratory analyses pooled across the treatment groups revealed median PFS of 11.1 versus 22.5 months in patients with versus without ctDNA-detectable ALK fusion at baseline (hazard ratio: 0.48 [95% confidence interval: 0.32-0.71])
- **Safety**: Treatment-related adverse events in more than 30% of patients (brigatinib, alectinib) were elevated levels of blood creatine phosphokinase (70%, 29%), aspartate aminotransferase (53%, 38%), and alanine aminotransferase (40%, 36%).
- **Conclusions**: Brigatinib was not superior to alectinib for PFS in crizotinib-pretreated ALK+ NSCLC. Safety was consistent with the well-established and unique profiles of each drug. The low proportion of patients with ctDNA-detectable ALK fusion may account for prolonged PFS with both drugs in ALTA-3.
- **Journal**: J Thorac Oncol., 2023
- **Industry sponsored**: False
