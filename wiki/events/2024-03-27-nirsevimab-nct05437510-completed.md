## Sanofi's Nirsevimab Phase 3 Trial (NCT05437510) Demonstrates High Efficacy Against RSV Hospitalization

**Date:** 2024-03-27 | **Type:** trial_completion | **Signal:** bullish

### Summary
Nirsevimab, developed by Sanofi, successfully completed its Phase 3 trial (NCT05437510), showing significant efficacy in preventing hospitalizations due to RSV-associated lower respiratory tract infection in infants. The study reported an 82.7% efficacy rate, with no apparent safety concerns, providing sustained protection for at least six months. These positive results highlight nirsevimab's potential to substantially reduce the burden of infant RSV.

### Market implication
This positive trial completion strengthens [[sanofi]]'s position in the RSV prevention market, potentially leading to increased market share and revenue.

### Clinical evidence
- **Study design:** RCT
- **Sample size:** 8057 patients
- **Comparator:** standard of care
- **Primary outcome:** incidence of hospitalisations due to RSV-associated lower respiratory tract infection up to 180 days after nirsevimab administration or randomisation in all randomised participants
- **Primary result:** Up to 180 days, 12 (0·3%) of 4038 infants in the nirsevimab group and 68 (1·7%) of 4019 infants in the standard care group had been hospitalised for RSV-associated lower respiratory tract infection, corresponding to a nirsevimab efficacy of 82·7% (95% CI 67·8-91·5; p<0·0001)
- **Secondary results:**
    - Most participants experienced grade 1 (2759 [68·7%] of 4016 in the nirsevimab group; 2696 [67·1%] of 4018 in the standard care group) treatment-emergent adverse events
    - grade 2 (1447 [36·0%] of 4016 in the nirsevimab group; 1436 [35·7%] of 4018 in the standard care group) treatment-emergent adverse events
- **Safety:** no apparent safety concerns were raised up to 365 days after dosing
- **Conclusions (verbatim):** Nirsevimab offers consistent and sustained protection against hospitalisation due to RSV-associated lower respiratory tract infection for at least 6 months. This finding provides global health systems greater flexibility when implementing nirsevimab, providing substantial benefit in the ongoing effort to reduce the burden of infant RSV and the potential wider public health value.
- **Journal:** Lancet Child Adolesc Health, 2025
