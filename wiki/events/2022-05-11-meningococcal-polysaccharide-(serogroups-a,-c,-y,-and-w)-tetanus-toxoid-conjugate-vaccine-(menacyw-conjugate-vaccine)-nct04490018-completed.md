## Sanofi's MenACYW-TT Meningococcal Vaccine Phase 3 Trial Completes with Positive Results

**Date:** 2022-05-11 | **Type:** trial_completion | **Signal:** bullish

### Summary
A Phase 3 randomized controlled trial (NCT04490018) evaluating Sanofi's Meningococcal polysaccharide (serogroups a, c, y, and w) tetanus toxoid conjugate vaccine (MenACYW-TT) completed on May 11, 2022. The study demonstrated non-inferiority of MenACYW-TT compared to Nimenrix (MCV4-TT) for seroprotection against serogroups A, C, W, and Y, with a comparable safety profile. These results support the vaccine's efficacy and safety, including concomitant administration with other adolescent vaccines.

### Market implication
The positive completion of this Phase 3 trial strengthens Sanofi's vaccine pipeline, indicating potential for a new meningococcal vaccine option and supporting future regulatory submissions.

### Clinical evidence
- **Study design:** RCT
- **Sample size:** 463
- **Comparator:** Nimenrix (MCV4-TT)
- **Primary outcome:** non-inferiority of the seroprotection rate (human serum bactericidal assay [hSBA] titer ≥ 1:8) to serogroups A, C, W, and Y 30 days post-vaccination with a single dose of MenACYW-TT or MCV4-TT
- **Primary result:** Non-inferiority based on seroprotection was demonstrated for MenACYW-TT versus MCV4-TT for all serogroups.
- **Secondary results:**
    - Immune responses were comparable whether MenACYW-TT was administered alone or concomitantly with Tdap-IPV and 9vHPV.
    - Post-vaccination hSBA GMTs were higher in MenACYW-TT vs. MCV4-TT for serogroups C, Y, and W and comparable for serogroup A.
    - The percentages of participants with an hSBA vaccine seroresponse were higher in MenACYW-TT vs. MCV4-TT for all serogroups.
    - For serogroup C, higher GMTs were observed in both MenC-naïve or -primed participants vaccinated with MenACYW-TT vs. MCV4-TT.
    - Seroprotection and seroresponse were higher in MenC-naïve participants vaccinated with MenACYW-TT vs. MCV4-TT and comparable in MenC-primed.
- **Safety:** The safety profiles were comparable between groups and no new safety concerns were identified. No serious side effects were experienced. Common non-serious side effects included injection site pain, muscle pain, and headache.
- **Conclusions:** These data support the concomitant administration of MenACYW-TT with 9vHPV and Tdap-IPV vaccines in adolescents.
- **Journal:** Infect Dis Ther., 2024
- **Industry sponsored:** True
