## Merck Reports Q3 2025 Earnings Driven by Oncology and Cardiovascular Growth, Announces Strategic Acquisitions and IRA Impacts

**Date:** 2025-11-05 | **Type:** earnings_signal | **Signal:** neutral

### Summary
Merck reported worldwide sales of $17.3 billion for Q3 2025, a 4% increase year-over-year, primarily fueled by strong performance in its oncology, cardiovascular, diabetes, and animal health franchises. Keytruda ([[pembrolizumab]]) and Januvia ([[sitagliptin]]) contributed significantly to growth, alongside the ongoing launch of Winrevair and Capvaxive. The company also announced the acquisition of Verona Pharma for $10.5 billion, securing Ohtuvayre (ensifentrine) for COPD, and a $700 million funding arrangement with Blackstone Life Sciences for the development of MK-2870 (sacituzumab tirumotecan). However, declines in vaccines (Gardasil/Gardasil 9), immunology (Simponi, Remicade due to rights transfer), and virology (Lagevrio) partially offset these gains, and the Inflation Reduction Act continues to impact pricing for drugs like Januvia, Janumet, and Keytruda.

### Market implication
Merck's Q3 2025 results indicate continued strength in key growth areas like oncology and cardiovascular, supported by strategic pipeline investments and acquisitions, while facing headwinds from patent expirations, rights transfers, and government pricing pressures under the IRA.
