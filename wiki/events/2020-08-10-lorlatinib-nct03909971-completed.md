## Pfizer's Lorlatinib Phase 2 Trial (NCT03909971) for NSCLC Completes with Robust Long-Term Efficacy and Safety Data

**Date:** 2020-08-10 | **Type:** trial_completion | **Signal:** bullish

### Summary
Pfizer announced the completion of its Phase 2 trial (NCT03909971) for lorlatinib in previously treated ALK-positive NSCLC patients. The study, which enrolled 109 patients, demonstrated robust overall and intracranial clinical activity with no new safety signals, supporting its use in this patient population.

### Market implication
The positive long-term data for lorlatinib reinforces its clinical profile and potential market position in ALK-positive NSCLC, positively impacting Pfizer.

### Clinical evidence
- **Study design:** null
- **Sample size:** 109 patients
- **Comparator:** null
- **Primary outcome:** progression-free survival (PFS)
- **Primary result:** Median PFS (95% CI) per independent central review was 26.3 months (16.6-35.9) in cohort 1 and 5.6 months (2.9-12.4) in cohort 2.
- **Secondary results:**
    - Median OS (95% CI) was not reached (NR; NR-NR) in cohort 1 and 21.9 months (11.9-NR) in cohort 2.
    - Median intracranial time to progression (95% CI) was NR (NR-NR) in cohort 1 and NR (9.7 months-NR) in cohort 2.
- **Safety:** No new safety signals emerged with long-term treatment.
- **Conclusions:** The long-term data confirm robust overall and intracranial clinical activity of lorlatinib, with no new safety signals emerging. These results support using lorlatinib in Chinese patients with previously treated ALK-positive NSCLC with or without brain metastases.
- **Journal:** Clin Lung Cancer, 2024
- **Industry sponsored:** True
