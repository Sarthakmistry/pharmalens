## Sanofi's Dupilumab sar231893 Trial NCT04718870 Completes, Demonstrating Restored Skin Barrier Function in Children with Atopic Dermatitis

**Date:** 2022-09-07 | **Type:** trial_completion | **Signal:** bullish

### Summary
A Phase 4 clinical trial (NCT04718870) sponsored by Sanofi, investigating Dupilumab sar231893, has completed, showing positive results in restoring skin barrier function in children with Atopic Dermatitis. The study found significant improvements in transepidermal water loss (TEWL) and epidermal thickness (TE), sustained over 28 weeks, indicating a positive impact on skin health.

### Market implication
This positive trial completion for Dupilumab sar231893 could reinforce Sanofi's position in dermatology, potentially leading to further development or label expansion for the drug.

### Clinical evidence
- **Study design:** null
- **Sample size:** 23
- **Comparator:** matched healthy volunteers
- **Primary outcome:** Transepidermal water loss (TEWL) before (basal) and after skin tape stripping and epidermal thickness (TE), measured by optical coherence tomography
- **Primary result:** Mean ± SD basal TEWL was significantly higher in AD lesional/nonlesional skin at baseline (55.1 ± 5.5/28.0 ± 3.1 g × m-2 × h-1) than in healthy skin (14.0 ± 1.6) and decreased to 30.3 ± 3.1/22.0 ± 2.6 at week 16 and to 29.7 ± 4.3/18.3 ± 2.1 at week 28. Baseline mean ± SD TE in lesional/nonlesional skin (251.1 ± 25.8/166.1 ± 15.2 µm) was significantly higher than in healthy skin (118.2 ± 5.1) and decreased to 196.8 ± 18.4/141.9 ± 11.9 at week 16 and to 160.4 ± 12.9/142.7 ± 15.6 at week 28. At weeks 16 and 28, there was no significant difference between TEWL and epidermal thickness in AD lesional and nonlesional skin vs healthy skin.
- **Secondary results:** TEWL after 5 of 20 skin tape stripping improved similarly.
- **Safety:** null
- **Conclusions:** Using noninvasive techniques to monitor epithelial function, dupilumab treatment restored skin barrier function in children with AD. Improvements achieved at week 16 were sustained to week 28.
- **Journal:** Ann Allergy Asthma Immunol., 2026
- **Industry sponsored:** False
