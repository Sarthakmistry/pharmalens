## Sanofi's RSV vaccine candidate (NCT04491877) demonstrates promising immunogenicity in Phase 1/2 trial

**Date:** 2023-04-13 | **Type:** trial_completion | **Signal:** bullish

### Summary
Sanofi announced the completion of its Phase 1/2 trial (NCT04491877) for an RSV vaccine candidate, referred to as RSV vaccine formulation 1. The study, which included 180 participants, showed promising immunogenicity profiles at both low and high dose strengths among infants and toddlers, with no identified safety concerns. This positive outcome suggests potential for further development of the vaccine.

### Market implication
The promising immunogenicity and safety profile of Sanofi's RSV vaccine candidate could support its continued development, potentially enhancing Sanofi's vaccine pipeline.

### Clinical evidence
- **Study design:** RCT
- **Sample size:** 180
- **Comparator:** placebo
- **Primary outcome:** geometric mean titers of RSV A serum neutralizing antibody after vaccinations 1 (day 56) and 2 (day 84) among RSV-naive participants at baseline
- **Primary result:** Neutralizing antibody titers among RSV-naive participants were 83.7 (95% confidence interval (CI), 49.5 to 142.0), 79.4 (95% CI, 47.2 to 134.0), and 20.6 (95% CI, 16.4 to 25.9) post vaccination 1, and 142.0 (95% CI, 86.4 to 232.2), 107.0 (95% CI, 70.0 to 163.0), and 26.3 (95% CI, 18.8 to 37.0) post vaccination 2, in the LD, HD, and placebo groups, respectively.
- **Secondary results:**
    - Solicited site reactions were reported by 83.1%, 74.5%, and 68.9% of participants post vaccination 1, and 75.6%, 77.8%, and 55.6% post vaccination 2, in the LD, HD, and placebo groups, respectively.
    - Solicited systemic reactions were reported by 79.7%, 73.2%, and 77.0% of participants post vaccination 1, and 66.7%, 66.7%, and 48.1% post vaccination 2, in the LD, HD, and placebo groups, respectively.
- **Safety:** No unsolicited systemic adverse events occurred within 30 minutes post vaccination in the LD or HD groups. The vaccine was without identified safety concerns.
- **Conclusions:** The RSVt vaccine demonstrated promising immunogenicity profiles at both LD and HD strengths among infants and toddlers, without identified safety concerns. (Funded by Sanofi; ClinicalTrials.gov number, NCT04491877).
- **Journal:** NEJM Evid., 2025
- **Industry sponsored:** True
