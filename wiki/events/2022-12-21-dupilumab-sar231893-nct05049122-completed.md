## Sanofi's Dupilumab Sar231893 Trial NCT05049122 Completes with Positive Results

**Date:** 2022-12-21 | **Type:** trial_completion | **Signal:** bullish

### Summary
On December 21, 2022, Sanofi announced the completion of its Phase 4 trial NCT05049122 for Dupilumab sar231893. The trial, which investigated dupilumab monotherapy in Japanese individuals with symptomatic Chronic Rhinosinusitis with Nasal Polyps (CRSwNP), concluded that the drug was effective and well tolerated, with a high proportion of responders at week 24.

### Market implication
The positive clinical findings from this trial could support future market positioning and potential label expansions for Dupilumab sar231893, positively impacting Sanofi.

### Affected entities
- Drug: Dupilumab sar231893
- Company: [[sanofi]]
- Indication: CRSwNP

### Clinical evidence
- **Study design:** observational
- **Sample size:** 25 patients
- **Comparator:** null
- **Primary outcome:** the proportion of responders at week 24
- **Primary result:** At week 24, 23 participants (92.0%) were considered responders to dupilumab.
- **Secondary results:** Reductions from baseline to week 24 in bilateral endoscopic NPS (mean change -2.4), nasal congestion severity score (-1.58), Lund-Mackay scores (-5.6)
- **Safety:** Over 52 weeks, 16 participants (69.6%) experienced a treatment-emergent adverse event (TEAE). Most TEAEs were mild in severity; moderate and severe TEAEs were reported in five (21.7%) and one (4.3%) participant, respectively.
- **Conclusions (verbatim):** Dupilumab monotherapy was effective and well tolerated in Japanese individuals with symptomatic CRSwNP.
- **Journal:** Laryngoscope, 2025
- **Industry sponsored:** False
