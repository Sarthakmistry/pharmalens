## Sanofi's CoV2 preS dTM-AS03 Boosters Show Robust Neutralizing Antibodies in Phase 2 Trial Completion (NCT04762680)

**Date:** 2023-06-29 | **Type:** trial_completion | **Signal:** bullish

### Summary
Sanofi announced the completion of its Phase 2 trial (NCT04762680) for its CoV2 preS dTM-AS03 boosters. The study demonstrated acceptable safety and elicited robust neutralizing antibodies against multiple SARS-CoV-2 variants, regardless of the priming vaccine, indicating positive progress for the vaccine candidate.

### Market implication
This positive trial completion could support further development and potential market entry for Sanofi's COVID-19 vaccine program, reinforcing its position in the vaccine market.

### Clinical evidence
- **Study design:** RCT
- **Sample size:** 3096
- **Comparator:** MV(D614) primary series in unvaccinated adults
- **Primary outcome:** Anti-D614G and anti-B.1.351 antibodies evaluated using pseudovirus neutralization (PsVN) assay 14 days post-booster in 18-55-year-old BNT162b2-primed participants, compared with pre-booster and D36 in controls.
- **Primary result:** Among BNT162b2-primed adults (18-55 years), geometric means of the individual post-booster versus pre-booster titer ratio (95% confidence interval [CI]) were: for MV (D614), 23.37 (18.58-29.38) (anti-D614G); for MV(B.1.351), 35.41 (26.71-46.95) (anti-B.1.351); and for BiV, 14.39 (11.39-18.28) (anti-D614G) and 34.18 (25.84-45.22) (anti-B.1.351). GMT ratios (98.3% CI) versus post-primary vaccination GMTs in controls, were: for MV(D614) booster, 2.16 (1.69; 2.75) [anti-D614G]; for MV(B.1.351), 1.96 (1.54; 2.50) [anti-B.1.351]; and for BiV, 2.34 (1.84; 2.96) [anti-D614G] and 1.39 (1.09; 1.77) [anti-B.1.351].
- **Secondary results:**
    - All booster formulations elicited cross-neutralizing antibodies against Omicron BA.2 (across priming vaccine subgroups), Omicron BA.1 (BNT162b2-primed participants) and Omicron BA.4/5 (BNT162b2-primed participants and MV D614-primed participants).
    - Similar patterns in antibody responses were observed for participants aged ≥56 years.
- **Safety:** Reactogenicity tended to be transient and mild-to-moderate severity in all booster groups. No safety concerns were identified.
- **Conclusions:** CoV2 preS dTM-AS03 boosters demonstrated acceptable safety and elicited robust neutralizing antibodies against multiple variants, regardless of priming vaccine.
