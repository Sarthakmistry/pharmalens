## Sanofi's Dupilumab sar231893 Phase 3 Trial Shows Positive Results in Prurigo Nodularis (PN)

**Date:** 2021-08-30 | **Type:** trial_completion | **Signal:** bullish

### Summary
Sanofi announced the completion of a Phase 3 randomized controlled trial (NCT04202679) for Dupilumab sar231893, demonstrating nominally significant improvements in signs, symptoms, and quality of life for patients with Prurigo Nodularis (PN). The study's positive outcomes support the continued treatment of PN with Dupilumab sar231893.

### Market implication
These positive Phase 3 results could strengthen [[sanofi]]'s pipeline in dermatology and support future regulatory submissions for Dupilumab sar231893 in Prurigo Nodularis, potentially expanding its market presence.

### Affected entities
- Drug: Dupilumab sar231893
- Company: [[sanofi]]
- Indication: Prurigo Nodularis (PN)

### Clinical evidence
- **Study design:** RCT
- **Sample size:** 243
- **Comparator:** placebo
- **Primary outcome:** ≥ 9-point reduction from baseline in Dermatology Life Quality Index (DLQI) score
- **Primary result:** 61.1% achieved a ≥ 9-point reduction from baseline in DLQI score (nominal P vs placebo significant)
- **Secondary results:**
    - 55.8% a PGIS score of "none" or "mild" (nominal P vs placebo significant)
    - 53.7% PAS-75 (nominal P vs placebo significant)
    - 45.7% the less stringent itch and skin lesion composite endpoint (nominal P vs placebo significant)
- **Safety:** None
- **Conclusions:** Dupilumab treatment versus placebo resulted in nominally significant improvements in signs, symptoms, and quality of life in patients with PN without optimal response by week 24, supporting the benefits of continued treatment. These results highlight the need to develop a treat-to-target strategy for the long-term management of PN.
- **Journal:** Dermatol Ther (Heidelb), 2026
- **Industry sponsored:** None
