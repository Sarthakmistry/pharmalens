## Sanofi's Phase 3 Quadrivalent Influenza Vaccine (QIV-HD) Trial Terminated, Published Results Show Benefit Trend

**Date:** 2020-05-31 | **Type:** trial_termination | **Signal:** neutral

### Summary
Sanofi's Phase 3 trial (NCT04137887) evaluating Quadrivalent influenza vaccine (split virion, inactivated) high-dose (QIV-HD) was terminated on May 31, 2020, due to the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic. The study, which compared QIV-HD to Standard-dose inactivated influenza vaccine quadrivalent, northern hemisphere strains (QIV-SD), published its results in 2024, indicating a trend towards benefit for QIV-HD in preventing respiratory and cardiovascular hospitalizations, though statistical power was insufficient.

### Market implication
The termination of the trial, while influenced by external factors, means Sanofi will need further studies to establish statistical significance for Quadrivalent influenza vaccine (split virion, inactivated) high-dose (QIV-HD)'s benefit in preventing hospitalizations.

### Clinical evidence
**Trial:** NCT04137887
**Phase:** PHASE3
**Status:** TERMINATED
**Enrollment:** 33096 (ACTUAL)
**Primary completion:** 2020-05-31
**Has results:** True

**Study design:** RCT
**Sample size:** 33,093
**Comparator:** QIV-SD
**Primary outcome:** first occurrence of an unscheduled acute respiratory or cardiovascular hospitalization (ICD-10 primary discharge J/I codes), from ≥14 days post-vaccination until May 31
**Primary result:** The rVE of QIV-HD versus QIV-SD to prevent respiratory/cardiovascular hospitalizations was 5.5% (95% CI, -12.4 to 20.7).
**Secondary results:**
- rVE estimates of QIV-HD versus QIV-SD for prevention of respiratory hospitalizations were 5.4% (95% CI, -28.0 to 30.1)
- rVE estimates of QIV-HD versus QIV-SD for prevention of cardiovascular hospitalizations were 7.1% (95% CI, -15.0 to 25.0)
**Safety:** Serious adverse reactions were <0.01% in both groups.
**Conclusions:** Despite insufficient statistical power due to the impact of COVID-19, rVE point estimates demonstrated a trend toward a benefit of QIV-HD over QIV-SD. QIV-HD was associated with lower respiratory or cardiovascular hospitalization rates than QIV-SD, with a comparable safety profile. Adequately powered studies conducted over multiple influenza seasons are needed to determine statistical significance of QIV-HD compared with QIV-SD against preventing respiratory and cardiovascular hospitalizations.
**Journal:** Influenza Other Respir Viruses, 2024
**Industry sponsored:** True
