## Sanofi-sponsored trial NCT04969276 on co-administration of influenza and mRNA COVID-19 vaccines completes with positive safety and immunogenicity results

**Date:** 2022-02-08 | **Type:** trial_completion | **Signal:** bullish

### Summary
A Sanofi-sponsored Phase 2 randomized controlled trial (NCT04969276) investigating the concomitant administration of high-dose quadrivalent influenza vaccine (QIV-HD) and an mRNA-1273 vaccine booster in older adults completed on February 8, 2022. The study, which enrolled 306 participants, found similar reactogenicity and immunogenicity profiles for both vaccines whether administered together or alone, with no safety concerns or immune interference observed.

### Market implication
The positive results support the feasibility and safety of co-administering influenza and mRNA COVID-19 vaccines, potentially streamlining vaccination efforts and increasing uptake, which is favorable for vaccine manufacturers like [[sanofi]].

### Clinical evidence
- **Study design:** RCT
- **Sample size:** 296
- **Comparator:** QIV-HD alone | mRNA-1273 vaccine alone
- **Primary outcome:** Safety and immunogenicity of concomitant administration of high-dose quadrivalent influenza vaccine (QIV-HD) and a mRNA-1273 vaccine booster dose in older adults.
- **Primary result:** Reactogenicity profiles were similar between the coadministration and mRNA-1273 groups, with lower rates in the QIV-HD group. Immunogenicity for both influenza and SARS-CoV-2 vaccines was similar whether administered concomitantly or alone.
- **Secondary results:**
    - Frequency of solicited injection site reactions: coadministration 86.0% (95% CI 77.6-92.1), mRNA-1273 91.3% (84.2-96.0), QIV-HD 61.8% (50.9-71.9).
    - Frequency of solicited systemic reactions: coadministration 80.0% (95% CI 70.8-87.3), mRNA-1273 83.7% (75.1-90.2), QIV-HD 49.4% (38.7-60.2).
    - Unsolicited adverse events: coadministration 17.0% (95% CI 10.2-25.8), mRNA-1273 14.4% (8.3-22.7), QIV-HD 10.9% (5.3-19.1).
    - Medically attended adverse events: 7 participants (3 in coadministration, 1 in QIV-HD, 3 in mRNA-1273 group).
    - Influenza A/H1N1 GMTs: coadministration 363 (95% CI 276-476), QIV-HD 366 (272-491).
    - Influenza A/H3N2 GMTs: coadministration 286 (233-352), QIV-HD 315 (257-386).
    - Influenza B/Yamagata GMTs: coadministration 429 (350-525), QIV-HD 471 (378-588).
    - Influenza B/Victoria GMTs: coadministration 377 (325-438), QIV-HD 390 (327-465).
    - SARS-CoV-2 binding antibody GMCs: coadministration 7634 (95% CI 6445-9042), mRNA-1273 7904 (6883-9077).
- **Safety:** No serious adverse events, adverse events of special interest, or deaths were reported. No safety concerns or immune interference were observed.
- **Conclusions:** No safety concerns or immune interference were observed for concomitant administration of QIV-HD with mRNA-1273 booster in adults aged 65 years and older, supporting co-administration recommendations.
- **Journal:** Lancet Respir Med, 2022
- **Industry sponsored:** True
