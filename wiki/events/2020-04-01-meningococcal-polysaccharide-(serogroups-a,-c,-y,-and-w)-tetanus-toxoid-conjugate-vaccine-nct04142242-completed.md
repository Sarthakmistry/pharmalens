## Sanofi's Meningococcal Vaccine Trial NCT04142242 Demonstrates Long-Term Immune Persistence and Robust Booster Response

**Date:** 2020-04-01 | **Type:** trial_completion | **Signal:** bullish

### Summary
A Phase 3 clinical trial (NCT04142242) for Meningococcal polysaccharide (serogroups a, c, y, and w) tetanus toxoid conjugate vaccine, sponsored by [[sanofi]], completed on April 1, 2020. The study showed that the vaccine provides long-term immune persistence for at least 5 years and elicits a robust anamnestic response to a booster dose in adults ≥56 years of age, supporting its use for primary and booster immunization.

### Market implication
These positive results strengthen the market position of Sanofi's meningococcal vaccine, indicating strong efficacy and potential for broad application in the target population.

### Clinical evidence
- Study design: None
- Sample size: 100 participants
- Comparator: None
- Primary outcome: Immune persistence and immune response to a booster dose of MenACYW-TT
- Primary result: One month post-booster, seroprotection rates of 100% for all serogroups (A, C, W, and Y) were observed, and GMTs increased 10- to 20-fold compared to pre-booster levels.
- Secondary results:
    - Seroprotection rates remained high (≥90%) for MenA, C, W, and Y at 5 years post-primary vaccination.
    - Geometric mean titers (GMTs) remained above baseline for MenA, W, and Y, but the GMT for MenC declined below baseline.
- Safety: The booster dose was well tolerated.
- Conclusions (verbatim): These findings demonstrate that MenACYW-TT provides long-term immune persistence for at least 5 years and elicits a robust anamnestic response to a booster dose in adults ≥56 years of age. This supports the use of MenACYW-TT for primary vaccination and booster immunization in this population.
- Journal: Hum Vaccin Immunother, 2024
- Industry sponsored: None
