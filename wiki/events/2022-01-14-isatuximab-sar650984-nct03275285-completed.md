## Isatuximab combination trial for relapsed/refractory multiple myeloma completes, shows positive secondary outcomes

**Date:** 2022-01-14 | **Type:** trial_completion | **Signal:** neutral

### Summary
A Phase 3 randomized controlled trial (NCT03275285) evaluating isatuximab in combination with carfilzomib-dexamethasone for relapsed or refractory multiple myeloma has completed. While the primary outcome of overall survival did not show a significant difference, improvements were observed in secondary endpoints like time to next treatment and second-progression-free survival. The study concludes that the combination is a key treatment option.

### Market implication
The mixed results, with a primary endpoint miss but positive secondary data, suggest a neutral impact on the drug's market outlook.

### Clinical evidence
- **Study design:** RCT
- **Sample size:** 302
- **Comparator:** carfilzomib-dexamethasone
- **Primary outcome:** overall survival
- **Primary result:** Median overall survival (in months) was not reached (NR; 95% CI 52·17-NR) in the isatuximab group and was 50·60 months (38·93-NR) in the control group (hazard ratio [HR] 0·855 [95% CI 0·608-1·202], nominal one-sided p=0·18).
- **Secondary results:** Improvements in time to next treatment (HR 0·583 [95% CI 0·429-0·792], nominal one-sided p=0·0002) were observed in the isatuximab group. second-progression-free survival (0·663 [0·491-0·895], nominal one-sided p=0·0035) were observed in the isatuximab group.
- **Safety:** The most common treatment-emergent adverse events were infusion reactions (82 [46%] patients in the isatuximab group and four [3%] in the control group) and upper respiratory tract infections (71 [40%] and 34 [28%], respectively). Discontinuations due to treatment-emergent adverse events were similar between treatment groups (24 [14%] in the isatuximab group and 22 [18%] in the control group). 12 (7%) patients in the isatuximab group and six (5%) patients in the control group had a treatment-related adverse event with a fatal outcome.
- **Conclusions:** At the time of the current analysis, a difference in overall survival could not be detected between the treatment groups, and no new safety signals were observed. Collectively, the evidence suggests that isatuximab plus carfilzomib-dexamethasone is a key treatment for patients with relapsed or refractory multiple myeloma.
