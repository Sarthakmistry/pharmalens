## Sanofi's Dupilumab Trial NCT04456673 Completes Phase 3 with Positive Results

**Date:** 2024-02-28 | **Type:** trial_completion | **Signal:** bullish

### Summary
Sanofi announced the completion of its Phase 3 trial (NCT04456673) for Dupilumab sar231893 on February 28, 2024. The randomized controlled trial, involving 1872 patients, demonstrated that Dupilumab significantly improved clinically significant outcomes by 31% compared to placebo, as measured by a win ratio based on hierarchical composite endpoints. These positive findings suggest a potential new treatment option for the studied condition.

### Market implication
The positive Phase 3 trial results for Dupilumab sar231893 could enhance Sanofi's pipeline value and market position.

### Clinical evidence
- Study design: RCT
- Sample size: 1872 patients
- Comparator: placebo
- Primary outcome: Win ratio based on hierarchical composite end points (occurrence of death, hospitalization, emergency department visits, exacerbations, lung function decline, symptoms, and quality of life)
- Primary result: Dupilumab increased the likelihood of improved clinically significant outcomes by 31% (win ratio, 1.31; 95% CI, 1.15-1.48)
- Secondary results: For any given untied pair of dupilumab vs placebo recipients, the estimated probability of a better outcome for dupilumab recipients was 57%
- Safety: None
- Conclusions: Our results show that dupilumab improved patient- and clinician-important outcomes by reducing the risks of clinically significant severe outcomes, together with improving symptoms and lung function. This analysis provides meaningful insights into the value of composite end points and win ratio analysis that could guide future phase 3 trial designs.
- Journal: Chest, 2026
- Industry sponsored: True
