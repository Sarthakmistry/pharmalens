## Vertex's Vx-147 (inaxaplin) Phase 2 Trial (NCT04340362) Completes with Positive Results for Focal Segmental Glomerulosclerosis

**Date:** 2021-11-11 | **Type:** trial_completion | **Signal:** bullish

### Summary
Vertex Pharmaceuticals announced the completion of its Phase 2 clinical trial (NCT04340362) for Vx-147, also known as inaxaplin, in patients with focal segmental glomerulosclerosis (FSGS) who have two APOL1 genetic variants. The trial demonstrated positive results, showing a significant reduction in proteinuria and a favorable safety profile, indicating potential for the drug in this rare kidney disease.

### Market implication
These positive Phase 2 results for Vx-147 (inaxaplin) could de-risk further development for Vertex in the rare kidney disease space, potentially opening a new market opportunity.

### Clinical evidence
- **Study design**: null
- **Sample size**: 16 patients
- **Comparator**: null
- **Primary outcome**: percent change from the baseline urinary protein-to-creatinine ratio at week 13 in participants who had at least 80% adherence to inaxaplin therapy
- **Primary result**: mean change from the baseline urinary protein-to-creatinine ratio at week 13 was -47.6% (95% confidence interval, -60.0 to -31.3)
- **Secondary results**: []
- **Safety**: Adverse events were mild or moderate in severity; none led to study discontinuation.
- **Conclusions (verbatim)**: Targeted inhibition of APOL1 channel function with inaxaplin reduced proteinuria in participants with two APOL1 variants and focal segmental glomerulosclerosis.
- **Journal**: N Engl J Med, 2023
- **Industry sponsored**: True
