## Takeda's Tak-662 Phase 1/2 Trial (NCT04984889) Completes with Comparable PK and Favorable Safety Profile

**Date:** 2022-03-29 | **Type:** trial_completion | **Signal:** neutral

### Summary
A Phase 1/2 clinical trial (NCT04984889) for Takeda's investigational drug Tak-662, focusing on plasma protein C activity levels and pharmacokinetic (PK) parameters, has completed. The study, conducted in Japanese patients, found PK parameters comparable to Western populations and reported a favorable safety profile with no serious adverse events.

### Market implication
This early-stage data provides foundational pharmacokinetic and safety information for Tak-662, supporting its continued development.

### Affected entities
- Drug: Tak-662
- Company: [[takeda]]

### Clinical evidence
- **Study design:** null
- **Sample size:** 5 patients
- **Comparator:** null
- **Primary outcome:** plasma protein C activity levels and PK parameters
- **Primary result:** The geometric mean (CV%) maximum concentration (Cmax) was 1.679 IU/mL (31.7%) and the geometric mean (CV%) area under the curve (AUCinf) was 21.88 IU·h/mL (47.1%). The median (range) half-life for protein C in plasma was 10.7 (7.35-12.4) hours.
- **Secondary results:** Cmax and AUCinf tended to be higher in older patients (≥20 years old) than in younger patients (<20 years old), whereas half-life was similar regardless of age.
- **Safety:** One patient had a mild treatment-related AE of pyrexia. No serious AEs or deaths were reported.
- **Conclusions:** PK parameters for protein C concentrate in Japanese patients with SCPCD were determined to be comparable to studies in western populations. A single intravenous 80 IU/kg dose was well tolerated, with no serious treatment-related AEs.
- **Journal:** J Blood Med., 2026
- **Industry sponsored:** True
