## Sanofi's Palbociclib Trial NCT03284957 Terminated

**Date:** 2024-11-08 | **Type:** trial_termination | **Signal:** neutral

### Summary
Sanofi's Phase 1/2 clinical trial (NCT03284957), investigating [[palbociclib]] in combination with other agents for advanced breast cancer, has been terminated as of November 8, 2024, after reaching its primary completion date. Separately, clinical findings for Amcenestrant, another drug in oncology-breast, were published, showing clinical activity and good tolerability in ER-positive/HER2-negative advanced breast cancer.

### Market implication
The termination of this Phase 1/2 trial for [[palbociclib]] is likely to have a neutral market impact, as the drug is already established, and the termination reason is not specified as negative.

### Clinical evidence for Amcenestrant (Nat Commun., 2022)
*   **Study design:** None
*   **Sample size:** 103 patients
*   **Comparator:** None
*   **Primary outcome:** Phase 1: safety and tolerability, maximum tolerated dose (MTD) and/or recommended phase 2 dose (RP2D) of amcenestrant. Phase 2: clinical benefit rate (CBR) at 24 weeks.
*   **Primary result:** Phase 1: MTD was not reached; RP2D was 400 mg once daily. Phase 2 (n=85): CBR at 24 weeks was 34.1% (95% CI 24.1-45.2).
*   **Secondary results:**
    *   Median progression-free survival (PFS) was 3.7 months (95% CI 2.0-5.5).
    *   Overall response rate (ORR) was 10.6% (95% CI 4.9-19.7).
    *   In patients with ESR1 mutations (n=30), CBR was 40.0% (95% CI 22.7-59.4), ORR was 16.7% (95% CI 5.7-34.7), and median PFS was 5.5 months (95% CI 2.0-8.3).
    *   In patients without ESR1 mutations (n=55), CBR was 30.9% (95% CI 19.1-44.7), ORR was 7.3% (95% CI 1.5-19.9), and median PFS was 2.0 months (95% CI 1.9-3.7).
*   **Safety:** The most common treatment-emergent adverse events (TEAEs) were grade 1 or 2 and included fatigue (34.0%), nausea (27.2%), and alopecia (26.2%). Grade ≥3 TEAEs occurred in 28.2% of patients, with neutropenia (5.8%) and fatigue (2.9%) being the most common. No deaths were considered treatment-related.
*   **Conclusions:** Amcenestrant 400 mg once daily showed clinical activity in postmenopausal women with ER-positive/HER2-negative advanced breast cancer, including those with ESR1 mutations, and was generally well tolerated. These results support further investigation of amcenestrant in this patient population.
