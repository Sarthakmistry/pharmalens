## Sanofi's Tolebrutinib Phase 3 Trial (HERCULES) Meets Primary Endpoint in Nonrelapsing Secondary Progressive Multiple Sclerosis

**Date:** 2024-08-29 | **Type:** trial_completion | **Signal:** bullish

### Summary
Sanofi announced the completion of its Phase 3 HERCULES trial (NCT04411641) for tolebrutinib in patients with nonrelapsing secondary progressive multiple sclerosis. The study met its primary endpoint, demonstrating a statistically significant reduction in confirmed disability progression compared to placebo. This positive result indicates potential efficacy for tolebrutinib in addressing this challenging neurological condition.

### Market implication
The positive Phase 3 results for tolebrutinib could enhance [[sanofi]]'s pipeline value in multiple sclerosis, potentially leading to future regulatory filings and market opportunities.

### Clinical evidence
- **Study design:** RCT
- **Sample size:** 1131
- **Comparator:** placebo
- **Primary outcome:** confirmed disability progression that was sustained for at least 6 months
- **Primary result:** A smaller percentage of participants in the tolebrutinib group than in the placebo group had confirmed disability progression sustained for at least 6 months (22.6% vs. 30.7%; hazard ratio, 0.69; 95% confidence interval, 0.55 to 0.88; P=0.003)
- **Secondary results:** []
- **Safety:** Serious adverse events occurred in 15.0% of the participants in the tolebrutinib group and in 10.4% of those in the placebo group. A total of 4.0% of the participants in the tolebrutinib group and 1.6% of those in the placebo group had increases in alanine aminotransferase levels to more than 3 times the upper limit of the normal range.
- **Conclusions:** In participants with nonrelapsing secondary progressive multiple sclerosis, the risk of disability progression was lower among those who received treatment with tolebrutinib than among those who received placebo. (Funded by Sanofi; HERCULES ClinicalTrials.gov number, NCT04411641.).
- **Journal:** N Engl J Med, 2025
- **Industry sponsored:** True
