## Sanofi's Rilzabrutinib Phase 2 Trial Shows Positive Results in Immune Thrombocytopenia (ITP)

**Date:** 2023-01-31 | **Type:** trial_completion | **Signal:** bullish

### Summary
A Phase 2 randomized, placebo-controlled trial (NCT03395210) of rilzabrutinib, developed by [[sanofi]], for immune thrombocytopenia (ITP) has completed with positive results. The study demonstrated superior efficacy of rilzabrutinib in achieving overall response and durable response compared to placebo, along with a favorable safety profile. These findings support further investigation of rilzabrutinib for ITP.

### Market implication
The positive Phase 2 results for rilzabrutinib could enhance [[sanofi]]'s pipeline value in hematology, potentially leading to further development and market opportunities in the ITP treatment landscape.

### Clinical evidence
- **Study design:** RCT
- **Sample size:** 123 patients
- **Comparator:** placebo
- **Primary outcome:** overall response (OR), defined as platelet count ≥50 × 10^9/L on ≥2 consecutive visits
- **Primary result:** OR was achieved by 40.0% of rilzabrutinib-treated patients (n = 40/100) versus 3.7% of placebo-treated patients (n = 1/27) (odds ratio [OR] 18.0; 95% confidence interval [CI] 2.3-142.0; p < .001).
- **Secondary results:**
    - Durable response (DR) was achieved by 29.0% of rilzabrutinib-treated patients (n = 29/100) versus 0% of placebo-treated patients (n = 0/27) (p < .001).
    - Median time to OR was 1.1 weeks (range 0.1-12.1) with rilzabrutinib.
    - Median time to DR was 1.1 weeks (range 0.1-12.1) with rilzabrutinib.
    - Rescue medication use was lower with rilzabrutinib (29.0%) versus placebo (59.3%).
    - Mean platelet counts increased from baseline with rilzabrutinib (from 19.9 × 10^9/L to 70.0 × 10^9/L at Week 24) versus placebo (from 19.8 × 10^9/L to 29.0 × 10^9/L at Week 24).
    - Bleeding events were reduced with rilzabrutinib (48.0%) versus placebo (66.7%).
- **Safety:** The most common treatment-emergent adverse events (TEAEs) with rilzabrutinib were headache (20.0%), nausea (19.0%), and diarrhea (18.0%). Serious TEAEs occurred in 12.0% of rilzabrutinib-treated patients and 14.8% of placebo-treated patients. No deaths were reported.
- **Conclusions:** Rilzabrutinib demonstrated superior efficacy versus placebo in achieving OR and DR, with a rapid onset of action, reduced rescue medication use, and a favorable safety profile in patients with ITP. These results support further investigation of rilzabrutinib in ITP.
- **Journal:** Am J Hematol., 2025
