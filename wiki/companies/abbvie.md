### Earnings intelligence
AbbVie reported full-year 2025 net revenues of $61.160 billion, an increase of 8.6 percent, with adjusted diluted EPS of $10.00, a decrease of 1.2 percent. The Immunology portfolio achieved $30.406 billion in net revenues, growing by 14.0%. Key immunology drugs include risankizumab (Skyrizi) with $17.562 billion in net revenues and upadacitinib (Rinvoq) with $8.304 billion, both showing bullish sentiment. Adalimumab (Humira) recorded $4.540 billion in net revenues. The Neuroscience portfolio grew by 19.6% to $10.767 billion, with cariprazine (Vraylar) generating $3.621 billion and onabotulinumtoxinA (Botox Therapeutic) $3.769 billion, both indicating bullish trends. Combined net revenues for ubrogepant and atogepant (Ubrelvy and Qulipta) were $2.307 billion, also reflecting bullish sentiment. The Oncology portfolio saw a slight increase of 1.5%, reaching $6.655 billion.

### Pipeline
- AGN-151597 (Phase I/II for Advanced Retinitis Pigmentosa): The Phase I/II trial (NCT02556736) for AGN-151597 in Advanced Retinitis Pigmentosa has completed, but efficacy was not demonstrated.
- venetoclax (Phase 2 for Chronic Lymphocytic Leukemia (CLL), Small Lymphocytic Lymphoma (SLL)): Study NCT02966756, a Phase 2, open-label, multicenter study evaluating venetoclax in participants with relapsed or refractory CLL/SLL, is Active, not recruiting. Estimated enrollment: 110 participants. Primary outcome: Overall Response Rate (ORR).
- venetoclax (Phase 3 for Acute Myeloid Leukemia (AML)): Study NCT03069352, a randomized controlled trial comparing venetoclax in combination with low dose cytarabine versus low dose cytarabine alone for treatment-naive acute myeloid leukemia patients, has completed.
- venetoclax (Phase 3 for Acute Myeloid Leukemia (AML)): Study NCT02993523, a randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled study comparing venetoclax + azacitidine vs. azacitidine + placebo, reached primary completion on December 1, 2021. The study enrolled 443 participants with treatment-naive AML who are ineligible for standard induction therapy.
- venetoclax (Phase 2 for Multiple Myeloma): Study NCT02899052 is a Phase 2, open-label, dose escalation trial evaluating venetoclax in combination with carfilzomib-dexamethasone (Kd) for participants with relapsed or refractory Multiple Myeloma (MM) who have received 1 to 3 prior lines of therapy. The study is currently 'ACTIVE_NOT_RECRUITING' with an estimated enrollment of 120 participants. Primary outcome measures include adverse events, Very Good Partial Response (VGPR) or Better Response Rate, Objective Response Rate (ORR), and Complete Response (CR) or Better Rate.
