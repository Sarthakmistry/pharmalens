#### Study: Mosunetuzumab plus polatuzumab vedotin in relapsed or refractory large B-cell lymphoma (NCT05171647)
**Study Design:** RCT
**Sample Size:** null
**Comparator:** Rituximab Plus Polatuzumab Vedotin
**Primary Outcome:** objective response rate (ORR)
**Primary Result:** The ORR was 70.0% (95% CI: 59.6-78.9) in the mosunetuzumab arm and 55.0% (95% CI: 44.2-65.4) in the rituximab arm.
**Secondary Results:**
- The complete response (CR) rate was 45.0% (95% CI: 34.7-55.7) in the mosunetuzumab arm and 30.0% (95% CI: 20.9-40.3) in the rituximab arm.
- Median progression-free survival (PFS) was 10.4 months (95% CI: 6.9-not estimable) in the mosunetuzumab arm and 6.2 months (95% CI: 4.1-10.4) in the rituximab arm (HR 0.68; 95% CI: 0.44-1.05).
- Median overall survival (OS) was not reached in either arm (HR 0.75; 95% CI: 0.43-1.30).
**Safety:** The most common grade ≥3 adverse events (AEs) were neutropenia (mosunetuzumab arm: 35.0%; rituximab arm: 30.0%), thrombocytopenia (mosunetuzumab arm: 15.0%; rituximab arm: 10.0%), and anemia (mosunetuzumab arm: 10.0%; rituximab arm: 5.0%). Cytokine release syndrome (CRS) occurred in 10.0% of patients in the mosunetuzumab arm (all grade 1 or 2).
**Conclusions:** These data suggest that mosunetuzumab plus polatuzumab vedotin may be a promising treatment option for patients with relapsed or refractory large B-cell lymphoma.
**Journal:** Am J Hematol.
**Industry Sponsored:** True

#### Study: Fenebrutinib in relapsing multiple sclerosis (NCT05119569)
**Study Design:** RCT
**Sample Size:** 109 patients
**Comparator:** placebo
**Primary Outcome:** the total number of new T1 Gd+ lesions on brain MRI at weeks 4, 8, and 12
**Primary Result:** The combined number of new T1 Gd+ lesions at weeks 4, 8, and 12 were 0·077 (95% CI 0·043-0·135) in the fenebrutinib group and 0·245 (0·144-0·418) in the placebo group (69% relative reduction [95% CI 34-85]; p=0·0022).
**Secondary Results:**
- During the open-label extension, through week 48, the unadjusted annualised relapse rate was 0·04
- 95 (96%) of 99 patients were relapse-free.
**Safety:** The most common adverse events that were more frequent in the fenebrutinib group than in the placebo group were hepatic enzyme elevations (four [6%] vs 0), headache (three [4%] vs one [3%]), and nasopharyngitis (two [3%] vs 0); no serious adverse events or deaths occurred.
**Conclusions:** Fenebrutinib was well tolerated and exerted an early, robust, and sustained effect of limiting new focal brain lesions. Further studies are needed to better characterise the safety and efficacy of fenebrutinib on both relapsing multiple sclerosis and non-relapsing progressive multiple sclerosis.
**Journal:** Lancet Neurol.
**Industry Sponsored:** True

#### Study: Crovalimab versus eculizumab in paroxysmal nocturnal hemoglobinuria (NCT04654468)
**Study Design:** meta-analysis
**Sample Size:** 504 patients
**Comparator:** eculizumab
**Primary Outcome:** tolerability of crovalimab versus eculizumab
**Primary Result:** Crovalimab's safety profile was consistent with eculizumab's, with 471 AEs per 100 PY for crovalimab and 581 AEs per 100 PY for eculizumab.
**Secondary Results:**
- Serious infection rates were 8.9 AEs per 100 PY for crovalimab and 13.7 AEs per 100 PY for eculizumab; no meningococcal infections were reported.
- Fatal AEs occurred in eight (2%) patients receiving crovalimab and one (1%) receiving eculizumab, all treatment unrelated.
- In C5i-switched patients, 39 (19%) had transient immune complex reactions (majority Grades 1-2 arthralgia and rash), and 16 (8%) had Grade 3 events.
**Safety:** Serious infection rates were 8.9 AEs per 100 PY for crovalimab and 13.7 AEs per 100 PY for eculizumab; no meningococcal infections were reported. Fatal AEs occurred in eight (2%) patients receiving crovalimab and one (1%) receiving eculizumab, all treatment unrelated. In C5i-switched patients, 39 (19%) had transient immune complex reactions, with 16 (8%) being Grade 3 events.
**Conclusions:** Crovalimab's safety profile was consistent with eculizumab's and was generally comparable between C5i-naive and C5i-switched patients.
**Journal:** Eur J Haematol
**Industry Sponsored:** True

#### Study: Obinutuzumab in lupus nephritis (NCT04221477)
**Study Design:** RCT
**Sample Size:** 271 patients
**Comparator:** placebo
**Primary Outcome:** complete renal response at week 76, defined by a urinary protein-to-creatinine ratio of less than 0.5 (with protein and creatinine both measured in milligrams), an estimated glomerular filtration rate of at least 85% of the baseline value, and no intercurrent event (i.e., rescue therapy, treatment failure, death, or early trial withdrawal)
**Primary Result:** A complete renal response at week 76 was observed in 46.4% of the patients in the obinutuzumab group and 33.1% of those in the placebo group (adjusted difference, 13.4 percentage points; 95% confidence interval [CI], 2.0 to 24.8; P = 0.02)
**Secondary Results:**
- A complete renal response at week 76 with a prednisone dose of 7.5 mg per day or lower between weeks 64 and 76 was observed in more patients in the obinutuzumab group than in the placebo group (42.7% vs. 30.9%; adjusted difference, 11.9 percentage points; 95% CI, 0.6 to 23.2; P = 0.04)
- A urinary protein-to-creatinine ratio lower than 0.8 without an intercurrent event was more common with obinutuzumab than with placebo (55.5% vs. 41.9%; adjusted difference, 13.7 percentage points; 95% CI, 2.0 to 25.4; P = 0.02)
**Safety:** No unexpected safety signals were identified. More serious adverse events, mainly infections and events related to coronavirus disease 2019, occurred with obinutuzumab than with placebo.
**Conclusions:** Among adults with active lupus nephritis, obinutuzumab plus standard therapy was more efficacious than standard therapy alone in providing a complete renal response.
**Journal:** N Engl J Med
**Industry Sponsored:** True

#### Study: Alectinib in resected early-stage ALK-positive NSCLC (NCT03456076)
**Study Design:** RCT
**Sample Size:** 256 patients
**Comparator:** platinum-based chemotherapy
**Primary Outcome:** investigator-assessed DFS

- DFS benefit with alectinib was maintained across surgical characteristic subgroups
- DFS benefit with alectinib was maintained when re-classifying patients according to the 8th edition of the Cancer Staging Manual of the American Joint Committee on Cancer (AJCC) and Union for International Cancer Control (UICC)

**Comparator:** null

**Conclusions:** Weight gain is under-reported as AE in trials. Actual weights showed ≥10% weight gain in 18% of patients. Clinicians should be aware of this AE, emphasizing the importance of timely identification and monitoring weight. Identifying predictors for weight gain remains challenging.

#### Study: atezolizumab plus Tiragolumab and chemotherapy in oesophageal cancer (NCT04540211)
**Study Design:** RCT
**Sample Size:** 152 patients
**Comparator:** atezolizumab and chemotherapy, chemotherapy alone
**Primary Outcome:** investigator-assessed confirmed objective response rate per Response Evaluation Criteria in Solid Tumours version 1.1 in patients who received at least one dose of each drug for their assigned treatment
**Primary Result:** Investigator-assessed confirmed objective response rate was 67.7% (95% CI 54.7-79.1; 42 of 62 patients) in the tiragolumab plus atezolizumab and chemotherapy group, 53.8% (41.0-66.3; 35 of 65) in the atezolizumab and chemotherapy group, and 47.8% (26.8-69.4; 11 of 23 patients) in the chemotherapy group.
**Secondary Results:**
- Median survival follow-up was 10.9 months (IQR 7.1-17.3) in the tiragolumab plus atezolizumab and chemotherapy group, 11.4 months (IQR 7.9-14.7) in the atezolizumab and chemotherapy group, and 8.7 months (3.9-12.0) in the chemotherapy group.
- 49 (79%) of 62, 52 (80%) of 65, and 17 (74%) of 23 patients had a grade 3-4 adverse event in each group respectively.
- 36 (58%) of 62, 33 (51%) of 65, and 11 (48%) of 23 had a serious adverse event.
- The most common treatment-related adverse events were nausea (46 [74%] of 62, 47 [72%] of 65, and 18 [78%] of 23) and decreased appetite (29 [47%], 29 [45%], and 10 [43%]).
**Safety:** 49 (79%) of 62, 52 (80%) of 65, and 17 (74%) of 23 patients had a grade 3-4 adverse event in each group respectively, and 36 (58%) of 62, 33 (51%) of 65, and 11 (48%) of 23 had a serious adverse event. No deaths were treatment related.
**Conclusions:** These results support the additional benefit of tiragolumab combined with atezolizumab and chemotherapy for patients with treatment-naive, locally advanced unresectable or metastatic oesophageal cancer. Further validation of tiragolumab plus atezolizumab and chemotherapy has been performed in the phase 3 SKYSCRAPER-08 trial (NCT04540211).

#### Study: PDS implant pre-filled with 100 mg/ml ranibizumab in diabetic retinopathy (NCT04503551)
**Study Design:** RCT
**Sample Size:** 174
**Comparator:** no PDS (control)
**Primary Outcome:** Proportion of participants with an improvement of at least 2 levels in Early Treatment Diabetic Retinopathy Study DRSS from baseline at week 52.
**Primary Result:** At week 52, 80.1% of those receiving PDS Q36W vs 9.0% of control participants had at least a 2-step DRSS improvement from baseline (difference, 71.1% [95% CI, 61.0% to 81.2%]; P < .001).
**Secondary Results:**
- rate of development of CI-DME, PDR, or ASNV through week 52 (PDS Q36W, 7.1%; control, 47.0%; hazard ratio, 0.12 [95% CI, 0.05 to 0.28]; P < .001)
- best-corrected visual acuity (BCVA) change from baseline to week 52 (+1.4 letters [95% CI, -0.5 to 3.3 letters] for those receiving PDS Q36W vs -2.6 letters [95% CI, -5.0 to -0.1 letters] for control participants; difference, 4.0 letters [95% CI, 0.9 to 7.1 letters]; P = .01)
**Safety:** The PDS Q36W group had a transient BCVA decrease of 7.4 letters (95% CI, -10.3 to -4.5 letters) at 4 weeks after implantation, resolving 8 weeks later. Ocular adverse events of special interest occurred in 17 of 105 participants (16.2%) receiving PDS Q36W (cataract, 7 participants [6.7%]; vitreous hemorrhage, 6 participants [5.7%]; conjunctival bleb, conjunctival retraction, and hyphema, each 2 participants [1.9%]; conjunctival erosion and retinal detachment, each 1 participant [1.0%]), with no endophthalmitis reported through week 52.
**Conclusions:** At 1 year, PDS Q36W resulted in substantially more participants achieving at least a 2-step DRSS improvement and a reduced risk of developing CI-DME, PDR, or ASNV compared with control participants, with safety outcomes consistent with previous reports. These findings should be balanced with the transient, postoperative decrease in BCVA 4 through 12 weeks after implantation and the need for longer-term BCVA and safety outcomes.
**Journal:** JAMA Ophthalmol.
**Industry Sponsored:** True
