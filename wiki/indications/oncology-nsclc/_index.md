### Clinical evidence
#### TP53 Mutations and Overall Survival in Advanced NSCLC
- **Study design**: Randomized Controlled Trial
- **Sample size**: 762 participants
- **Comparator**: chemotherapy
- **Primary outcome**: overall survival (OS)
- **Primary result**: TP53 mutations based on binary categorization were associated with poorer but not statistically significant OS compared to wild-type (adjusted-HR 1.15; 95%CI 0.96-1.38; P = .12).
- **Secondary results**:
    - Nonsense mutations classified by coding mutations (adjusted-HR 1.71; 95% CI 1.22-2.39; P = .002) were associated with statistically significant poorer OS.
    - Non-missense mutations classified by Olivier's classification (adjusted-HR 1.33; 95% CI 1.03-1.74; P = .03) were associated with statistically significant poorer OS.
    - Disruptive mutations classified by Poeta's classification (adjusted-HR 1.33; 95% CI 1.37-1.77; P = .03) were associated with statistically significant poorer OS.
    - TP53 status did not predict differential benefit from ICI versus chemotherapy (interaction P = .45).
- **Safety**: None
- **Conclusions**: In advanced driver-negative NSCLC following progression on first-line chemotherapy, nonsense, non-missense and disruptive mutations of TP53 were strongly associated with inferior OS. These data support utilizing a nuanced classification of TP53 mutations as a stratification factor in future trials, and laboratory reporting to aid prognostication.
- **Journal**: Lung cancer (Amsterdam, Netherlands), 2025
- **Industry sponsored**: None

#### Adjuvant Atezolizumab in Japanese NSCLC Patients (IMpower010 Subgroup)
- **Study design**: RCT
- **Sample size**: 117 patients
- **Comparator**: best supportive care (BSC)
- **Primary outcome**: disease-free survival (DFS)
- **Primary result**: unstratified hazard ratio (HR) (95% confidence interval [CI]) for DFS in the atezolizumab versus BSC arms was 0.57 (0.34-0.98) in the ITT populations.
- **Secondary results**:
    - OS remained immature; median OS was not reached in both treatment arms for all subgroups.
    - DFS HRs (95% CI) for atezolizumab vs BSC: Stage II-IIIA PD-L1 TC ≥1% was 0.54 (0.28-1.07).
    - DFS HRs (95% CI) for atezolizumab vs BSC: Stage II-IIIA PD-L1 TC 1%-49% was 0.64 (0.27-1.52).
    - DFS HRs (95% CI) for atezolizumab vs BSC: Stage II-IIIA PD-L1 TC ≥50% was 0.52 (0.17-1.54).
    - DFS HRs (95% CI) for atezolizumab vs BSC: Stage II-IIIA all-randomized was 0.58 (0.34-1.00).
- **Safety**: Grade 3/4 adverse events occurred in 15 (26.8%) and 7 patients (12.1%) in the atezolizumab and BSC arms, respectively; no deaths were reported.
- **Conclusions**: In this exploratory subgroup analysis, adjuvant atezolizumab demonstrated numerically improved DFS and OS versus BSC in Japanese patients and was well tolerated, similar to the global IMpower010 population.
- **Journal**: Cancer science, 2026
- **Industry sponsored**: None

#### Immune Landscape and ICI Response in EGFR-Mutated NSCLC (NCT03991403)
- **Study design**: RCT
- **sample size**: 143 patients
- **Comparator**: pemetrexed plus carboplatin
- **Primary outcome**: overall response rate (ORR) and progression-free survival (PFS)
- **Primary result**: Higher TILs in CA were associated with a better overall response rate (ORR) and progression-free survival (PFS). Similarly, higher EC levels in CA correlated with improved ORR and PFS.
- **Secondary results**:
    - Post-TKI samples showed reduced TILs in the CA (p=0.045) and increased ECs in the CA (p=0.005) compared with pre-TKI samples.
    - These changes differed according to EGFR mutation subtype.
    - In the ATTLAS cohort, these factors were associated with clinical benefits from ABCP, with a significant association with TILs and a marginal association with ECs.
- **Safety**: None
- **Conclusions**: Our findings suggest that EGFR-TKIs affect the immune landscape of patients with EGFR-mutated NSCLC. Higher TILs or ECs in the CA were significantly associated with a favorable response to subsequent ICI-based treatment.
- **Journal**: Journal for immunotherapy of cancer, 2025
- **Industry sponsored**: False

#### Olaparib + TRT Phase I study in ES-SCLC
- **Study design**: single-arm
- **Sample size**: 24 patients
- **Comparator**: None
- **Primary outcome**: safety and maximum tolerated dose (MTD) of olaparib + TRT
- **Primary result**: The MTD of olaparib with TRT was 200 mg twice daily.
- **Secondary results**:
    - The 12-month cumulative incidence of local recurrence was 27%.
    - The median progression-free survival was 3.6 months.
    - The median overall survival was 17.7 months.
- **Safety**: There were three grade 3 (G3) dose-limiting adverse events (AE), including pneumonitis/pneumonia, esophagitis, and abdominal pain. The most common G2 to G3 treatment-related AE were esophagitis (n = 12) and pneumonitis/pneumonia (n = 2). There were no G4 or G5 AE.
- **Conclusions**: This study established the MTD and recommended a phase II dose of olaparib at 200 mg twice daily with concurrent low-dose TRT, and the combination seemed safe without unexpected toxicities.
- **Journal**: Clinical cancer research : an official journal of the American Association for Cancer Research, 2025
- **Industry sponsored**: False

#### Atezolizumab IPSOS study (NCT03191786)
- **Study design**: RCT
- **Sample size**: 453 patients
- **Comparator**: single-agent chemotherapy (vinorelbine or gemcitabine)
- **Primary outcome**: overall survival
- **Primary result**: overall survival (unstratified hazard ratio 0.78; 95% confidence interval: 0.63-0.98)
- **Secondary results**:
    - fewer grade 3 or 4 treatment-related adverse events (46 of 266 [17.3%] versus 46 of 134 [34.3%] with chemotherapy)
    - fewer grade 5 treatment-related adverse events (two of 266 [0.8%] versus four of 134 [3.0%])
    - stabilization or improvement of patient-reported health-related quality of life
- **Safety**: Atezolizumab was associated with fewer grade 3 or 4 treatment-related adverse events (46 of 266 [17.3%] versus 46 of 134 [34.3%] with chemotherapy) and grade 5 treatment-related adverse events (two of 266 [0.8%] versus four of 134 [3.0%]).
- **Conclusions**: This subanalysis suggests that first-line treatment with atezolizumab provides long-term overall survival, consistent with the results from the intention-to-treat population, and a favorable safety profile compared with single-agent chemotherapy in the sPI population. In addition, the selection criteria for platinum ineligibility from the sPI population may provide a structured approach for treatment selection in NSCLC in clinical practice.
- **Journal**: Journal of thoracic oncology : official publication of the International Association for the Study of Lung Cancer, 2025
- **Industry sponsored**: True

#### Durvalumab in Unresectable Stage III EGFR Mutant NSCLC
- **Study design**: meta-analysis
- **Sample size**: 1156 patients
- **Comparator**: CRT | TKI monotherapy | CRT-Durva | TKI-free regimens
- **Primary outcome**: progression-free survival (PFS) and overall survival (OS)
- **Primary result**: The PFS of patients treated with TKI-containing regimens was significantly longer than that of patients treated with TKI-free regimens (hazard ratio [HR] = 0.37, 95% confidence interval [CI], 0.20-0.66). RT-TKI ranked first in the Bayesian ranking, with the longest OS (60.8 months, 95% CI = 37.2-84.3 months) and the longest PFS (21.5 months, 95% CI, 15.4-27.5 months) in integrated analysis.
- **Secondary results**:
    - The PFS of TKI monotherapy was significantly longer than that of CRT (HR = 0.66, 95% CI, 0.50-0.87) but shorter than RT-TKI (HR = 1.78, 95% CI, 1.17-2.67).
    - Furthermore, the PFS of RT-TKI or CRT-TKI were both significantly longer than that of CRT or CRT-Durva.
- **Safety**: None
- **Conclusions**: For unresectable stage III EGFR mutant NSCLC, RT and TKI are both essential. Based on the current evidence, RT-TKI brings a superior survival advantage, while CRT-TKI needs further estimation. Large randomized clinical trials are urgently needed to explore the appropriate application sequences of TKI, radiotherapy, and chemotherapy.
- **Journal**: Chinese medical journal, 2025
- **Industry sponsored**: None

#### Durvalumab, Carboplatin, and Paclitaxel in Resectable Stage III N2 NSCLC (WJOG 12119L)

#### IPH5201 with or without durvalumab in advanced solid tumors
- **Study design**: clinical trial, phase 1
- **Sample size**: 57 patients
- **Comparator**: None
- **Primary outcome**: to evaluate safety and tolerability
- **Primary result**: The most common treatment-related adverse events were infusion-related reactions and fatigue. There were no adverse event-related deaths, and the maximum tolerated dose was not reached.
- **Secondary results**:
    - Overall, 23/57 patients (40.4%) had stable disease as their best overall response.
    - IPH5201 exhibited a linear pharmacokinetic profile and an estimated terminal half-life of 8 to 9 days at higher doses.
    - On-treatment tumor biopsies revealed decreased CD39 ATPase activity in 5/7 patients, including all evaluable patients receiving the maximum dose of IPH5201 3,000 mg every 3 weeks.
- **Safety**: The most common treatment-related adverse events were infusion-related reactions and fatigue. There were no adverse event-related deaths, and the maximum tolerated dose was not reached.
- **Conclusions**: IPH5201 as monotherapy, or in combination with durvalumab, was well tolerated at pharmacologically active doses that induced reduction of intratumoral CD39 enzymatic activity and showed preliminary evidence for disease stabilization.
- **Journal**: Cancer research communications, 2025
- **Industry sponsored**: None

#### Durvalumab in Oligometastatic Non-small Cell Lung Cancer
- **Study design**: None
- **Sample size**: 35 patients
- **Comparator**: None
- **Primary outcome**: investigator-assessed progression-free survival (PFS) according to Response Evaluation Criteria in Solid Tumors version 1.1
- **Primary result**: In the full analysis set (n = 35), the median PFS (mPFS) was 10.4 months (95% CI, 4.4-26.1)
- **Secondary results**:
    - The 2-year PFS rate and overall survival (OS) rate were 38.5% (95% CI, 21.9-54.8) and 67.7% (95% CI, 49.0-80.7), respectively (full analysis set).
    - In the per-protocol set (n = 32), mPFS was 14.6 months (95% CI, 4.7-26.1), and the 2-year PFS rate and OS rate were 42.4% (95% CI, 24.3-59.4) and 70.8% (95% CI, 51.2-83.8), respectively.
    - The subgroup analysis showed that the mPFS was 24.3 months (95% CI, 7.6-NE) with SABR versus 3.1 months (95% CI, 1.4-4.7) without SABR (hazard ratio [HR], 0.2; 95% CI, 0.09-0.5; P < .001).
- **Safety**: Grade 3 or higher treatment-related adverse events (TRAEs) and immune-mediated adverse events (imAEs) were reported in 57.1% (20/35) and 25.7% (9/35) of patients, respectively.
- **Conclusions**: In oligometastatic NSCLC, the combination of durvalumab, chemotherapy, and SABR was effective and tolerable. Patients who did not experience disease progression after prior systemic therapy and received SABR might have optimal outcomes.
- **Journal**: International journal of radiation oncology, biology, physics, 2025
- **Industry sponsored**: None

#### Durvalumab and Atezolizumab in Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (APOLO trial, NCT04776447)
- **Study design**: clinical trial
- **Sample size**: 38 patients
- **Comparator**: standard of care
- **Primary outcome**: 12-month progression-free survival (PFS)
- **Primary result**: PFS was 68.4% (95% CI, 51.1-80.7) at 12 months (statistically significant compared to null hypothesis of 55%)
- **Secondary results**:
    - 24-month PFS was 50.0%
    - OS was 86.8% at 12 months and 60.5% at 24 months
    - After induction, 18 (47.4%) had partial response, 14 (36.8%) stable disease, and 5 (13.2%) progressive disease
    - Overall, 18 patients had disease progression: 8 local (44.4%) and 10 distant (55.6%)
- **Safety**: Grade ≥3 TRAEs occurred in 11 (28.9%) during induction, 10 (26.3%) during CT-RT, and 2 (5.3%) during maintenance.
- **Conclusions**: ChIO induction before CT-RT and IO maintenance showed activity and safety, warranting confirmation in larger studies.
- **Journal**: Nature communications, 2025
- **Industry sponsored**: None

#### Neoadjuvant Nivolumab Plus Ipilimumab vs Chemotherapy in Resectable NSCLC
- **Study design**: RCT
- **Sample size**: 221 patients
- **Comparator**: chemotherapy
- **Primary outcome**: event-free survival (EFS)
- **Primary result**: At a median follow-up of 49.2 months, the median EFS was 54.8 months (95% CI, 24.4 to not reached [NR]) with nivolumab plus ipilimumab versus 20.9 months (95% CI, 14.2 to NR) with chemotherapy (HR, 0.77 [95% CI, 0.51 to 1.15])
- **Secondary results**:
    - 3-year EFS rates were 56% versus 44%.
    - 3-year OS rates were 73% versus 61% (HR, 0.73 [95% CI, 0.47 to 1.14])
    - pathologic complete response rates were 20.4% versus 4.6%, respectively.
- **Safety**: Grade 3-4 treatment-related adverse events occurred in 14% and 36% of patients, respectively.
- **Conclusions**: Neoadjuvant nivolumab plus ipilimumab showed potential long-term clinical benefit versus chemotherapy, despite early crossing of EFS curves in the preoperative phase and a lower rate of high-grade toxicity.
- **Journal**: Journal of clinical oncology : official journal of the American Society of Clinical Oncology, 2025
- **Industry sponsored**: None

Two participants had AEs leading to discontinuation: grade 3 pneumonia (n = 1; not treatment-related) and grade 2 organizing pneumonia (n = 1; treatment-related).

#### Gut Microbiota and Chemoimmunotherapy in Advanced NSCLC (JCOG2007)
- **Study design**: RCT
- **Sample size**: 270 patients
- **Comparator**: pembrolizumab plus platinum doublet chemotherapy (PC) with nivolumab and ipilimumab plus platinum doublet chemotherapy (NIC)
- **Primary outcome**: Association of gut microbiota with overall survival and adverse events
- **Primary result**: The beta diversity analysis of the overall cohort (n = 270) revealed distinct microbial structures between the subpopulations defined by whether overall survival (OS) exceeds 12 or 18 months.
- **Secondary results**:
    - Fusicatenibacter, Butyricicoccus, and Blautia were enriched in patients with longer OS.
    - Lower microbial alpha diversity and the presence of certain taxa were linked to a higher risk of serious (≥grade 4) AEs.
    - Favorable genera, including Fusicatenibacter and Butyricicoccus, were associated with a lower risk of serious AEs.
    - Higher abundance of Fusicatenibacter and Butyricicoccus was linked to better OS in the NIC arm compared with that in the CP arm (hazard ratio for OS = 0.56 and 0.52, respectively).
    - Higher abundance of Prevotellaceae NK3B31 was associated with higher mortality risk in the NIC arm (hazard ratio for OS = 2.33).
- **Safety**: Lower microbial alpha diversity and the presence of certain taxa were linked to a higher risk of serious (≥grade 4) AEs. Favorable genera, including Fusicatenibacter and Butyricicoccus, were associated with a lower risk of serious AEs.
- **Conclusions**: Gut microbiota may serve as a biomarker for chemoimmunotherapy in advanced NSCLC. Differences in microbial diversity and specific bacterial genera were associated with prognosis and serious AEs, with potential regimen-specific effects. #### ADVISE: Biomarker-Adapted I-O Combination Treatment for PD-1-Refractory Cancer (NCT03335540)
- **Study design**: Adaptive Clinical Trial
- **Sample size**: 23 patients
- **Comparator**: None
- **Primary outcome**: the proportion of patients with qualified baseline tumor biopsy specimens where decision-enabling biomarker analysis was completed within 12 business days to select an I-O combination therapy
- **Primary result**: 20/23 (87%) of eligible patients were successfully allocated and started on treatment within the 12-day window, meeting the primary endpoint.
- **Secondary results**:

#### Nivolumab plus Ipilimumab vs Chemotherapy in Metastatic NSCLC
- **Study design**: Randomized Controlled Trial
- **Sample size**: None
- **Comparator**: chemotherapy
- **Primary outcome**: restricted-mean survival time (between Kaplan-Meier curves for time to treatment discontinuation and time to subsequent systemic therapy or death) over 6 years after randomization
- **Primary result**: The 6-year mean TFS was 12.2 versus 5.0 months (difference 7.2 [95% confidence interval: 5.4-9.2])
- **Secondary results**:
    - At 6 years after randomization, the estimated OS rate was 20% with nivolumab plus ipilimumab versus 11% with chemotherapy.
    - 13% versus 2% of patients were treatment free with nivolumab plus ipilimumab versus chemotherapy.
    - The 6-year mean TFS without grade 3 or greater treatment-related adverse events was 11.6 versus 4.8 months (difference, 6.9 [95% confidence interval: 5.1-8.9]).
- **Safety**: The 6-year mean TFS without grade 3 or greater treatment-related adverse events was 11.6 versus 4.8 months, indicating that a significant portion of TFS was free from severe toxicity.
- **Conclusions**: Nivolumab plus ipilimumab improved TFS versus chemotherapy, regardless of tumor PD-L1 expression, supporting its use as an efficacious first-line treatment for metastatic NSCLC.
- **Journal**: Journal of thoracic oncology : official publication of the International Association for the Study of Lung Cancer, 2025
- **Industry sponsored**: None
