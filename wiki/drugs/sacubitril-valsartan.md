### Clinical evidence
A 2026 systematic review and meta-analysis found that Angiotensin Receptor-Neprilysin Inhibitor (ARNI), including sacubitril-valsartan, may be effective in preventing the recurrence of atrial fibrillation (AF) after ablation. Constant usage of ARNI for 3, 6, and 12 months partly reduced AF recurrence after ablation in RCTs (e.g., 3-month: RR=0.56, p=0.006), and ARNI was more effective than ACEI/ARB or Blank in reducing recurrence rates.
An observational study published in 2025, involving 4794 patients with Heart Failure with Preserved Ejection Fraction (HFpEF) from the PARAGON-HF trial, identified that 23.3% had at least one abnormal nutritional score at randomization, with 38.2% developing one over a median 2.9 years. Any abnormal nutritional score during follow-up was significantly associated with a higher risk of fatal and nonfatal outcomes, and all types of hospitalizations. Furthermore, any hospitalization during follow-up was linked to a significantly higher risk of developing at least one abnormal nutritional score post-hospitalization (adjusted hazard ratio, 1.37).
The 2025 EVALUATE-HF trial, a randomized controlled trial with 406 patients with heart failure with reduced ejection fraction (HFrEF), demonstrated that sacubitril-valsartan improved the KCCQ-12 overall summary score by +9 points over 24 weeks. This improvement correlated with increases in mitral e' velocities and decreases in septal E/e' ratio and left atrial volume index, suggesting that reductions in left ventricular filling pressures and left atrial size are closely coupled with improved physical function and quality of life in HFrEF patients.
