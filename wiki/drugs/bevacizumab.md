### Management sentiment
This network meta-analysis demonstrates that antiangiogenic combinations improve outcomes in driver-negative advanced NSCLC, leading to a bullish sentiment for [[bevacizumab]]. The latest systematic review and meta-analysis further supports a bullish sentiment by highlighting the efficacy of [[nivolumab]] + [[bevacizumab]] + chemotherapy and [[atezolizumab]] + [[bevacizumab]] + chemotherapy for PFS in advanced nsNSCLC. The identification of molecular clusters that predict response to therapy for tumors that metastasize to the pancreas offers insights for treatment strategies, contributing to a bullish outlook. ICI-based combinations with anti-VEGF/anti-angiogenic agents, including [[bevacizumab]], generally outperform sorafenib in advanced HCC, with discernible etiology-specific optima. Furthermore, [[bevacizumab]]'s role in maintenance regimens, such as with [[durvalumab]] and [[olaparib]] in advanced ovarian cancer, contributes to a positive outlook. While [[pembrolizumab]] + lenvatinib has shown superior PFS and ORR compared to combinations including [[atezolizumab]] + [[bevacizumab]] in first-line aRCC, [[bevacizumab]] continues to demonstrate efficacy in various combination therapies across multiple oncology indications.

### Clinical evidence
A 2026 meta-analysis published in Frontiers in immunology, involving 13,572 patients, investigated ICI-based combinations with anti-VEGF/anti-angiogenic agents for advanced HCC. The primary result was that regimens with significant OS advantage over sorafenib included sintilimab plus bevacizumab biosimilar (HR = 0.57), camrelizumab plus rivoceranib (HR = 0.62), and [[atezolizumab]] plus [[bevacizumab]] (HR = 0.66). Etiology-stratified analyses indicated different optimal regimens, with HBV-related HCC favoring sintilimab plus bevacizumab biosimilar or [[atezolizumab]] plus [[bevacizumab]] for OS, and HCV-related HCC favoring [[atezolizumab]] plus [[bevacizumab]] for OS. The study concluded that ICI-based combinations with anti-VEGF/anti-angiogenic agents generally outperform sorafenib, with discernible etiology-specific optima.
A 2026 study published in Clinical cancer research : an official journal of the American Association for Cancer Research, investigating the pharmacodynamics of durvalumab-based regimens in unresectable hepatocellular carcinoma (uHCC), found that objective response rates were 24.0% with STRIDE, 21.3% with [[durvalumab]] plus [[bevacizumab]] (D+B), and 11.5% with [[durvalumab]] monotherapy. While STRIDE increased expanded T-cell clones associated with clinical response, D+B upregulated IFNγ response gene expression signatures (GES) and IFNα response, myeloid cell, and endothelial GES. The study concluded that STRIDE and D+B have distinct, and potentially complementary, mechanisms of action in uHCC.
A 2026 RCT published in Annals of oncology : official journal of the European Society for Medical Oncology, involving 1130 patients, investigated [[durvalumab]] plus carboplatin/paclitaxel and [[bevacizumab]] followed by [[durvalumab]], [[bevacizumab]] plus [[olaparib]] maintenance versus carboplatin/paclitaxel and [[bevacizumab]] followed by [[bevacizumab]] in advanced ovarian cancer. The primary outcome was investigator-assessed progression-free survival (PFS). The primary result was that the PFS hazard ratio (HR) for the [[durvalumab]] + [[olaparib]] arm versus control was 0.49 (95% CI 0.34-0.69, P < 0.0001; median PFS 37.3 versus 23.0 months) in the non-tBRCAm HRD-positive population and 0.63 (95% CI 0.52-0.76, P < 0.0001; median PFS 24.2 versus 19.3 months) in the non-tBRCAm ITT population. The study concluded that DUO-O met its primary PFS endpoints for the [[durvalumab]] + [[olaparib]] + [[bevacizumab]] regimen in both non-tBRCAm HRD-positive and ITT populations.
A 2026 systematic review and meta-analysis published in International journal of gynaecology and obstetrics: the official organ of the International Federation of Gynaecology and Obstetrics, involving 3302 patients, compared [[pembrolizumab]] with standard therapy, placebo, or other immune checkpoint inhibitors for overall survival (OS) and progression-free survival (PFS). The primary result was that [[pembrolizumab]] ranked highest in OS and PFS among patients with locally advanced or advanced cervical cancer. In the advanced disease setting, [[pembrolizumab]] plus chemotherapy improved OS and PFS overall compared to other ICIs, especially in patients receiving [[bevacizumab]]. The study concluded that [[pembrolizumab]] ranked highest in OS and PFS among patients with locally advanced or advanced cervical cancer, supporting its preferential use over other ICIs.
A 2025 meta-analysis published in Targeted oncology compared [[pembrolizumab]] + lenvatinib with other immune checkpoint inhibitors (ICI) combination therapies, including [[nivolumab]] + ipilimumab, avelumab + axitinib, [[atezolizumab]] + [[bevacizumab]], [[pembrolizumab]] + axitinib, bempegaldesleukin + [[nivolumab]], and [[nivolumab]], in first-line advanced renal cell carcinoma. The primary outcomes were overall survival (OS), progression-free survival (PFS), and objective response rate (ORR). [[pembrolizumab]] + lenvatinib demonstrated statistically significant improvement in PFS compared with [[nivolumab]] + ipilimumab (HR = 0.53; 95% credible interval (CrI): 0.40-0.71), avelumab + axitinib (HR = 0.71; 95% Crl: 0.53-0.94), [[atezolizumab]] + [[bevacizumab]] (HR = 0.54; 95% CrI: 0.40-0.73), and [[pembrolizumab]] + axitinib (HR = 0.69; 95% CrI: 0.51-0.91). A statistically significant higher ORR was shown for [[pembrolizumab]] + lenvatinib compared with [[nivolumab]] + ipilimumab (OR = 3.29; 95% Crl: 2.21-4.93), [[pembrolizumab]] + axitinib (OR = 1.92; 95% CrI: 1.27-2.94), [[atezolizumab]] + [[bevacizumab]] (OR = 4.05; 95% Crl: 2.71-6.05), bempegaldesleukin + [[nivolumab]] (OR = 6.20; 95% CrI: 3.69-10.48), and [[nivolumab]] (OR = 5.92; 95% CrI: 2.70-13.24). No statistically significant difference was observed for OS. The overall population analysis indicated that [[pembrolizumab]] + lenvatinib improves PFS and ORR compared with other approved ICI combination therapies in first-line aRCC. No significant differences in OS were observed between [[pembrolizumab]] + lenvatinib and other combination immune checkpoint inhibitor-based therapies.
A 2024 RCT published in Annals of oncology : official journal of the European Society for Medical Oncology, involving 400 patients, investigated a regimen (ABC, including [[atezolizumab]], [[bevacizumab]], Carboplatin, and pemetrexed) against a comparator of [[bevacizumab]] and carboplatin-pemetrexed chemotherapy (BC) for advanced pleural mesothelioma. The primary outcome was OS in all randomised patients. The primary result was that the median OS was 20.5 months for ABC versus 18.1 months for BC [HR 0.84, 95% confidence interval (CI) 0.66-1.06, P = 0.14]. Median PFS was significantly longer for ABC than for BC (9.2 versus 7.6 months) (HR 0.72, 95% CI 0.59-0.89, P = 0.0021). Histology showed significant treatment interaction for both PFS and OS, with an OS HR of 0.51 (95% CI 0.32-0.80) for non-epithelioid and 1.01 (95% CI 0.77-1.32) for epithelioid (interaction P = 0.012). Grade ≥3 treatment-related AEs were reported in 55% of patients in ABC and 47% in BC. QoL was maintained with ABC with no clinically meaningful differences from BC. The study concluded: The significant benefit in median PFS for ABC found in this study translated into a numerical but not significant increase in median OS. Thus, the primary endpoint was not met. In the pre-specified analysis by histology, superior OS and PFS were found for ABC in non-epithelioid cases.
A 2025 phase II trial (BOLD) published in CPT: pharmacometrics & systems pharmacology, involving 74 patients, investigated a triplet regimen ([[bevacizumab]], [[olaparib]], [[durvalumab]]) in recurrent platinum-sensitive/resistant advanced ovarian cancer. The primary outcome was the prognostic value of KELIM-B (CA-125 kinetics alone) and KELIM-joint (integrating CA-125 kinetics and tumor size) for progression-free survival (PFS) and overall survival (OS). The primary result was that the prognostic value of the categorical KELIM-joint (PFS, HR = 0.29, 95% CI [0.12-0.72]; OS, HR = 0.24, 95% CI [0.08-0.74]) was not clinically different from that of KELIM-B (PFS, HR = 0.35, 95% CI [0.14-0.84]; OS, HR = 0.34, 95% CI [0.12-0.99]). The study concluded that the improvement in prognostic value was not sufficient to justify the higher complexity of the joint model, and assessing early longitudinal CA-125 kinetics alone remains the best pragmatic strategy for future development.

The study concluded: The development of irAEs is associated with favorable clinical outcomes in HCC, including improved progression-free survival and higher objective response rate. Overall survival benefit was noted in patients with mild irAEs but not those with severe irAEs.

### Competitive position
Bevacizumab, a monoclonal antibody targeting VEGF, holds a significant position in oncology, particularly in colorectal, non-small cell lung, and breast cancers. It competes with other anti-angiogenic agents and a growing number of immune checkpoint inhibitors (ICIs) and their combinations. For instance, in advanced renal cell carcinoma (aRCC), [[pembrolizumab]] + lenvatinib has shown superior PFS and ORR compared to combinations including [[atezolizumab]] + [[bevacizumab]], indicating a strong competitive challenge in this specific indication. In NSCLC, while bevacizumab combinations with chemotherapy and ICIs like [[atezolizumab]] show promise, other regimens like sintilimab + anlotinib or prolgolimab + chemotherapy also demonstrate strong efficacy. The drug's differentiation often lies in its established safety profile and its role as a backbone therapy in various combination regimens, particularly in viral-HCC where ICI + VEGF/VEGFRi combinations show benefit. However, cost-effectiveness remains a challenge in some indications, as seen in advanced HCC where [[atezolizumab]] + [[bevacizumab]] was not considered cost-effective compared to [[atezolizumab]] + cabozantinib. The emergence of novel agents and combinations, such as FAP-IL2v with [[atezolizumab]] and [[bevacizumab]] in renal cell carcinoma, continues to evolve the competitive landscape.
