### Clinical evidence
A Japan subset analysis of the DUO-E trial (NCT04269200), an RCT with 88 patients, evaluated durvalumab-based regimens for advanced or recurrent endometrial cancer. Progression-free survival (PFS) favored durvalumab + olaparib (HR=0.44, 95% CI=0.22-0.85) compared to control, with a median PFS of 15.1 months versus 9.5 months. The safety profile was consistent with established profiles. This study was industry sponsored.
A phase II clinical trial with 29 patients evaluated the combination of neoadjuvant durvalumab and olaparib in resectable urothelial bladder cancer, observing a pathologic complete response in 13 cases (44.8%) with no major safety concerns.
The BOLD phase II trial (NCT04015739) investigated a triplet regimen of bevacizumab, olaparib, and durvalumab in 74 patients with recurrent platinum-sensitive/resistant advanced ovarian cancer. The study found that the prognostic value of a joint model integrating CA-125 kinetics and tumor size was not clinically different from CA-125 kinetics alone for predicting PFS and OS, suggesting CA-125 kinetics alone remains the best pragmatic strategy.
A randomized four-arm superiority phase II trial (NRG-GY023) with 153 patients with high-grade serous/endometrioid or clear-cell platinum-resistant ovarian cancer with prior bevacizumab exposure compared standard-of-care chemotherapy (SOC) to durvalumab + olaparib + cediranib (DOC), durvalumab + cediranib (DC), or olaparib + cediranib (OC). The trial was closed due to futility, as all experimental arms failed to reach the primary objective of improving progression-free survival (PFS) compared with SOC, with median PFS of 3.4 months for SOC, 2.9 months for DOC, 2.5 months for DC, and 2.8 months for OC. No new safety signals were observed.
An RCT with 302 patients compared olaparib monotherapy to standard of care (capecitabine, vinorelbine, or eribulin) for metastatic breast cancer in gBRCA1/2 mutation patients. The primary outcome was Progression-free Survival (PFS) Using Blinded Independent Central Review (BICR) According to Modified Response Evaluation Criteria In Solid Tumours (RECIST v1.1). Primary result: None. Conclusions: None. This study was industry sponsored. An industry-sponsored study (NCT01078662) enrolled 298 patients to assess Tumour Response Rate. No primary result or conclusions were reported. An RCT with 162 patients compared olaparib in combination with carboplatin and paclitaxel versus carboplatin and paclitaxel for Progression Free Survival (PFS). Primary result: None. Conclusions: None. This study was industry sponsored.
