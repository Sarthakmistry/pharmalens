### Clinical evidence
The phase 3 DUO-E trial's exploratory analyses focused on the mismatch repair proficient (pMMR) subpopulation in advanced/recurrent endometrial cancer. Durvalumab plus olaparib consistently improved progression-free survival (PFS) benefit over durvalumab alone across a range of biomarker and histological subgroups in pMMR patients, with PFS hazard ratios improved versus control. The pMMR subpopulation was highly heterogeneous, with 84% positive for at least one biomarker. This study was industry sponsored.
The DUO-O trial, an RCT with 1130 patients, investigated durvalumab plus carboplatin/paclitaxel and bevacizumab followed by durvalumab, bevacizumab plus olaparib maintenance versus control (carboplatin/paclitaxel plus bevacizumab followed by bevacizumab) for advanced ovarian cancer. The trial met its primary PFS endpoints, with the durvalumab + olaparib arm showing a PFS HR of 0.49 (95% CI 0.34-0.69, P < 0.0001; median PFS 37.3 vs 23.0 months) in the non-tBRCAm HRD-positive population, and 0.63 (95% CI 0.52-0.76, P < 0.0001; median PFS 24.2 vs 19.3 months) in the non-tBRCAm ITT population. Safety was consistent with individual agents.
A study with 61 patients evaluated olaparib plus durvalumab in HRR-unselected metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer (mCRPC). The median radiographic progression-free survival (rPFS) was 5.0 months, and median overall survival (OS) was 19.1 months. Clinical benefit was enriched in BRCA2-variant disease, with patients having BRCA2 variants experiencing longer rPFS (13.2 months) compared to those without (4.8 months, p=0.0026).
A single-arm Phase II study (NCT02484404) with 15 patients (14 evaluable) investigated durvalumab and olaparib in heavily pretreated metastatic triple-negative breast cancer (TNBC). The overall response rate (ORR) was 28.6% (3 gBRCAm and 1 gBRCAwt), with a median progression-free survival (PFS) of 3.6 months and overall survival (OS) of 10.7 months. No new safety concerns were identified, but the study noted modest clinical benefits in subsets of heavily pretreated TNBC. This study was not industry sponsored.
The SOLACE2 trial, a randomized controlled trial with 114 patients, investigated olaparib, or cyclophosphamide-olaparib priming, followed by durvalumab-olaparib, compared to olaparib monotherapy in platinum-sensitive recurrent ovarian cancer. While priming regimens showed longer PFS than monotherapy, the trial did not reach its pre-specified primary endpoint (PFS36 threshold). PFS36 rates were 47.4% (olaparib priming), 48.7% (olaparib-cyclophosphamide priming), and 35.1% (olaparib monotherapy). A prognostic blood signature (CUP-CC) was identified, with the CUP-CC+ subgroup having significantly longer PFS (HR 0.31, 0.19-0.49). This study was not industry sponsored.
The DOLAF trial, a Phase II clinical trial with 172 patients, evaluated durvalumab, olaparib, and fulvestrant in ER+/HER2- metastatic breast cancer patients with selected genomic alterations. The triplet combination was active and had an acceptable toxicity profile, with a 24-week progression-free survival rate of 66.7% (95% CI, 58.6-74.1) in the evaluable population and a median overall survival of 30 months. Median PFS was 9.3 months (ITT) and 12.6 months (gBRCA1/2m populations). Common adverse events were nausea (59%) and asthenia (43%), with no new toxicity warnings.
A Japan subset analysis of the DUO-E trial (NCT04269200), an RCT with 88 patients, evaluated durvalumab-based regimens for advanced or recurrent endometrial cancer. Progression-free survival (PFS) favored durvalumab + olaparib (HR=0.44, 95% CI=0.22-0.85) compared to control, with a median PFS of 15.1 months versus 9.5 months. The safety profile was consistent with established profiles. This study was industry sponsored.
A phase II clinical trial with 29 patients evaluated the combination of neoadjuvant durvalumab and olaparib in resectable urothelial bladder cancer, observing a pathologic complete response in 13 cases (44.8%) with no major safety concerns.
The BOLD phase II trial (NCT04015739) investigated a triplet regimen of bevacizumab, olaparib, and durvalumab in 74 patients with recurrent platinum-sensitive/resistant advanced ovarian cancer. The study found that the prognostic value of a joint model integrating CA-125 kinetics and tumor size was not clinically different from CA-125 kinetics alone for predicting PFS and OS, suggesting CA-125 kinetics alone remains the best pragmatic strategy.
A randomized four-arm superiority phase II trial (NRG-GY023) with 153 patients with high-grade serous/endometrioid or clear-cell platinum-resistant ovarian cancer with prior bevacizumab exposure compared standard-of-care chemotherapy (SOC) to durvalumab + olaparib + cediranib (DOC), durvalumab + cediranib (DC), or olaparib + cediranib (OC). The trial was closed due to futility, as all experimental arms failed to reach the primary objective of improving progression-free survival (PFS) compared with SOC, with median PFS of 3.4 months for SOC, 2.9 months for DOC, 2.5 months for DC, and 2.8 months for OC. No new safety signals were observed.
An RCT with 302 patients compared olaparib monotherapy to standard of care (capecitabine, vinorelbine, or eribulin) for metastatic breast cancer in gBRCA1/2 mutation patients. The primary outcome was Progression-free Survival (PFS) Using Blinded Independent Central Review (BICR) According to Modified Response Evaluation Criteria In Solid Tumours (RECIST v1.1). Primary result: None. Conclusions: None. This study was industry sponsored. An industry-sponsored study (NCT01078662) enrolled 298 patients to assess Tumour Response Rate. No primary result or conclusions were reported. An RCT with 162 patients compared olaparib in combination with carboplatin and paclitaxel versus carboplatin and paclitaxel for Progression Free Survival (PFS). Primary result: None. Conclusions: None. This study was industry sponsored.
