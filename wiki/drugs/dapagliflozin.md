### Clinical evidence
A Randomized Controlled Trial (RCT) involving 34 participants compared [[dapagliflozin]] to [[sitagliptin]] for body weight changes from baseline. Weight reduction in the [[dapagliflozin]] group was lower than expected, with a 1.99 kg difference at 24 weeks (P < 0.001). Leptin levels significantly decreased in the [[dapagliflozin]] group. Food intake and appetite scores showed no differences between the groups. Food intake at 12 weeks of [[empagliflozin]] treatment in OLETF rats showed no difference from the controls, while increased intake during the first 3 weeks in the [[empagliflozin]] group. SGLT2 inhibitors support metabolic safety with respect to energy intake by not stimulating appetite or affecting appetite-regulating molecules. Sustained SGLT2 inhibitor treatment does not cause compensatory appetite increase after weight stabilization. Furthermore, it does not stimulate appetite or affect appetite-regulating molecules, thereby supporting its metabolic safety with respect to energy intake. This finding suggests that less-than-expected weight loss with SGLT2 inhibitors may result from mechanisms other than sustained changes in appetite or food intake. (Journal of Korean medical science, 2026)
A Randomized Controlled Trial (RCT) involving 28 participants compared combined zibotentan/[[dapagliflozin]] to placebo. The primary outcome was The absolute change in hepatic venous pressure gradient (HVPG) from baseline to week 6. The primary result was: Absolute change in HVPG at week 6 was not different between groups (1.02 mmHg [90% CI -0.31, 2.35]). Secondary results included a trend towards decreased HVPG in patients with baseline HVPG ≥12 mmHg receiving zibo/dapa, and systolic and diastolic blood pressure were also reduced by zibo/dapa vs. placebo. Three mild adverse events (peripheral oedema) were reported (zibotentan/[[dapagliflozin]], n = 2; placebo, n = 1). No serious adverse events or drug-induced liver injuries were observed. Conclusions: Combined zibo/dapa was well tolerated and had a good safety profile in patients with compensated cirrhosis, but had no conclusive effect on HVPG. (British journal of clinical pharmacology, 2026)
A meta-analysis of eight studies investigated the effects of [[dapagliflozin]] on hemoglobin. The primary result showed significant increase (MD = 4.37; 95% CI = 0.71-8.03; p = 0.02). Secondary results included sensitivity analysis for hematocrit revealed a highly significant increase (MD = 2.15; 95% CI = 1.86-2.44; p < 0.00001), [[dapagliflozin]] significantly reduced the overall risk [of adverse events] (RR = 0.77; 95% CI = 0.59-0.99; p = 0.04), and significantly lower mortality (RR = 0.67; p = 0.02). In the sensitivity analysis for adverse events [[dapagliflozin]] significantly reduced the overall risk (RR = 0.77; 95% CI = 0.59-0.99; p = 0.04). This finding was associated with significantly lower mortality (RR = 0.67; p = 0.02), while no significant differences were observed for cardiovascular (p = 0.22) or genitourinary events (p = 0.70). Conclusions: Dapagliflozin is associated with clinically meaningful improvements in hemoglobin and anemia outcomes in CKD. These findings suggest a potential erythropoietic benefit beyond glycemic control, although dedicated anemia-focused trials are needed to confirm clinical applicability. (Renal failure, 2026)
A systematic review | meta-analysis compared SGLT2i to non-SGLT2i controls or other SGLT2i. SGLT2i versus non-SGLT2i were associated with reduced risks of acute renal failure (ARF) (OR 0.86, 95% CI 0.79-0.94), mortality (OR 0.84, 0.75-0.93), and serious adverse events (SAEs) (OR 0.84, 0.78-0.89), but increased risks of genital infections (OR 3.32, 2.68-4.12) and volume depletion (OR 1.18, 1.09-1.27). The risk of genital infections was observed more frequently with higher doses (high-dose OR 4.73 vs. low-dose OR 2.90) and escalated sharply with age (≥75 years OR 9.29, 3.13-27.6). The mortality benefit was strongest in adults ≥75 years (OR 0.58, 0.38-0.88). Intra-class analysis revealed distinct safety profiles; for instance, [[empagliflozin]] reduced the ARF risk, while sotagliflozin increased the volume depletion risk. Increased risks of genital infections and volume depletion require vigilance, especially with higher doses and in older adults for genital infections. In older adults, SGLT2i maintain a favorable benefit-risk profile, with significant reductions in mortality and SAEs, though risks of genital infections and volume depletion require vigilance. The risk of genital infections exhibits a strong dose-response relationship and increases markedly in the oldest adults, while the mortality benefit appears to be most pronounced in those aged 75 years and older. This study provides actionable insights for personalized therapy in geriatric care. (Medical sciences (Basel, Switzerland), 2026)
A Randomized Controlled Trial (RCT) involving 56 patients compared dapagliflozin to placebo. The primary outcome was reduction in liver MRI-PDFF. The primary result was reduction in liver MRI-PDFF (-3.7% vs. 0.5%, p=0.001). Secondary results included body weight (-3.84 vs. -1.42 kg, p=0.015) and HbA1c (-0.52 vs. 0.11, p=0.012). Twelve months of dapagliflozin was associated with a significant reduction in liver fat as measured by MRI-PDFF compared to placebo; this effect was independent of weight loss and other known beneficial metabolic effects of SGLT2 inhibition. (Obesity (Silver Spring, Md.), 2026)
A Randomized Controlled Trial involving 52 kidney transplant recipients (KTR) compared [[dapagliflozin]] to placebo to evaluate blood pressure lowering. Compared with placebo, [[dapagliflozin]] did not lower systolic BP at 1 or 12 weeks, although it did reduce mean arterial pressure after 1 week (3.9 mm Hg; 95% confidence interval [CI], -7.5 to -0.2). Secondary results showed [[dapagliflozin]] led to significant, placebo-adjusted reductions in iohexol-measured GFR from baseline to 1 week (4.2 ml/min per 1.73 m 2; 95% CI, -7.14 to -1.24 ml/min per 1.73 m 2) and 12 weeks (-3.49 ml/min per 1.73 m 2; 95% CI, -6.33 to -0.64). [[dapagliflozin]] significantly increased glucosuria without altering proximal sodium handling or evidence of sympathetic activation. Acute decreases in arterial stiffness (carotid augmentation index, -3.5%; 95% CI: -6.0 to -1.1) were observed in the [[dapagliflozin]] group after 12 weeks, although this was not significant compared with placebo. [[dapagliflozin]] was generally safe and well tolerated. No episodes of urinary tract or genitourinary infections were observed in either treatment group throughout the trial. Conclusions: Dapagliflozin activated expected physiologic pathways, although key differences observed in KTR might suggest mechanistic heterogeneity compared with nontransplant populations. Clinical trials evaluating sodium-glucose cotransporter-2 inhibitors in KTR are important to determine whether these mechanistic effects translate to improvements in kidney and cardiovascular outcomes. (Clinical journal of the American Society of Nephrology : CJASN, 2026)
A meta-analysis involving 32,471 patients compared [[dapagliflozin]] to placebo for all-cause and CV mortality. Dapagliflozin significantly reduced all-cause (HR: 0.88; 95 % CI: 0.80-0.97; p = 0.008) and CV mortality (HR: 0.89; 95 % CI: 0.80-0.98; p = 0.015) compared to placebo. Secondary results included lowered the rate of MACE (HR: 0.92; 95 % CI: 0.85-0.99; p = 0.019), substantially reduced HF hospitalization (HR: 0.72; 95 % CI: 0.66-0.79; p < 0.001), substantially reduced the composite of HF hospitalization or CV death (HR: 0.79; 95 % CI: 0.73-0.85; p < 0.001), and no significant effects were observed for MI or stroke. This meta-analysis confirms that dapagliflozin significantly reduces both all-cause and CV mortality, along with MACE and HF hospitalization, across a broad CKM spectrum. (Nutrition, metabolism, and cardiovascular diseases : NMCD, 2025)

A meta-analysis involving 98,516 patients compared SGLT2 inhibitors to placebo. The primary outcome was kidney disease progression. The primary result: SGLT2 inhibitors reduced progressive kidney disease risk in diabetic (OR = 0.64, 95% CI: 0.58 - 0.71) and non-diabetic patients (OR = 0.69, 95% CI: 0.57 - 0.83), with no effect modification by diabetes status (p = 0.49). Secondary results included: Among diabetics, risk reductions were notable for [[canagliflozin]] 100mg, [[dapagliflozin]] 10mg, and [[empagliflozin]] 10-mg, particularly in patients with CKD stage 2-3 and baseline eGFR of 30-90 mL/min/1.73m2. DKA risk was elevated in diabetics receiving SGLT2 inhibitors (OR = 2.18, 95% CI: 1.61 - 2.97), particularly with ertugliflozin, sotagliflozin, and [[dapagliflozin]]. Conclusions: SGLT2 inhibitors confer renal protection in both diabetic and non-diabetic populations, supporting their use in CKD management across a broad spectrum of patients. These findings suggest that [[dapagliflozin]] may mitigate medullary hypoxia in patients with CKD. A randomized controlled trial (RCT) applied the win ratio method to evaluate the treatment effect of [[dapagliflozin]] in hierarchically ordered clinical outcomes in the DapaTAVI trial. ### Competitive position
Dapagliflozin, as an SGLT2 inhibitor, competes directly with other drugs in its class such as [[empagliflozin]] and [[canagliflozin]] for the treatment of type 2 diabetes and heart failure. Its differentiation lies in its demonstrated efficacy across various indications, including significant reductions in cardiovascular and all-cause mortality, and benefits in chronic kidney disease, as well as emerging data in MASH and potential neuroprotective effects. The recent finding that sustained SGLT2 inhibitor treatment does not cause compensatory appetite increase after weight stabilization, supporting its metabolic safety, further strengthens its position, particularly in indications where weight management is a factor. While some studies show comparable efficacy with competitors like pioglitazone for glycemic control, dapagliflozin's broader cardiorenal benefits and consistent safety profile across diverse patient populations, including those with HFimpEF and pediatric T2D, position it strongly. Recent findings for zibotentan/dapagliflozin in compensated cirrhosis, while showing no conclusive effect on the primary outcome, expand the potential therapeutic exploration into new disease areas. The potential benefits of dapagliflozin-statin combination therapy for ischemic heart disease patients, as indicated by a recent RCT, further broaden its therapeutic scope and competitive edge. However, the class-specific risks like genital infections and volume depletion, as highlighted in recent meta-analyses, require careful patient selection and monitoring. The distinct safety profiles within the SGLT2i class, where [[empagliflozin]] reduced ARF risk while sotagliflozin increased volume depletion risk, suggest that individual drug characteristics within the class can influence competitive positioning for specific patient needs.
