### Clinical evidence
A randomized controlled trial (TEMPER study) published in 2026, comparing modified EXTREME (cetuximab-based) and modified TPEx regimens for recurrent or metastatic head and neck squamous cell carcinoma (R/M HNSCC) in Japanese patients (n=61), found no significant differences in median progression-free survival (6.0 months for mEXTREME vs 5.3 months for mTPEx, p=0.28) or overall survival (17.4 months vs 18.7 months, p=0.72), with similar toxicity profiles. However, mTPEx showed significantly higher early tumor shrinkage (44% vs 20%, p=0.01).
A systematic review and meta-analysis of 18 RCTs, published in 2025, for advanced biliary tract cancer, found that while durvalumab or pembrolizumab combined with gemcitabine and cisplatin (GC) significantly improved overall survival, cetuximab showed higher serious adverse event risks (OR 1.95) compared to chemotherapy alone.
A Phase II clinical trial, published in 2025, investigating triple therapy with evorpacept, cetuximab, and pembrolizumab for refractory microsatellite-stable metastatic colorectal cancer (n=16), demonstrated antitumor activity but was terminated early due to safety concerns, including two treatment-related grade 5 events (hemophagocytic lymphohistiocytosis and cytokine release syndrome). The objective response rate was 6.3%, disease control rate 12.5%, median progression-free survival 2.3 months, and median overall survival 10.9 months.
An open-label Phase Ib/II clinical trial, published in 2026, evaluating ASP-1929 photoimmunotherapy plus pembrolizumab for recurrent/metastatic HNSCC (n=18), reported a confirmed objective response rate of 27.8% (95% CI 9.7-53.5), with four of five responders achieving complete responses. Median overall survival was 25.6 months and median progression-free survival was 2.9 months. The combination was generally tolerable, with dysphagia and tongue edema being the most common serious adverse reactions.
A long-term follow-up study (median 47.4 months) published in 2026, involving 88 patients, found that the combination of cetuximab and nivolumab was efficacious and tolerable for recurrent/metastatic head and neck squamous cell carcinoma (HNSCC). The median overall survival (OS) was 12.7 months (32% 2-year OS rate) for all patients, and 17.5 months (35% 2-year OS rate) for those with no prior therapy for R/M HNSCC. Patients with p16-negative tumors showed a significantly higher response rate, and the study identified a more inflamed tumor microenvironment in responders.
A network meta-analysis published in 2025, involving 18 RCTs, found that cetuximab showed higher serious adverse event (SAE) risks (OR 1.95) when used in advanced biliary tract cancer compared to chemotherapy alone. The study was a network meta-analysis with chemotherapy alone as the comparator, focusing on overall survival (OS).
A cost-effectiveness analysis published in 2025 found that pembrolizumab combination therapy offers substantial survival and quality-adjusted life-years (QALYs) gains for recurrent or metastatic head and neck squamous cell carcinoma (R/M HNSCC) patients, with an incremental cost-effectiveness ratio (ICER) lower than the Colombian willingness-to-pay threshold, making it a cost-effective treatment compared to the EXTREME regime (cetuximab + platinum + 5-FU).
An indirect treatment comparison meta-analysis published in 2025 found that KN-B10 (pembrolizumab with carboplatin and paclitaxel) was comparable to EXTREME (cetuximab + platinum + 5-FU) and more efficacious than other treatments for objective response in R/M HNSCC. KN-B10 also showed comparable overall survival (OS) and progression-free survival (PFS) to EXTREME, with improvements in OS after month 12.
5-year follow-up data from the randomized phase III KEYNOTE-048 study, published in 2025, demonstrated that pembrolizumab monotherapy and pembrolizumab-chemotherapy had superior overall survival (OS) compared to cetuximab-chemotherapy (EXTREME) in first-line R/M HNSCC, particularly in PD-L1 positive populations, supporting their use as first-line standards of care.
A Phase II clinical trial published in 2025 found that the addition of cetuximab to pembrolizumab in locally advanced/metastatic cutaneous squamous cell carcinomas (LA/M cSCC) reverted primary and acquired resistance with manageable toxicities, achieving a cumulative objective response rate of 63% (95% CI 48-77).
A Network Meta-Analysis of 37 RCTs for advanced EGFR-mutated NSCLC, published in 2025, identified that afatinib + cetuximab provided the best overall survival outcomes for 19del and male cases.
