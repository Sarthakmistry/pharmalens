### Management sentiment
Management sentiment for osimertinib is bullish. Recent clinical data from the FLAURA2 study demonstrated significantly longer overall survival when osimertinib is combined with platinum-pemetrexed chemotherapy as a first-line treatment for EGFR-mutated advanced NSCLC. Studies also show promising activity and manageable safety for osimertinib in combination with savolitinib for MET-aberrant NSCLC and with Dato-DXd for patients progressing on first-line osimertinib. Furthermore, neoadjuvant osimertinib has shown statistically significant improvement in major pathologic response rates in resectable NSCLC, and first-line osimertinib is effective in patients with active brain metastases. While some competitive regimens show superior outcomes, osimertinib continues to demonstrate strong efficacy and safety profiles in various settings, reinforcing its position as a key treatment.

### Clinical evidence
- **2026:** Osimertinib plus Dato-DXd demonstrated clinical benefit in patients with EGFR-mutated advanced NSCLC who progressed on first-line osimertinib, with a confirmed ORR of 36-43%, median PFS of 9.5-11.7 months, and median OS of 19.8-26.2 months. Grade ≥3 AEs were manageable. (NCT03944772)
- **2026:** In the China cohort of the LAURA study (n=40), osimertinib after definitive chemoradiotherapy for unresectable stage III EGFR-mutated NSCLC demonstrated a median PFS not reached versus 3.7 months for placebo, with an ORR of 63% versus 15%. The safety profile was manageable.
- **2026:** First-line osimertinib treatment is effective in patients with active brain metastases (BM), with an overall response rate (ORR) of 69.6% in patients with active BM and an intracranial ORR of 81.8% in those with measurable BM. L858R-mutation or uncommon EGFR-mutations and presence of ctDNA at baseline were associated with poorer outcomes.
- **2026:** Amivantamab-lazertinib significantly improved OS versus osimertinib among Asian participants with previously untreated EGFR-mutated advanced NSCLC, with median OS not reached for the combination versus 38.4 months for osimertinib (HR, 0.74; nominal P = 0.026).
- **2026:** A phase 1/2 study of brigatinib plus panitumumab in 5 patients with osimertinib-resistant EGFR-mutated NSCLC harboring EGFR C797S mutation showed no objective responses and a median PFS of 1.7 months, leading to discontinuation of clinical development due to lack of feasibility and efficacy.
- **2026:** The SACHI trial (NCT05015608) showed that savolitinib-osimertinib significantly improved investigator-assessed progression-free survival (PFS) versus platinum-based doublet chemotherapy in EGFR mutation-positive, MET-amplified NSCLC patients after EGFR TKI failure, with median PFS of 9.8 months vs 5.4 months in TKI-naive patients (HR 0.34; p<0.0001).
- **2026:** Patient-reported outcomes from a study of amivantamab-chemotherapy showed that clinical benefits were achieved without compromising health-related quality of life, and time to symptomatic progression was prolonged versus chemotherapy alone (HR 0.73; nominal P = .0259).
- **2026:** The FLOWERS randomized phase 2 trial (NCT05163249) investigated osimertinib with or without savolitinib for MET-aberrant, EGFR-mutant NSCLC. The combination therapy achieved a confirmed objective response rate (ORR) of 90.5% compared to 60.9% for osimertinib monotherapy, with manageable safety.
- **2026:** A meta-analysis of six studies found that PD-L1 ≥50% was associated with a more than twofold increased risk of progression or death in EGFR-mutant NSCLC patients on first-line osimertinib, suggesting it is a negative prognostic factor.
- **2026:** The FLAURA2 study (NCT04035486) found that first-line osimertinib plus platinum-pemetrexed led to significantly longer overall survival (median 47.5 months vs 37.6 months; HR 0.77; P=0.02) than osimertinib monotherapy in EGFR-mutated advanced NSCLC, with manageable, reversible grade 3 or higher adverse events.
- **2026:** Amivantamab plus lazertinib has antitumor activity in EGFR-mutant lung cancers with new or progressing brain metastases (BrM) or leptomeningeal disease (LMD), with composite ORRs of 50% for BrM and 33% for LMD. (NCT04965090)
- **2026:** In patients with non-CNS progression on first-line osimertinib, osimertinib plus chemotherapy (cisplatin/carboplatin/pemetrexed) was associated with improved PFS (median 8.4 vs 4.4 months; HR 0.43) and longer OS (median 15.9 vs 9.8 months; HR 0.71) compared with placebo plus chemotherapy. (NCT04765059)
- **2026:** A meta-analysis of EGFR-TKIs in NSCLC found that new-generation EGFR-TKIs (afatinib, osimertinib, dacomitinib) demonstrated superior efficacy and safety, including decreased risks of hepatotoxicity, compared to first-generation TKIs. Osimertinib extended overall survival (HR 0.71).
- **2025:** A prospective study (NCT05219162) on first-line osimertinib failure in EGFR-mutated advanced NSCLC highlighted the utility of plasma samples for identifying genomic profiles, showing high specificity (90.7-100%) for most genomic alterations compared to tissue samples.
- **2025:** A systematic review and network meta-analysis found that third-generation EGFR tyrosine kinase inhibitors, including osimertinib, were associated with significantly increased risks of cardiac adverse events (OR 2.18, 95% CI 1.46 to 3.27; pooled incidence 9.5%) compared with placebo, and higher risks than first-generation inhibitors.
- **2025:** An observational study (NCT06020625) found that in EGFR M+ patients treated with osimertinib, concurrent NRG1 alterations were an independent poor prognostic factor for both progression-free survival (PFS) and overall survival (OS), with significantly shorter PFS (12.07 vs 26.07 months) and OS (20.45 vs 34.06 months) for NRG1 M+ patients.
- **2025:** A study found that a reduced dose of cobicistat boosted osimertinib (80 mg 2 days on, 1 day off, plus cobicistat 150 mg QD) could theoretically save approximately 33% in osimertinib treatment costs while maintaining adequate osimertinib exposure, though not equivalent for the active metabolite AZ5104.
- **2025:** The PALOMA-3 study showed that subcutaneous amivantamab demonstrated noninferior pharmacokinetics and response rates versus intravenous amivantamab, with faster administration, a 5-fold reduction in infusion-related reactions, and improved patient satisfaction.
- **2025:** A population pharmacokinetics analysis of FLAURA2 (NCT04035486) reinforced the benefits of osimertinib plus platinum-pemetrexed chemotherapy as a first-line treatment for EGFR-mutated advanced NSCLC, finding no clinically meaningful impact of covariates on osimertinib pharmacokinetics and no exposure-PFS relationship for osimertinib.
- **2025:** The MARIPOSA study found that lazertinib monotherapy demonstrated comparable efficacy and safety to osimertinib monotherapy in treatment-naive EGFR-mutant advanced NSCLC, with similar median PFS (18.5 vs 16.6 months; HR 0.98) and OS, and lower rates of QT interval prolongation for lazertinib.
- **2025:** A study of 98 patients with EGFR and TP53 co-mutations in NSCLC found that osimertinib plus chemotherapy had a tendency to increase ORR (80.0% vs 71.7%) and prolong PFS (26.0 vs 20.7 months) compared to osimertinib monotherapy, with a statistically significant prolongation of median PFS in the L858R mutation subgroup.
- **2025:** The ORCHARD study (NCT03944772) evaluated osimertinib plus necitumumab in 19 patients with EGFR-mutated advanced NSCLC who progressed on first-line osimertinib, showing a modest objective response rate of 11% and median PFS of 4.0 months. The study concluded that further evaluation of this regimen is not warranted due to modest clinical benefit.
- **2025:** Savolitinib 300 mg b.i.d. plus osimertinib demonstrated high, clinically meaningful, and durable responses (ORR 56.3%, mDoR 7.1 months, mPFS 7.4 months) in patients with EGFR-mutated, advanced NSCLC with MET overexpression/amplification following progression on first-line osimertinib, with a well-tolerated safety profile. (NCT03778229)
- **2025:** Neoadjuvant osimertinib with or without chemotherapy demonstrated statistically significant improvement in major pathologic response (MPR) rates (25-26%) over chemotherapy alone (2%) in resectable, EGFR-mutated, stage II-IIIB NSCLC, with no new safety concerns.
- **2025:** A meta-analysis of 9 RCTs (2603 participants) found that adjuvant EGFR TKIs probably improve overall survival (HR 0.54) and may improve disease-free survival (HR 0.34) compared to placebo/best supportive care, and may improve DFS (HR 0.54) with reduced serious adverse events compared to chemotherapy.
- **2025:** In Asian participants of a study comparing amivantamab-lazertinib to osimertinib, amivantamab-lazertinib showed a 35% reduction in the risk of disease progression or death versus osimertinib (HR 0.65; P < 0.001), with a median PFS of 27.5 months vs 18.3 months, and a tolerable safety profile.
- **2025:** Osimertinib combined with anlotinib demonstrated promising long-term efficacy and manageable safety in 31 patients with EGFR T790M-positive NSCLC, with a median PFS of 16.2 months, median OS of 31.4 months, ORR of 45.2%, and DCR of 96.8%. (NCT04029350)
- **2025:** A randomized Phase III study (501 patients) comparing EGFR TKI monotherapy vs. EGFR TKI with intercalated platinum-doublet chemotherapy for advanced non-squamous NSCLC with EGFR mutation showed improved progression-free survival (median 18.0 vs 12.0 months; HR 0.762) for the combination, but no overall survival advantage.
- **2025:** A randomized controlled trial (NCT02511106) found that osimertinib showed significant disease-free survival and molecular residual disease event-free rate improvement in resected EGFR-mutated NSCLC, with a 36-month DFS and MRD event-free rate of 86% for osimertinib vs. 36% for placebo (HR, 0.23).
- **2025:** A clinical trial of TY-9591 (asandeutertinib) in 105 patients with EGFR-mutated NSCLC reported a favorable safety profile and substantial efficacy, with a median PFS of 21.5 months for first-line treatment and an ORR of 85.9%.
- **2025:** The SKIPPirr study (NCT05663866) found that prophylaxis with 8 mg oral dexamethasone meaningfully reduced infusion-related reactions (IRRs) for amivantamab-lazertinib (C1D1 IRR incidence of 22.5% vs historical 67.4%), making the regimen more manageable.
- **2025:** Teliso-V plus osimertinib (NCT02099058) showed promising activity and a manageable safety profile in 38 patients with c-Met protein-overexpressing, EGFR-mutated non-squamous NSCLC after progression on osimertinib, with an objective response rate (ORR) of 50.0% and median progression-free survival (PFS) of 7.4 months.
- **2025:** A pilot study (NCT04351555, NCT04816838) of neoadjuvant osimertinib in 25 patients with resectable EGFR-mutant NSCLC reported an objective response rate of 44% and a major pathologic response rate of 24%, concluding that it is a feasible approach with a manageable safety profile, despite not meeting the primary endpoint of ORR.
- **2024:** A Phase 1/2 clinical trial of amivantamab plus lazertinib in 162 patients with EGFR-mutated NSCLC demonstrated an investigator-assessed objective response rate (ORR) of 28% and a median progression-free survival of 4.5 months, with a manageable safety profile.

### Competitive position
Osimertinib is a leading third-generation EGFR TKI for NSCLC. While it demonstrates strong efficacy as monotherapy and in combination with chemotherapy, particularly in first-line settings and for patients with brain metastases, it faces increasing competition. Regimens like amivantamab-lazertinib have shown superior overall survival benefits in some EGFR-mutated advanced NSCLC populations, especially in Asian participants, though often with a higher incidence of adverse events. Combinations with other agents like savolitinib and Dato-DXd are being explored to overcome resistance mechanisms and improve outcomes in specific patient subsets. However, some combinations, such as with necitumumab or brigatinib/panitumumab, have shown modest or no clinical benefit, indicating the need for careful selection of combination partners. The economic accessibility of osimertinib also varies by region, being more accessible in China than in the USA.
