### Management sentiment
Management expressed bullish sentiment regarding tirzepatide's commercial performance, with Mounjaro and Zepbound being the primary drivers of Eli Lilly's significant revenue increase in Q1 2025. However, the company also noted the withdrawal of the U.S. application for [[tirzepatide]] in heart failure with preserved ejection fraction, indicating a setback in that specific pipeline expansion.

### Clinical evidence
A systematic review and meta-analysis published in the European journal of medical research in 2026, involving 12 articles, found that GLP-1RAs were associated with an increase in heart rate (HR) in non-diabetic individuals with overweight or obesity. Tirzepatide showed an increase (MD 2.05), with tirzepatide 5 mg associated with the least pronounced HR increase (MD 0.52).
A meta-analysis published in The Journal of clinical endocrinology and metabolism in 2025, involving 2600 patients, demonstrated that GLP-1RAs significantly reduced liver fat content (LFC) and induced histological improvements in steatosis, hepatocellular ballooning, and lobular inflammation in MASLD and MASH. Tirzepatide showed more robust evidence for histological improvements compared to semaglutide and liraglutide, and no drug-related adverse effects involving the liver were observed.
A Network Meta-Analysis involving 1667 patients compared various treatments against placebo for fibrosis improvement (≥1 stage) without worsening steatohepatitis. The primary result showed that All active treatments, including [[dapagliflozin]] 10 mg, survodutide (2.4 mg/wk, 4.8-6 mg/wk), [[tirzepatide]] (5 mg/wk, 10-15 mg/wk), and [[semaglutide]] (0.7-1.4 mg/wk, 2.4 or 2.8 mg/wk), improved fibrosis versus placebo (I2 = 0%). For MASH resolution, dose-dependent effects led to significant heterogeneity (I2 = 73%), with lower-dose [[semaglutide]] demonstrating no benefits and [[dapagliflozin]] showing benefits in the F2-F3 subgroup only on sensitivity analysis. survodutide exhibited the highest ranking (SUCRA = 0.822-0.849), followed by [[tirzepatide]] (SUCRA = 0.622-0.681) and higher-dose [[semaglutide]] (SUCRA = 0.327) for MASH resolution. Meta-regression using data from 16 interventions, including placebo arms, showed that weight loss significantly explained heterogeneity in treatment effects on fibrosis improvement (R2 = 54.26%) and MASH resolution (R2 = 78.16%). The study concluded: SGLT2 inhibitor and incretin-based agents improved fibrosis in MASH, with weight loss being a significant mediator. Targeting multiple incretin pathways, especially involving glucagon receptors, may offer greater MASH resolution. Dose-dependent effects were more prominent for MASH resolution than fibrosis improvement, indicating potential weight-loss-independent anti-fibrotic pathways. (Diabetes, obesity & metabolism, 2026)

A meta-analysis published in Diabetes, obesity & metabolism in 2026, involving 3173 subjects, compared treatments against placebo for changes in biopsy MASLD Activity Scores (MAS). Steatohepatitis scores (MAS) improved with treatment versus placebo by mean difference (md) = -1.27 ± 0.16 SD Units, p < 0.001. MAS sub scores improved for steatosis, lobar inflammation, and ballooning for [[dapagliflozin]], [[semaglutide]], and pioglitazone (all p < 0.002). Fibrosis Grades improved compared to placebo (md = -0.352 ± 0.03 Units, p < 0.001). Relative rates (rr) of RSw/oF and RFw/oS were found with resmetirom, [[semaglutide]], [[tirzepatide]], and [[dapagliflozin]] (all p < 0.015). Changes in RSw/oF and RFw/oS inversely correlated with the baseline levels of Fibrosis Grades (p = 0.025, and p = 0.076 respectively), with greater improvements of both at lower Fibrosis Grades. Clinically available medications are beneficial in reversing MASH. Improvements in RSw/oF and RFw/oS were greater at earlier stages of fibrosis. Future analyses of drug effects should include assessments adjusted for baseline study characteristics of Fibrosis Grades and may evaluate whether preventive therapy will have long term benefits if started at earlier stages of MASLD.
A meta-analysis of 62 studies (66,232 patients) published in Endocrinology, diabetes & metabolism in 2025, investigating the safety of GLP-1 RAs, found a significantly increased risk of pancreatitis (RR: 1.44, 95% CI 1.09-1.89, p = 0.009). However, the overall risk for pancreatic cancer was not significantly associated with GLP-1 RA use (RR: 1.30, 95% CI 0.86-1.97), and any observed differences in pancreatic cancer risk with background medications were considered likely minimal.
A meta-analysis published in Diabetes technology & therapeutics in 2025, involving 56,683 patients, compared the efficacy of GLP-1RAs for weight management and glycemic control. Tirzepatide (15 mg qw) demonstrated good weight loss ability, and tirzepatide (10 mg qw) was found to be the most effective drug for glycemic control (ΔHbA1c).
A meta-analysis published in Obesity surgery in 2025 found that GLP-1 agonists, including tirzepatide, were associated with a statistically significant decrease in weight (MD = 8.07 kg) and body mass index (BMI) (MD = 4.42 kg/m2 for liraglutide and semaglutide) in patients experiencing weight recurrence or suboptimal clinical response after metabolic bariatric surgery.
An indirect treatment comparison (ITC) informed by pivotal trials (SURMOUNT-2 and STEP 2) published in Diabetes, obesity & metabolism in 2025, concluded that tirzepatide 10 mg and 15 mg were associated with statistically significant greater reductions in weight, BMI, and HbA1c compared to semaglutide 2.4 mg. Tirzepatide 15 mg also showed significant improvements in cardiometabolic risk factors, with both doses having a generally comparable safety profile to semaglutide.
A meta-analysis published in Diabetes, obesity & metabolism in 2025, including 16,660 individuals with type 2 diabetes, found that while the efficacy of incretin mimetics varied widely, larger therapeutic efficacy (e.g., tirzepatide's -1.79% HbA1c reduction) was not systematically associated with higher gastrointestinal adverse event or drug discontinuation rates, suggesting better tolerability for more effective agents.
A meta-analysis published in Obesity reviews in 2025, involving 2372 participants, indicated that discontinuation of GLP-1RA therapy, including semaglutide/tirzepatide, leads to significant weight regain (9.69 kg), proportional to the original weight loss. The study suggests GLP-1RA treatment should be considered a chronic therapy to prevent weight regain.
A post hoc analysis of RCTs published in Diabetes, obesity & metabolism in 2025, involving 6246 participants with T2D, demonstrated that tirzepatide resulted in substantially longer continuous time in glycaemic control (80% of treatment duration with HbA1c < 7.0% vs 70% for semaglutide) and more sustained body weight reductions compared to placebo and active comparators.
A meta-analysis of 29,506 patients published in Obesity (Silver Spring, Md.) in 2025, comparing weight loss treatments, found that retatrutide and dual agonists (like tirzepatide) achieved equivalent mean weight loss (-11.0 kg), surpassing GLP-1RAs (-9.0 kg). However, retatrutide had the highest adverse event risk, while dual agonists provided a favorable efficacy-safety balance.
