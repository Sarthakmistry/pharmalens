### Clinical evidence
The combination of nivolumab and hyperfractionated reirradiation appears feasible and safe for recurrent HNSCC. (Radiotherapy and oncology : journal of the European Society for Therapeutic Radiology and Oncology, 2026)

Long-term results from COSMIC-313 continue to demonstrate a PFS benefit with the addition of cabozantinib to nivolumab and ipilimumab. There was no OS benefit and no new safety signals were observed. Exploratory biomarker analyses suggest adding cabozantinib to nivolumab and ipilimumab improves survival in patients with high levels of M2-like macrophages. (Annals of oncology : official journal of the European Society for Medical Oncology, 2026)

Dual immunotherapy significantly improved ORR compared with monotherapy but this benefit was accompanied by significantly increased toxicity and did not consistently translate into statistically significant PFS or OS benefits across trials. These findings highlight the need for personalized risk-benefit assessment. Future research should focus on biomarker-driven patient selection and strategies to overcome resistance. (BMC cancer, 2026)

Nivolumab combined with SOC was associated with encouraging FFBR in this high-risk GG5 PCa population and may represent a promising therapeutic intensification strategy. (Journal for immunotherapy of cancer, 2026)

Nivolumab in combination with neoadjuvant CRT is safe for patients with locally advanced ESCC, and may be beneficial in those with high PD-L1 expression. (British journal of cancer, 2026)

For advanced nsNSCLC patients, irrespective of PD-L1 expression, prolgolimab, [[pembrolizumab]] or cemiplimab, each combined with chemotherapy, are most efficacious by OS; [[nivolumab]] or [[atezolizumab]] combined with [[bevacizumab]] and chemotherapy demonstrate the highest PFS. PD-L1 expression does not modify the effect of examined immunotherapy options on OS, though the opposite is true for PFS. (Critical reviews in oncology/hematology, 2026)

First-line anti-PD-1-based immunotherapy combinations significantly improve survival and response outcomes over sunitinib in advanced RCC, with consistent efficacy across PD-1 subtypes and PD-L1 expression groups. (Urology practice, 2026)

Ultra-low-dose [[nivolumab]] significantly improves OS versus chemotherapy in pretreated solid tumors, with fewer severe toxicities and better QoL. These findings support re-evaluation of ICI dosing strategies and may enhance global access. (Journal of clinical oncology : official journal of the American Society of Clinical Oncology, 2026)

Among cisplatin-ineligible patients with MIBC, [[nivolumab]] alone was well tolerated. Ipilimumab/nivolumab caused toxicity that delayed cystectomy. Cases of progression before cystectomy indicated insufficient efficacy of pure neoadjuvant immunotherapy for unselected patients. Despite low response rates, some patients experienced sustained clinical CR without cystectomy. (Clinical cancer research : an official journal of the American Association for Cancer Research, 2026)

Results from this study support the use of BV and [[nivolumab]] in combination with limited chemotherapy for patients with nonbulky, early-stage cHL. (Blood, 2026)

Larger baseline tumor burden was associated with worse OS, but not PFS, and was not predictive of tumor regression after dual ICI therapy in a large cohort with rare cancer types. (Cancer, 2026)

This phase I study demonstrates the feasibility and safety of combining FMT with anti-PD-1 therapy in patients with ICI-refractory gastric cancer. The observed preliminary efficacy signals and identified microbial signatures generate hypotheses for future trials to investigate microbiome-based approaches to enhance immunotherapy efficacy. (Journal for immunotherapy of cancer, 2026)

While further studies are needed, initial findings suggest that [[nivolumab]] plus sunitinib could be a valuable addition to the current armamentarium for CCS management. (Cancer communications (London, England), 2026)

The MTD of KPT-9274 was not reached in a dose escalation study conducted in patients with advanced solid tumors. Clinical activity was not observed at study doses in combination with niacin or [[nivolumab]]. Further investigation into potential therapeutic areas where KPT-9274 may serve as a targeted agent is warranted. (Targeted oncology, 2026)

Adding ipilimumab and [[nivolumab]] to percutaneous hepatic perfusion significantly improved progression-free survival, but with a higher rate of adverse events. The combination therapy offers a promising new treatment paradigm for patients with metastatic uveal melanoma. These results would ideally be validated in larger, multicentre randomised trials; however, conducting such studies is challenging due to the low incidence of uveal melanoma. (The Lancet. Oncology, 2026)

In this first stage of a pilot study that awaits confirmation with larger numbers of patients, our results suggest that vaccination targeting AR given prior to androgen deprivation therapy might improve outcome for patients with high-risk prostate cancer. Here we show that chemotherapy with dual checkpoint inhibition in parallel increases toxicity without improving efficacy, while FOLFOX followed by immunotherapy is insufficient. FLOT with [[nivolumab]] appears feasible and shows promising efficacy. (Nature communications, 2026)

TMB, somatic BRAF status and systemic inflammation should be prospectively investigated as practical biomarkers for predicting potential responsiveness to immune checkpoint inhibitors in metastatic microsatellite-stable/mismatch repair-proficient colorectal cancer. These findings highlight the value of longitudinal profiling to resolve evolving tumor microenvironment dynamics driving ICI response. (Nature cancer, 2026)

The prognostic impact of PLR is particularly robust in the [[nivolumab]]-treated RCC, [[pembrolizumab]]-treated NSCLC, camrelizumab-treated gastrointestinal tumors, and various advanced-stage malignancies. ### Competitive position
A recent meta-analysis in advanced renal cell carcinoma (aRCC) suggests that the combination of [[pembrolizumab]] and lenvatinib may offer superior progression-free survival (PFS) and objective response rate (ORR) compared to other approved immune checkpoint inhibitor (ICI) combinations, including [[nivolumab]] + [[ipilimumab]]. While no significant difference in overall survival (OS) was observed, the improved PFS and ORR for the competitor combination could pose a competitive challenge for [[nivolumab]]-based regimens in this indication. A recent RCT in resectable stage III melanoma indicated that tobemstomig, a competitor, achieved a similar pathological response rate to [[nivolumab]] plus [[ipilimumab]] while demonstrating an improved safety profile. This suggests a potential competitive challenge for [[nivolumab]]-based regimens in this melanoma setting, particularly regarding safety. Additionally, a recent network meta-analysis in advanced squamous non-small cell lung cancer (SqNSCLC) highlighted that for tumors with PD-L1 <1%, [[nivolumab]] provided significant survival benefit, suggesting a specific patient population where it demonstrates strong efficacy compared to other immune checkpoint inhibitors. Furthermore, a meta-analysis on first-line advanced HCC indicated that while ICI-based combinations generally outperform sorafenib, other specific combinations like sintilimab plus bevacizumab biosimilar, camrelizumab plus rivoceranib, and [[atezolizumab]] plus bevacizumab showed significant OS advantages. However, [[nivolumab]] was noted to be associated with one of the lowest incidences of AEs≥3, suggesting a potential competitive advantage in terms of safety profile. Moreover, recent clinical trial results for the add-on strategy of regorafenib plus [[nivolumab]] in second-line hepatocellular carcinoma demonstrated manageable safety and significant clinical activity with a noteworthy median overall survival of 20 months, further supporting [[nivolumab]]'s potential in this challenging indication. A systematic review and meta-analysis in advanced non-squamous non-small cell lung cancer (nsNSCLC) found that while prolgolimab + chemotherapy, [[pembrolizumab]] + chemotherapy, and cemiplimab + chemotherapy were most efficacious by OS, [[nivolumab]] + [[bevacizumab]] + chemotherapy demonstrated the highest PFS, indicating a strong competitive edge in this specific outcome for nsNSCLC. Additionally, recent RCT data in unresectable stage IIIA/IIIB NSCLC demonstrated that both [[nivolumab]] alone and in combination with [[ipilimumab]] significantly improved 18-month progression-free survival over historical controls, reinforcing its efficacy in this challenging lung cancer setting.

New data further refines [[nivolumab]]'s competitive landscape. In muscle-invasive bladder cancer, adjuvant [[nivolumab]] after NAC and surgery was identified as the most effective strategy, positioning it strongly in this indication. However, in perioperative NSCLC, while immunotherapeutic schemes are superior to placebo for PD-L1 ≥50%, P-toripalimab showed a greater PFS effect for PD-L1 <1% and 1-49%, suggesting potential competition in these subgroups. For advanced NSCLC, a TRD predictive score indicated that high-risk patients treated with [[nivolumab]] and [[ipilimumab]] plus platinum doublet chemotherapy (NIC) had significantly higher TRD incidence and worse outcomes compared to [[pembrolizumab]] plus chemotherapy (PC), which could limit its use in this patient population. In advanced melanoma, a targeted-therapy induction regimen did not improve PFS compared to first-line [[nivolumab]] plus [[ipilimumab]], reinforcing the role of ICI as first-line. However, an observational study in melanoma identified elevated plasma TNF as a factor associated with poorer ICI outcomes, including for [[nivolumab]]. In the context of cost-effectiveness for MSI-H/dMMR mCRC in China, [[nivolumab]] + [[ipilimumab]] was found not to be cost-effective compared to [[pembrolizumab]] alone, which could impact market access. Despite these challenges, [[nivolumab]] plus [[ipilimumab]] demonstrated significant OS benefit and manageable safety in unresectable hepatocellular carcinoma compared to lenvatinib or sorafenib, strengthening its position. It also showed durable efficacy in MSI/dMMR chemoresistant mCRC after 5-year follow-up. In rare cancers, [[nivolumab]] plus [[ipilimumab]] showed promising durable responses in SCCOHT and refractory pancreatic neuroendocrine neoplasms (PNEN), highlighting its utility in niche indications. Furthermore, in non-clear cell renal cell cancer, [[ipilimumab]]/[[nivolumab]] demonstrated significantly longer OS and acceptable toxicity compared to standard of care. The combination of [[nivolumab]] with personalized neoantigen-targeting vaccines also showed promise in boosting T cell responses in melanoma. The high pathological response rates and 100% 2-year DSS in resectable cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma, especially with organ preservation, represent a strong positive for [[nivolumab]], potentially expanding its market or solidifying its position in this niche. Additionally, the identification of LRP1b mutations as a predictive biomarker for [[nivolumab]] in NSCLC could help in patient selection, potentially improving treatment efficacy and competitive standing by targeting the right patient population.
