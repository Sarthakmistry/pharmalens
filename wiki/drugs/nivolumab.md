### Clinical evidence
A systematic review and meta-analysis published in Critical reviews in oncology/hematology in 2026 compared anti-PD-1/PD-L1-containing regimens for advanced non-squamous non-small cell lung cancer (nsNSCLC). The primary outcome was overall (OS) and progression-free (PFS). Regardless of PD-L1 expression, treatments with the highest probability of being the best (SUCRA) by OS are prolgolimab + chemotherapy, [[pembrolizumab]] + chemotherapy and cemiplimab + chemotherapy (SUCRA 0.80-0.94), by PFS [[nivolumab]] + [[bevacizumab]] + chemotherapy and [[atezolizumab]] + [[bevacizumab]] + chemotherapy (SUCRA 0.91-0.96). The hazard ratio for the interaction between PD-L1-negative status and anti-PD-1/PD-L1-containing therapy was 1.09 (95% CrI, 0.95-1.25) for OS and 1.41 (95% CrI, 1.16-1.71) for PFS. For advanced nsNSCLC patients, irrespective of PD-L1 expression, prolgolimab, [[pembrolizumab]] or cemiplimab, each combined with chemotherapy, are most efficacious by OS; [[nivolumab]] or [[atezolizumab]] combined with [[bevacizumab]] and chemotherapy demonstrate the highest PFS. PD-L1 expression does not modify the effect of examined immunotherapy options on OS, though the opposite is true for PFS. (Critical reviews in oncology/hematology, 2026)

A network meta-analysis published in Urologic oncology in 2026 included 12 randomized controlled trials with 5,026 patients with muscle-invasive bladder cancer (MIBC). The primary outcome was overall survival (OS). The most effective strategy was neoadjuvant chemotherapy (NAC) followed by surgery and adjuvant [[nivolumab]] (SUCRA: 99%, HR 0.43, 95% CrI 0.24-0.74). Perioperative [[durvalumab]]-based regimens (SUCRA: 82%) and dose-dense MVAC (SUCRA: 78%) also demonstrated significant benefit. Moderate heterogeneity (I² = 46%) was observed. The study concluded that these findings support integrating chemotherapy and immunotherapy into MIBC treatment, with adjuvant [[nivolumab]] after NAC and surgery appearing most effective. (Urologic oncology, 2026)

A network meta-analysis published in Farmacia hospitalaria in 2026 compared perioperative immunotherapies for resectable non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) based on tumor PD-L1 expression, including P-toripalimab, P-[[nivolumab]], P-[[pembrolizumab]], and P-[[durvalumab]]. P-toripalimab showed a greater magnitude of effect in progression-free survival (PFS) for populations with PD-L1 <1% and 1-49%. No benefit of any immunotherapeutic combination over P-placebo was observed for PD-L1 expression <1%. Immunotherapeutic schemes were superior to P-placebo for PD-L1 expression ≥50%. The study concluded that the meta-analysis provides reliable evidence on the efficacy of perioperative immunotherapy in resectable NSCLC according to PD-L1 expression levels. (Farmacia hospitalaria, 2026)

A meta-analysis of 24 randomized controlled trials (n=13,572 patients) investigated first-line therapy in advanced hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC), comparing various ICI-based combinations against sorafenib. The primary outcome was overall survival (OS) and progression-free survival (PFS). In the overall population, regimens with significant OS advantage over sorafenib included sintilimab plus [[bevacizumab]] biosimilar (HR = 0.57, 95% CrI 0.43-0.75), camrelizumab plus rivoceranib (HR = 0.62, 0.48-0.79), and [[atezolizumab]] plus [[bevacizumab]] (HR = 0.66, 0.51-0.84). For PFS, top-ranked combinations were camrelizumab plus rivoceranib (HR = 0.52, 0.41-0.66), anlotinib plus penpulimab (HR = 0.53, 0.41-0.68), lenvatinib plus [[pembrolizumab]] (HR = 0.55, 0.44-0.68), and sintilimab plus [[bevacizumab]] biosimilar (HR = 0.56, 0.45-0.69). ORR was highest with lenvatinib plus [[pembrolizumab]] (RR = 8.00, 4.98-12.86). In HBV-related HCC, sintilimab plus [[bevacizumab]] biosimilar and [[atezolizumab]] plus [[bevacizumab]] led OS rankings, with PFS favoring cabozantinib plus [[atezolizumab]] and [[atezolizumab]] plus [[bevacizumab]]. In HCV-related HCC, only [[atezolizumab]] plus [[bevacizumab]] conferred a significant OS benefit (HR = 0.43, 0.25-0.73), while PFS superiority was observed only for cabozantinib plus [[atezolizumab]] (HR = 0.73, 0.54-0.99). In non-viral HCC, the STRIDE regimen (single priming dose tremelimumab plus [[durvalumab]]) was the only regimen to significantly improve OS (HR = 0.75, 0.59-0.96). Regarding safety, tislelizumab (RR = 0.42, 0.33-0.52) and [[nivolumab]] (RR = 0.45, 0.36-0.56) were associated with the lowest incidence of AEs≥3. For first-line therapy in advanced HCC, ICI-based combinations with anti-VEGF/anti-angiogenic agents generally outperform sorafenib, with discernible etiology-specific optima: HBV-related HCC favors sintilimab plus [[bevacizumab]] biosimilar or [[atezolizumab]] plus [[bevacizumab]]; HCV-related HCC favors [[atezolizumab]] plus [[bevacizumab]]; and in non-viral disease, STRIDE demonstrates a unique OS advantage. This etiology-stratified evidence framework may guide individualized first-line decision-making, pending confirmation in head-to-head trials. (Frontiers in immunology, 2026)

A Phase I/IIa investigator-initiated trial (GOING) evaluated the add-on strategy of regorafenib plus [[nivolumab]] in 67 patients with second-line hepatocellular carcinoma, with safety as the primary outcome. All experienced adverse events and 9% led to discontinuation. Severe treatment-related adverse events occurred in 28.4% overall, 32.1% in Cohort-A, and 14.3% in Cohort-B. Secondary results showed a median overall survival of 20.0 (95% CI 11-37) months, with 40.6% survival at 36 months, and an objective response rate of 16.4%. The study concluded: The add-on strategy of regorafenib plus [[nivolumab]] in second-line had manageable safety and significant clinical activity with a noteworthy median overall survival of 20 months. Among those progressing on sorafenib, 45% were alive at 3 years. These findings support further evaluation with mechanism-guided trials after progression on immunotherapy. (Alimentary pharmacology & therapeutics, 2026)

A Systematic Review and Network Meta-Analysis of 25 randomized controlled trials compared various immune checkpoint inhibitors (ICIs) to chemotherapy alone for overall survival (OS) and progression-free survival (PFS) in advanced squamous non-small cell lung cancer. Cemiplimab [HR = 0.48, 95% CI: (0.34-0.67)] showed the best OS benefit. Sugemalimab+chemo provided the best PFS benefit [HR = 0.34, 95% CI: (0.24-0.48)]. For PD-L1≥50% tumors, penpulimab showed excellent OS and PFS. For PD-L1 1-49% tumors, [[pembrolizumab]]+chemo and camrelizumab+chemo achieved the best OS and PFS, respectively. For PD-L1 <1% tumors, [[nivolumab]] and serplulimab+chemo provided significant survival benefit. In Asian patients, [[pembrolizumab]] or [[pembrolizumab]] + chemotherapy had favorable OS and PFS benefits. In non-Asian patients, cemiplimab showed favorable OS and PFS benefit. For former/current smokers, [[pembrolizumab]]+chemotherapy and camrelizumab+chemotherapy had significant OS and PFS benefit. Camrelizumab+chemo [OR = 3.5, 95% CI: (2.3-5.3)] had the best overall response rate (ORR) benefit. [[Ipilimumab]]+chemo had the highest incidence of adverse events (AEs) [OR = 2.0, 95% CI:(1.5-2.7)]. (Frontiers in immunology, 2025)

A randomized controlled trial (RCT) involving 719 patients investigated the efficacy of [[nivolumab]] plus [[ipilimumab]] with chemotherapy versus chemotherapy alone in patients with metastatic non-small-cell lung cancer (NSCLC). The primary outcome was overall survival (OS). The primary result showed that [[nivolumab]] plus [[ipilimumab]] with chemotherapy demonstrated continued OS benefit versus chemotherapy (hazard ratio 0.74, 95% confidence interval 0.63-0.87, 6-year OS rates 16% versus 10%). The benefit was observed regardless of tumor programmed death ligand 1 (PD-L1) expression, histology, or select somatic mutation status (KRAS, STK11, KEAP1, and TP53). The 6-year duration of response (DOR) rate was 19% with [[nivolumab]] plus [[ipilimumab]] with chemotherapy. No new safety signals were observed. The study concluded that these final analyses demonstrate the durable, long-term OS and response benefit with first-line [[nivolumab]] plus [[ipilimumab]] with chemotherapy over chemotherapy in patients with metastatic NSCLC, further supporting this regimen as a standard-of-care treatment option. (ESMO open, 2025)

A randomized controlled trial (RCT) involving 295 patients developed a treatment-related death (TRD) predictive score for treatment-naïve advanced NSCLC patients treated with combined chemotherapy with [[pembrolizumab]] (PC) or [[nivolumab]] and [[ipilimumab]] plus platinum doublet chemotherapy (NIC). The primary outcome was the development of a risk stratification score for TRDs. The primary result showed that the dichotomized TRD score stratified 290 treated patients into 92 high-risk and 198 low-risk patients. In low-risk patients, TRD incidence was 2.2% (0/92) with PC and 2.8% (3/106) with NIC. In high-risk patients, TRD incidence was 1.9% (1/52) with PC and 20.0% (8/40) with NIC. Worse outcomes in the NIC arm were consistently observed for both immune-related adverse events (irAEs) and progression-free survival. The study concluded that the TRD score is a valuable tool for identifying patients with comparable toxicity-efficacy profiles among PC and NIC regimens. (Lung cancer (Amsterdam, Netherlands), 2025)

Median progression-free survival (PFS) was 1.4 months; median overall survival (OS) was 14.2 months. No adverse events caused treatment discontinuation. A systematic review and meta-analysis published in Clinical and experimental medicine in 2025 compared the safety profiles and hematologic toxicities of various immune checkpoint inhibitors (ICIs) in digestive system tumors, including [[nivolumab]] and [[ipilimumab]]. The study concluded that despite only 6 months of consolidation immunotherapy, both [[nivolumab]] alone and combination [[nivolumab]]/[[ipilimumab]] demonstrated improved 18-month PFS over appropriate historical controls, though overall toxicity was higher in the combination arm. (Journal for immunotherapy of cancer, 2025)

A meta-analysis published in Targeted oncology in 2025 compared various immune checkpoint inhibitors (ICI) combination therapies in advanced renal cell carcinoma. The primary outcomes were overall survival (OS), progression-free survival (PFS), and objective response rate (ORR). [[Pembrolizumab]] + lenvatinib demonstrated statistically significant improvement in PFS compared with [[nivolumab]] + [[ipilimumab]] (HR = 0.53; 95% credible interval (CrI): 0.40-0.71), avelumab + axitinib (HR = 0.71; 95% Crl: 0.53-0.94), [[atezolizumab]] + [[bevacizumab]] (HR = 0.54; 95% CrI: 0.40-0.73), and [[pembrolizumab]] + axitinib (OR = 0.69; 95% CrI: 0.51-0.91). A statistically significant higher ORR was shown for [[pembrolizumab]] + lenvatinib compared with [[nivolumab]] + [[ipilimumab]] (odd ratio (OR) = 3.29; 95% Crl: 2.21-4.93), [[pembrolizumab]] + axitinib (OR = 1.92; 95% CrI: 1.27-2.94), [[atezolizumab]] + [[bevacizumab]] (OR = 4.05; 95% Crl: 2.71-6.05), bempegaldesleukin + [[nivolumab]] (OR = 6.20; 95% CrI: 3.69-10.48), and [[nivolumab]] (OR = 5.92; 95% Crl: 2.70-13.24). No statistically significant difference was observed for OS. The overall population analysis indicated that [[pembrolizumab]] + lenvatinib improves PFS and ORR compared with other approved ICI combination therapies in first-line aRCC. No significant differences in OS were observed between [[pembrolizumab]] + lenvatinib and other combination immune checkpoint inhibitor-based therapies.

A randomized controlled trial (RCT) involving 102 patients compared tobemstomig with [[nivolumab]] plus [[ipilimumab]] in patients with resectable stage III melanoma. The primary outcome was pathological response by independent pathological review. The primary result was that tobemstomig showed a similar pathological response rate (pRR) versus [[nivolumab]] plus [[ipilimumab]] (80.0% (32/40) versus 77.3% (17/22)). Major pathological responses were less frequent with tobemstomig versus [[nivolumab]] plus [[ipilimumab]] treatment (62.5% (25/40) versus 72.7% (16/22)). Tobemstomig plus tiragolumab showed a lower pRR versus [[nivolumab]] plus [[ipilimumab]] (60.0% (12/20) versus 77.3% (17/22)). [[Atezolizumab]] plus tiragolumab showed a lower pRR versus [[nivolumab]] plus [[ipilimumab]] (45.0% (9/20) versus 77.3% (17/22)). Tobemstomig demonstrated improved safety versus [[nivolumab]] plus [[ipilimumab]], with 2.5% (1/40) and 22.7% (5/22) of patients experiencing grade 3 or higher treatment-related adverse events (TRAEs), respectively, and 0% (0/40) and 13.6% (3/22) of patients discontinuing treatment due to TRAEs, respectively. Grade 3 or higher TRAEs were reported by 15% (3/20) of patients in the tobemstomig plus tiragolumab arm and by no patients in the [[atezolizumab]] plus tiragolumab arm. In conclusion, in the Morpheus-Melanoma study, tobemstomig demonstrated a similar pathological response and improved safety profile versus [[nivolumab]] plus [[ipilimumab]] in patients with resectable stage III melanoma. (Nature medicine, 2025)

A Systematic Review and Network Meta-Analysis of 25 randomized controlled trials compared various immune checkpoint inhibitors (ICIs) to chemotherapy alone for overall survival (OS) and progression-free survival (PFS) in advanced squamous non-small cell lung cancer. Cemiplimab [HR = 0.48, 95% CI: (0.34-0.67)] showed the best OS benefit. Sugemalimab+chemo provided the best PFS benefit [HR = 0.34, 95% CI: (0.24-0.48)]. For PD-L1≥50% tumors, penpulimab showed excellent OS and PFS. For PD-L1 1-49% tumors, [[pembrolizumab]]+chemo and camrelizumab+chemo achieved the best OS and PFS, respectively. For PD-L1 <1% tumors, [[nivolumab]] and serplulimab+chemo provided significant survival benefit. In Asian patients, [[pembrolizumab]] or [[pembrolizumab]] + chemotherapy had favorable OS and PFS benefits. In non-Asian patients, cemiplimab showed favorable OS and PFS benefit. For former/current smokers, [[pembrolizumab]]+chemotherapy and camrelizumab+chemotherapy had significant OS and PFS benefit. Camrelizumab+chemo [OR = 3.5, 95% CI: (2.3-5.3)] had the best overall response rate (ORR) benefit. [[Ipilimumab]]+chemo had the highest incidence of adverse events (AEs) [OR = 2.0, 95% CI:(1.5-2.7)]. (Frontiers in immunology, 2025)

A meta-analysis involving 7,333 patients compared ICI therapy to a control arm (placebo or standard of care) to assess irAEs associated with ICI therapy in bladder cancer. The primary result showed that Hypothyroidism (RR: 5.87 (3.23, 10.67)), hyperthyroidism (RR: 11.05 (4.20, 29.03)), pruritus (RR: 4.95 (2.82, 8.70)), rash (RR: 2.92 (1.51, 5.64)), colitis (RR: 2.15 (1.11, 4.15)), pneumonitis (RR: 3.91 (2.18, 7.02)), and nephritis (RR: 4.97 (1.43, 17.33)) were found to be significant irAEs associated with ICI therapy. Bladder cancer patients treated with ICIs are at significant risk of irAEs. These events vary in severity, and appropriate management of these adverse events should be prioritized to improve quality of life. (Urologic oncology, 2025)
An RCT involving 168 patients investigated the efficacy of adding PD-1 or PD-L1 blockade (such as [[nivolumab]] and [[atezolizumab]]) to either chemotherapy or CAR-T therapy in relapsed/refractory acute lymphoblastic leukemia, compared to FLAG alone. The primary outcome was progression-free survival (PFS). The primary result was: Median PFS was 7.7 months in Group A, 11.7 months in Group B, and 4.1 months in the control group (p<0.001). Secondary results included: Median OS was 10.75, 13.5, and 5.65 months, respectively (p<0.001). MRD negativity rates were significantly higher in experimental arms compared to control (Group A: 19.5%; Group B: 27.8%; Control: 3%; p<0.001). Higher baseline PD-L1 expression was independently associated with improved survival (HR 0.90 per 10% increase; p=0.002). Safety analysis showed that the addition of checkpoint inhibitors did not significantly increase severe toxicities, and infection rates were lower in experimental groups compared to control. The study concluded: Adding PD-1 or PD-L1 blockade to either chemotherapy or CAR-T therapy improved clinical outcomes without excess toxicity in R/R ALL. (Leukemia research, 2025)

A meta-analysis of 24 randomized controlled trials (n=13,572 patients) investigated first-line therapy in advanced hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC), comparing various ICI-based combinations against sorafenib. The primary outcome was overall survival (OS) and progression-free survival (PFS). In the overall population, regimens with significant OS advantage over sorafenib included sintilimab plus bevacizumab biosimilar (HR = 0.57, 95% CrI 0.43-0.75), camrelizumab plus rivoceranib (HR = 0.62, 0.48-0.79), and [[atezolizumab]] plus [[bevacizumab]] (HR = 0.66, 0.51-0.84). For PFS, top-ranked combinations were camrelizumab plus rivoceranib (HR = 0.52, 0.41-0.66), anlotinib plus penpulimab (HR = 0.53, 0.41-0.68), lenvatinib plus [[pembrolizumab]] (HR = 0.55, 0.44-0.68), and sintilimab plus bevacizumab biosimilar (HR = 0.56, 0.45-0.69). ORR was highest with lenvatinib plus [[pembrolizumab]] (RR = 8.00, 4.98-12.86). In HBV-related HCC, sintilimab plus bevacizumab biosimilar and [[atezolizumab]] plus [[bevacizumab]] led OS rankings, with PFS favoring cabozantinib plus [[atezolizumab]] and [[atezolizumab]] plus [[bevacizumab]]. In HCV-related HCC, only [[atezolizumab]] plus [[bevacizumab]] conferred a significant OS benefit (HR = 0.43, 0.25-0.73), while PFS superiority was observed only for cabozantinib plus [[atezolizumab]] (HR = 0.73, 0.54-0.99). In non-viral HCC, the STRIDE regimen (single priming dose tremelimumab plus [[durvalumab]]) was the only regimen to significantly improve OS (HR = 0.75, 0.59-0.96). Regarding safety, tislelizumab (RR = 0.42, 0.33-0.52) and [[nivolumab]] (RR = 0.45, 0.36-0.56) were associated with the lowest incidence of AEs≥3. For first-line therapy in advanced HCC, ICI-based combinations with anti-VEGF/anti-angiogenic agents generally outperform sorafenib, with discernible etiology-specific optima: HBV-related HCC favors sintilimab plus bevacizumab biosimilar or [[atezolizumab]] plus [[bevacizumab]]; HCV-related HCC favors [[atezolizumab]] plus [[bevacizumab]]; and in non-viral disease, STRIDE demonstrates a unique OS advantage. This etiology-stratified evidence framework may guide individualized first-line decision-making, pending confirmation in head-to-head trials. (Frontiers in immunology, 2026)

### Competitive position
A recent meta-analysis in advanced renal cell carcinoma (aRCC) suggests that the combination of [[pembrolizumab]] and lenvatinib may offer superior progression-free survival (PFS) and objective response rate (ORR) compared to other approved immune checkpoint inhibitor (ICI) combinations, including [[nivolumab]] + [[ipilimumab]]. While no significant difference in overall survival (OS) was observed, the improved PFS and ORR for the competitor combination could pose a competitive challenge for [[nivolumab]]-based regimens in this indication. A recent RCT in resectable stage III melanoma indicated that tobemstomig, a competitor, achieved a similar pathological response rate to [[nivolumab]] plus [[ipilimumab]], while demonstrating an improved safety profile. This suggests a potential competitive challenge for [[nivolumab]]-based regimens in this melanoma setting, particularly regarding safety. Additionally, a recent network meta-analysis in advanced squamous non-small cell lung cancer (SqNSCLC) highlighted that for tumors with PD-L1 <1%, [[nivolumab]] provided significant survival benefit, suggesting a specific patient population where it demonstrates strong efficacy compared to other immune checkpoint inhibitors. Furthermore, a meta-analysis on first-line advanced HCC indicated that while ICI-based combinations generally outperform sorafenib, other specific combinations like sintilimab plus bevacizumab biosimilar, camrelizumab plus rivoceranib, and [[atezolizumab]] plus bevacizumab showed significant OS advantages. However, [[nivolumab]] was noted to be associated with one of the lowest incidences of AEs≥3, suggesting a potential competitive advantage in terms of safety profile. Moreover, recent clinical trial results for the add-on strategy of regorafenib plus [[nivolumab]] in second-line hepatocellular carcinoma demonstrated manageable safety and significant clinical activity with a noteworthy median overall survival of 20 months, further supporting [[nivolumab]]'s potential in this challenging indication. A systematic review and meta-analysis in advanced non-squamous non-small cell lung cancer (nsNSCLC) found that while prolgolimab + chemotherapy, [[pembrolizumab]] + chemotherapy, and cemiplimab + chemotherapy were most efficacious by OS, [[nivolumab]] + [[bevacizumab]] + chemotherapy demonstrated the highest PFS, indicating a strong competitive edge in this specific outcome for nsNSCLC. Additionally, recent RCT data in unresectable stage IIIA/IIIB NSCLC demonstrated that both [[nivolumab]] alone and in combination with [[ipilimumab]] significantly improved 18-month progression-free survival over historical controls, reinforcing its efficacy in this challenging lung cancer setting.

New data further refines [[nivolumab]]'s competitive landscape. In muscle-invasive bladder cancer, adjuvant [[nivolumab]] after NAC and surgery was identified as the most effective strategy, positioning it strongly in this indication. However, in perioperative NSCLC, while immunotherapeutic schemes are superior to placebo for PD-L1 ≥50%, P-toripalimab showed a greater PFS effect for PD-L1 <1% and 1-49%, suggesting potential competition in these subgroups. For advanced NSCLC, a TRD predictive score indicated that high-risk patients treated with [[nivolumab]] and [[ipilimumab]] plus platinum doublet chemotherapy (NIC) had significantly higher TRD incidence and worse outcomes compared to [[pembrolizumab]] plus chemotherapy (PC), which could limit its use in this patient population. In advanced melanoma, a targeted-therapy induction regimen did not improve PFS compared to first-line [[nivolumab]] plus [[ipilimumab]], reinforcing the role of ICI as first-line. However, an observational study in melanoma identified elevated plasma TNF as a factor associated with poorer ICI outcomes, including for [[nivolumab]]. In the context of cost-effectiveness for MSI-H/dMMR mCRC in China, [[nivolumab]] + [[ipilimumab]] was found not to be cost-effective compared to [[pembrolizumab]] alone, which could impact market access. Despite these challenges, [[nivolumab]] plus [[ipilimumab]] demonstrated significant OS benefit and manageable safety in unresectable hepatocellular carcinoma compared to lenvatinib or sorafenib, strengthening its position. It also showed durable efficacy in MSI/dMMR chemoresistant mCRC after 5-year follow-up. In rare cancers, [[nivolumab]] plus [[ipilimumab]] showed promising durable responses in SCCOHT and refractory pancreatic neuroendocrine neoplasms (PNEN), highlighting its utility in niche indications. Furthermore, in non-clear cell renal cell cancer, [[ipilimumab]]/[[nivolumab]] demonstrated significantly longer OS and acceptable toxicity compared to standard of care. The combination of [[nivolumab]] with personalized neoantigen-targeting vaccines also showed promise in boosting T cell responses in melanoma.
