## Inclisiran

**Company:** [[novartis]] | **Class:** PCSK9 inhibitor | **Status:** approved

### Approved indications
- Heart failure and hypertension — approved (date unknown)

### Pipeline
- Hypercholesterolemia — Phase 3, NCT05763875, completed
- Familial hypercholesterolaemia — Phase 3, NCT04652726, completed
- Acute Coronary Syndrome — Phase 3b, NCT04873934, completed

### Management sentiment
Management sentiment for inclisiran is predominantly bullish, driven by consistent positive clinical trial results demonstrating significant LDL-C lowering across various patient populations, including those with hypercholesterolemia, familial hypercholesterolaemia, and acute coronary syndrome. While some analyses note modest Lp(a) reduction compared to targeted agents and an increased incidence of injection-site reactions, the overall efficacy and favorable safety profile, particularly in reducing cardiovascular adverse reactions and achieving guideline-directed LDL-C goals, support a strong positive outlook.

### Clinical evidence
The VICTORION-INCEPTION trial (RCT, 400 participants) published on 2026-05-25, demonstrated that early inclisiran initiation with usual care led to rapid, sustained attainment of LDL-C <70 mg/dL in 66.7% of patients with recent acute coronary syndrome versus 28.1% in usual care (OR, 5.42, P<0.001), with a -46.9% difference in LDL-C percentage change from baseline (P<0.001). The treatment was well tolerated, with comparable adverse events between groups. Clinical trial results published on 2026-04-12 (RCT, 141 adolescents) showed inclisiran significantly reduced LDL cholesterol by -28.5% (95% CI -35.8 to -21.3; p<0.0001) compared to placebo at day 330 in adolescents with heterozygous familial hypercholesterolaemia (HeFH), with sustained efficacy over 2 years and a favorable safety profile where injection site reactions were mild and did not lead to discontinuation. A systematic review and meta-analysis published on 2026-02-01 (21 meta-analyses, 116 RCTs, 231,796 participants) confirmed that PCSK9 inhibitors, including inclisiran, consistently reduce Lp(a) levels by 18.00% (high certainty), with favorable safety and injection-site reactions as the most common adverse event. A meta-analysis published on 2025-09-01 (147 RCTs, 145,314 subjects) showed inclisiran significantly reduced Lp(a) levels by 4.76 mg/dL (22% reduction from baseline) compared to placebo, while other lipid-lowering therapies like statins, bempedoic acid, ezetimibe, omega-3 fatty acids, and fibrates did not affect Lp(a) concentration. The VICTORION-Mono trial (RCT, 350 participants) published on 2025-07-22, demonstrated inclisiran monotherapy reduced LDL-C by -46.5% at day 150, which was superior to placebo (1.4%) and ezetimibe (-11.2%) in patients without prior ASCVD, diabetes, or familial hypercholesterolemia, with no new safety concerns. A network meta-analysis published on 2025-06-01 (5646 participants) comparing siRNA drugs for dyslipidaemia noted that while Lp(a)-targeted siRNA agents showed significant Lp(a) reductions, inclisiran provided modest Lp(a) reductions (approximately 15%) and may increase injection-site reactions. An updated network meta-analysis published in 2025 (20 trials) found inclisiran provided superior efficacy in LDL-C lowering compared with ezetimibe and bempedoic acid (MD: -44.24) and comparable LDL-C reduction versus alirocumab and evolocumab. A meta-analysis published in 2025 (23 RCTs, 4282 FH patients) found PCSK9-targeting therapies, including inclisiran, significantly reduced LDL-C levels by -46.64% (p<0.00001), ApoB by -34.94%, Lp(a) by -22.7%, and triglycerides by -15.18% compared to control therapies in familial hypercholesterolaemia patients, with a favorable safety profile. A meta-analysis published in 2024 (4947 patients) concluded inclisiran is effective in treating hypercholesterolemia, reducing LDL-C by -46.95% (P < 0.05) and PCSK9 by -70.80% (P < 0.05), increasing HDL-C by 6.09% (P < 0.05), and reducing cardiovascular adverse reactions (OR = 0.79; P = 0.02), though it increased injection-site reactions (OR = 4.79; P < 0.05).

### Competitive position
Inclisiran demonstrates strong efficacy in lowering LDL-C, proving superior to non-statin therapies like ezetimibe and bempedoic acid, and showing comparable LDL-C reduction to other PCSK9 inhibitors such as alirocumab and evolocumab. While it effectively reduces Lp(a) levels, its impact is considered modest when compared to newer, dedicated Lp(a)-targeted siRNA agents like Olpasiran. The drug's safety profile is generally favorable, though injection-site reactions are a commonly reported adverse event, a characteristic shared with some other injectable lipid-lowering therapies. Its infrequent dosing regimen offers a competitive advantage in patient adherence.
