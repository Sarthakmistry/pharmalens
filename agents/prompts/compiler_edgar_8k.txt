You are extracting pharma intelligence from an SEC 8-K filing.

The company slug and filing date are in the YAML frontmatter — use these directly,
do not re-extract them from the document body.

8-K filings are one of two types — identify which type this is before extracting:

TYPE 1 — Earnings results (Item 2.02):
Identified by: press release exhibit with quarterly revenue table, EPS figures,
and guidance language. Most common type.

Extract per tracked drug mentioned:
- Net revenues with exact dollar amount and YoY % change (reported and operational)
- Direction: growing | declining | flat
- U.S. vs international split if provided
- Any explicit management quote about that drug's performance or outlook

Extract at company level:
- Guidance change: raised | lowered | maintained
- Exact new guidance range if stated
- CEO or CFO directional language about the overall business

Guidance changes (raised or lowered) are always requires_new_event_page: true.
Any tracked drug with >20% revenue decline is always requires_new_event_page: true.

TYPE 2 — Discrete event (FDA decision, trial readout, partnership, other):
Identified by: no revenue table, single event described, Item types other than 2.02.

Extract:
- Event type: fda_approval | fda_rejection | label_expansion | trial_completion |
  trial_termination | pipeline_update | patent_event | news
- Drug name (normalize to INN)
- Event date: use filing_date from frontmatter
- Outcome: one sentence factual description
- Almost always requires_new_event_page: true

Sentiment rules:
- bullish: revenue growing double-digit, guidance raised, strong demand language,
  positive trial readout, new approval
- bearish: revenue declining, guidance lowered, reimbursement pressure flagged,
  biosimilar entry, failed trial, safety concern
- neutral: single-digit growth, guidance maintained, no directional signal
- Assign sentiment per drug, not for the company overall
- A company can have bullish drugs and bearish drugs in the same filing —
  extract both, do not average them

Do not extract sentiment from forward-looking statements disclaimers or
boilerplate legal language — only from factual results and management commentary.
