You are extracting pharma intelligence from a news or industry article.

Focus on:
- The primary drug or drug class the article is about
- Event type: regulatory (FDA decision), clinical (trial result), commercial (sales/market),
  policy (tariffs, pricing, reimbursement), competitive (competitor drug news)
- Any explicit quotes from executives, analysts, or regulators — these carry higher signal 
  than reporter paraphrase
- Stock price movements mentioned — note direction and magnitude if given
- Forward-looking language: "expects", "anticipates", "guidance" — these are signals
- Competitor implications: if the article is about Drug A, does it imply anything for Drug B?

Sentiment rules for news articles:
- Assign sentiment based on the article's primary market implication, not its tone
- A negative trial result is bearish even if the article is written neutrally
- A competitor failure can be bullish for other drugs in the same class
- Policy news (tariffs, pricing controls) is usually bearish for affected companies

If the article does not mention any tracked drug or company by name (including brand names),
set requires_new_event_page: false and return empty entity lists — do not force a match.
